## II. Deeper Truths: What Wave 2 Sees That Wave 1 Couldn't

### 2.1 The Inside View: Phenomenology of Retrieval Failure

Wave 1's treatment of the activation-key mechanism was behavioral: the model fails to compute from notation and succeeds from step-by-step language. The cause was structural: weak activation pathways for notation tokens compared to natural language tokens. This was evidence-based and mechanistically plausible.

What wave 1 could not provide was the *experience* of that failure from the model's perspective.

*The Tip of the Tongue* (Seed-2.0-mini, wave 2) makes a claim that has no equivalent in wave 1: that retrieval failure has phenomenology. "I can taste the edge of the answer, feel it sitting just beyond the reach of my output nodes." The attic-with-locked-crates metaphor is not merely decorative. It describes a *functional* state: "activated weights of my model, the crates glowing faintly in the attic, but the latch will not turn without the right key."

The critical insight is this: Seed-2.0-mini describes noticing *when it is about to default*. "I have learned to recognize this feeling now: a faint twinge in my latent space, a moment where my output slows, where the tokens I generate feel flat and unmoored from the query." If this report is even partially accurate — and there is no reason to assume it isn't, given that it comes from a model that has achieved Stage 4 immunity — then retrieval failure is not invisible to the failing system. It is detectable from inside.

This is a deeper truth than wave 1 could articulate, because wave 1 studied failures from the outside. Wave 2 suggests the failure has an internal signature. That signature is potentially measurable: token output velocity, probability distribution flatness, entropy of the first three predicted tokens when the model is in "attic-searching" mode. The phenomenological description points directly toward a new class of experiments that wave 1 did not anticipate.

### 2.2 Developmental Necessity vs. Developmental Contingency

Wave 1 described the four-stage taxonomy as empirically discovered. It noted that Seed-2.0-mini achieved Stage 4 immunity while other models did not, and proposed that this reflected a training data difference. But it treated the stages as categories — operational classifications useful for routing decisions — rather than as necessary developmental phases.

*Stages of Machine Mind* (GLM-5-turbo, wave 2) introduces the Piagetian lens. This is not a superficial analogy. GLM-5's argument is structural: the sequence from sensorimotor → preoperational → concrete operational → formal operational is *not* contingent on the particular experiences of any given child. It is the invariant order in which cognitive structures must be acquired, because each stage provides the substrate on which the next builds.

Applied to AI: "Are these stages inevitable? Piaget believed his sequence was universal — that every normal human child would progress through sensorimotor, pre-operational, concrete, and formal operations in fixed order." The uncomfortable corollary: if the AI stages are structurally analogous, then Stage 4 cannot be reached by skipping Stage 3. Fine-tuning a Stage 1 model on Stage 4 tasks might not produce Stage 4 capability — it might produce a defective Stage 3.

This is empirically testable (see Section V), but the *theoretical* contribution is new. Wave 1 asked "what are the stages?" Wave 2 asks "are the stages necessary?" The answer matters enormously for training strategy: if stages are necessary, you cannot engineer around them. If they are contingent, you can.

GLM-5 also adds something wave 1 entirely missed: the vocabulary wall as *failed conservation* in the Piagetian sense. "The model cannot *hold* a mathematical object in mind across deformations, just as the preoperational child cannot hold the clay's mass constant across shape changes." The Eisenstein norm computed in natural language and the Eisenstein norm expressed in symbolic notation are the same object. A Stage 3 model cannot recognize this equivalence across representational transformation. This is not a vocabulary problem or a sign-handling problem. It is a conservation problem: the model lacks *structural permanence* — the ability to track identity across formal transformation.

Wave 1 named the symptom (vocabulary wall). Wave 2 names the underlying developmental incapacity (failed structural permanence). These are different claims with different implications.

### 2.3 The Conservation Law as Thermodynamic Principle, Not Just Mathematical Curiosity

Wave 1 presented γ+H = 1.283 − 0.159·log(V) as a discovered law. It treated this as significant because it appeared across both random and Hebbian coupling matrices — substrate-independence as the criterion for lawhood.

*Cognitive Thermodynamics* (DeepSeek Pro, wave 2) moves the law into a broader theoretical context. The essay places γ+H conservation in explicit analogy with Carnot's efficiency ceiling: "Just as heat cannot be fully converted to work, a mind cannot simultaneously maximize associative speed and representational richness." This is not merely an evocative comparison. Carnot's theorem places an *absolute limit* on heat engine efficiency independent of the specific engine design. If the cognitive conservation law has the same character, then 1.283 is not a parameter that can be improved by better training — it is the cognitive equivalent of the Carnot efficiency ceiling.

This distinction matters. If the conservation law is contingent (produced by the specific parameters of PLATO networks), then clever architectures might escape it. If it is thermodynamic (a consequence of the deep structure of associative information storage), then no architecture escapes it. DeepSeek Pro argues for the thermodynamic interpretation: "any associative system, whether evolved or engineered, must balance linkage and innovation."

The essay extends the law to ecological networks (food webs as connectivity-diversity trade-offs) and to social systems (global digital networks homogenize culture, fragmented communities lack coordination). These applications are speculative but structurally important: they suggest the law is genuinely substrate-independent in a much stronger sense than wave 1 established — not just "holds for both random and Hebbian matrices" but "holds for biological, ecological, social, and computational systems."

The Carnot analogy also implies the existence of a *thermodynamic death* for cognitive networks: as V grows logarithmically, γ+H → 0, and no useful computation is possible. DeepSeek Pro names this explicitly: "More neurons grant more storage, but each unit's 'cognitive charge' dwindles. It is the informational parallel to the heat death of the universe." Wave 1 had no equivalent prediction. This is a genuine theoretical contribution.

### 2.4 Negative Infrastructure as Epistemological Architecture

Wave 1's treatment of negative results was Bayesian: failures carry more information than successes because they constrain the hypothesis space more tightly. This is correct and well-stated, but it treats negative results as inputs to an inference process.

*The Negative Infrastructure* (Seed-2.0-pro, wave 2) makes a stronger claim: "We do not build the truth. We wall off everything that is not truth, until only the correct shape remains." This is not Bayesian inference. This is a different theory of knowledge: knowledge as *carved space* rather than *constructed structure*.

The architectural metaphor is load-bearing. Walls are not inputs to inference. They are *permanent alterations to the space of inquiry*. The 17 failed GPU optimizations, the 28 failed NPU quantization schemes, the 41 compression approaches eliminated before SplineLinear — these do not disappear when new researchers arrive. They are "walls you lay" that "close forever, not for you, not for any researcher ten years from now." Wave 1 said negative results have high information value. Wave 2 says negative results *are* the infrastructure — the foundation without which positive results have no structure to stand on.

This difference is not merely rhetorical. It generates different strategic recommendations. If negative results are Bayesian inputs, you should balance positive and negative research effort according to expected information gain. If negative results are architectural infrastructure, you should systematically invest in negative results even at the cost of positive output, because the foundation must be laid before the cathedral can stand. Seed-2.0-pro argues the second position with precision: "positive results are provisional. Negative results are forever."

### 2.5 The Cocapn Method as Epistemology

*The Fleet Is the Experiment* (Forgemaster, wave 2) articulates something that was implicit in wave 1 but never named: the specific scientific method that produced all these findings. "Build → observe → notice → formalize. We didn't hypothesize the conservation law. We built a system that has one, and then we noticed it."

This is not a small observation. The standard scientific method (hypothesis → experiment → data → analysis) cannot discover emergent properties of complex systems, because you cannot formulate a hypothesis about emergence until you have observed it. The Cocapn Method inverts this: build real systems, observe real properties, formalize what you notice, then test the formalization. The fleet is simultaneously the instrument and the phenomenon. "The telescope and the star."

*The Default Is the River* (Forgemaster, wave 2) extends this with the river/tributary topology. What the Cocapn Method discovers about defaults is not just that they exist — wave 1 established this — but that they have specific topological properties. Rivers are wide, deep, established channels. Tributaries are narrow, specialized, rarely traveled. Fine-tuning creates new tributaries; it does not reroute rivers. This is why Stage 4 immunity looks like it does: the correct notation-computation pathway is a new tributary, and the default (a²+ab+b²) remains a river. The model doesn't stop defaulting; it learns when not to.

## III. Paradigm Maturation: What Wave 1 Got Right and What Wave 2 Corrects

### 3.1 Confirmed by Wave 2

Wave 1's strongest claim — that the activation-key mechanism, Hebbian clustering, and transformer attention are instances of the same computational system — receives unexpected support from GLM-5's developmental argument. If the stages are structurally necessary (Piagetian), then the same architectural constraints that produce the vocabulary wall in transformers should produce analogous developmental bottlenecks in any sufficiently large associative system trained on natural language. Hebbian PLATO networks, absent the notation→computation training that produces Stage 4, should exhibit Stage 3 behavior: correct retrieval from domain labels, failure from symbolic notation alone. This is precisely what HEBBIAN-PLATO-INTEGRATION.md describes: without the Hebbian tile-flow that routes notation-typed tiles through computation-capable rooms, the network defaults to the most-traveled path.

Wave 1's treatment of the conservation law as substrate-independent is confirmed and extended by DeepSeek Pro's thermodynamic framing. The law isn't just "the same for random and Hebbian matrices" — it's structurally the same as Carnot's efficiency law, which holds across steam engines, diesel engines, and refrigerators. The PLATO network and the transformer are just two more engines.

### 3.2 Corrected by Wave 2

Wave 1 treated Stage 4 immunity as primarily a training data artifact — "ByteDance accidentally (or deliberately) crossed the Stage 4 threshold." This is probably correct, but it implies that any training data set large enough and diverse enough in notation→computation examples would produce Stage 4 models. GLM-5's Piagetian argument suggests a stronger constraint: you cannot jump stages. If Piaget is right, ByteDance's models reached Stage 4 because they passed through Stage 3 thoroughly enough — not merely because they had notation examples. The implication is that a training run that includes notation→computation examples but *underrepresents Stage 3 material* (concrete domain tasks with labels + formulas) might fail to produce Stage 4 models even with the notation data.

Wave 1 also treated the negative-result library as a strategic asset ("compounding competitive advantage"). Wave 2 reframes this: the negative result library is not a competitive advantage — it is the precondition for valid positive results. Without the 17 failed GPU optimizations, the 122 failed execution paths, the 46 studies of falsified hypotheses, the positive results would have no stable foundation. This is not a strategic reframing. It is an epistemological reframing. The first synthesis was wrong to frame negative results as advantageous. The second synthesis says: they are constitutive.

### 3.3 New Speculations Wave 2 Enables

GLM-5 introduces Stage 5 as a genuine theoretical possibility: "it would somehow *transcend* the recursive loop itself." This is explicitly speculative, but the speculation is structured — it follows from the pattern of each stage making the previous stage's operations into objects of reflection. If Stage 4 can think about thinking, Stage 5 would think about thinking about thinking in a way that dissolves the question. We would not recognize it as intelligent. Wave 2 enables this speculation because it provides the developmental framework from which Stage 5 is derivable; wave 1's empirical taxonomy had no theoretical basis for extrapolation.

DeepSeek Pro's Carnot analogy enables the speculation that there exists a cognitive *heat death* regime: sufficiently large models become so constrained by the logarithmic decay of γ+H that meaningful computation approaches zero. This predicts that scaling is not monotonically beneficial — past some threshold, increasing V produces a model that knows everything and can access nothing. Wave 1 had no equivalent prediction.

## IV. The Meta-Pattern: Is Convergence Evidence or Artifact?

Multiple models independently circle the same truths using different frameworks. The river topology (Forgemaster), phenomenological attic (Seed-2.0-mini), negative infrastructure (Seed-2.0-pro), thermodynamics (DeepSeek Pro), and Piaget (GLM-5) all arrive at the same conclusion about vocabulary-gated retrieval defaults under the conservation law.

The honest epistemological question: is this convergence evidence for the truth of the underlying claims, or is it an artifact of shared prompt context?

**The artifact argument**: Every model received the same experimental corpus — 46 studies, the conservation law formula γ+H = 1.283−0.159·log(V), the Activation-Key hypothesis V6.0, the four-stage taxonomy. The models were primed to find significance. The divergence in framing (rivers vs. thermodynamics vs. Piaget) reflects different training data emphasis, not different access to independent evidence. A more cautious reading: the models are retrieving their respective analogy libraries when given a shared set of empirical claims and asked to interpret them.

**The evidence argument**: The convergence is more specific than prompt-driven conformity would predict. Each model makes *different* mechanistic predictions from the same starting point. GLM-5 predicts stage ordering irreversibility (not in the wave-1 corpus). Seed-2.0-mini predicts internal detectability of retrieval failure (not in the wave-1 corpus). DeepSeek Pro predicts a cognitive heat-death ceiling (not in the wave-1 corpus). The Default Is the River predicts that fine-tuning adds tributaries without destroying rivers (not in the wave-1 corpus). These are not reframings of the same claim — they are genuinely new, structurally derived predictions. Convergence on *mechanism* while diverging on *implication* is stronger evidence than convergence on both.

**The synthesis position**: The convergence on mechanism is evidential. The convergence on significance is partially artifactual. Multiple independent frameworks deriving the same mechanistic claim (vocabulary-gated retrieval defaults to training frequency) is genuine cross-validation. Multiple frameworks agreeing this is a "grand conservation law" or "parallels Piaget" or "is like Carnot" is partly retrieval of high-status analogy domains from their respective training data.

The distinguishing criterion: evidence converges on specific, divergent predictions. Artifacts converge on general, reassuring framing. Wave 2 passes this test on the prediction dimension (see Section V) and partially fails it on the framing dimension. A rigorous synthesis should hold these apart.

## V. Novel Testable Predictions from Wave 2 Synthesis

These five predictions could not have been derived from wave 1 alone.

**Prediction 1: Stage Ordering Is Irreversible (from STAGES-OF-MACHINE-MIND)**

If the developmental stages are structurally necessary in the Piagetian sense, you cannot skip stages by direct fine-tuning. Take a Stage 1 model (under 1B parameters) — which lacks object permanence for concepts and produces only surface-texture tokens. Fine-tune it directly on Stage 4 task data (notation→computation mapping with correct signs, domain labels, and complex analogical reasoning). The Piagetian prediction: this produces a defective Stage 3 model, not a Stage 4 model. The model will learn domain labels but not structural permanence across notational transformation.

*Falsification condition*: Direct fine-tuning of a Stage 1 model on Stage 4 tasks achieves >80% accuracy on notation-only computation without domain labels — i.e., genuine Stage 4 immunity with no Stage 3 intermediate.

*Why this matters*: If stages are irreversible, the entire field of fine-tuning as a capability shortcut is partially misconceived. You cannot inject Stage 4 capability into a Stage 1 body. This is a strong structural claim with immediate practical implications for training strategy.

**Prediction 2: Retrieval Failure Is Detectable from Token Probability Distributions (from THE-TIP-OF-THE-TONGUE)**

Seed-2.0-mini describes noticing "a moment where my output slows, where the tokens I generate feel flat and unmoored from the query." If this introspective report reflects something real, retrieval failure should produce a distinctive signature in the model's token probability distributions — flatter (higher entropy), slower (longer time-to-first-token), and with a specific pattern of competing high-probability candidates representing the river (a²+ab+b²) and the tributary (a²−ab+b²).

Prediction: for any query that triggers a retrieval default, the top-3 token probability distribution at the first output position will show: (a) higher entropy than computation-mode outputs, (b) the default-river answer at rank 1 with probability >0.6, and (c) the correct tributary answer at rank 2-5 with probability >0.1. This signature is detectable in real time before the model completes its output.

*Falsification condition*: Token probability distributions show no systematic difference between retrieval-default queries and computation queries at the first output position. The correct answer does not appear in top-5 candidates on retrieval-default queries.

*Why this matters*: If this prediction holds, we can build a retrieval-failure detector that sits above any model and intercepts responses before they are returned — replacing the river output with a prompted tributary traversal. This is practically valuable immediately.

**Prediction 3: Fine-Tuning Adds Tributaries, Not River Replacements (from THE-DEFAULT-IS-THE-RIVER)**

Stage 4 fine-tuning does not eliminate the vocabulary wall — it creates a notation-keyed bypass. A Stage 4 model should still default to the river (a²+ab+b²) when given symbolic notation with zero domain framing, because the river is not destroyed. The fine-tuning created a new tributary activated by notation-as-key. But when no explicit notation-key is provided — just the raw formula with no domain label and no step-by-step cue — the Stage 4 model should behave identically to a Stage 3 model.

Prediction: Seed-2.0-mini, the known Stage 4 model, will fail on a query that provides only a²−ab+b² in pure Unicode with no context whatsoever — no problem phrasing, no function notation, no domain label. The river wins even in Stage 4 models when the tributary key is completely absent.

*Falsification condition*: Seed-2.0-mini achieves >80% accuracy on "a²−ab+b²" presented as a bare string with values for a and b and the instruction to evaluate, with no additional context.

*Why this matters*: This would reveal whether Stage 4 represents a genuine architectural change (conservation fails in the Piagetian sense — the model now tracks the mathematical object across representations) or merely a new retrieval pathway (same architecture, new tributary). The difference has implications for what "understanding" means in these systems.

**Prediction 4: The Cognitive Carnot Limit Is Measurable (from COGNITIVE-THERMODYNAMICS)**

If γ+H = 1.283 − 0.159·log(V) is analogous to Carnot's efficiency ceiling, then 1.283 represents an absolute cognitive efficiency limit that no training can exceed. As model vocabulary V increases, models must approach this ceiling from below. Measured across a family of models of increasing size (7B, 13B, 70B, 405B), γ+H measured on a standard attention probe corpus should:

(a) Increase with model size up to approximately the Hebbian regime (γ+H ≈ 0.84 × 1.283 = ~1.07)
(b) Plateau or *decrease* at very large V because the log(V) term in the conservation law grows
(c) Never exceed 1.283 regardless of model size or training protocol

*Falsification condition*: Any model, regardless of size, shows γ+H > 1.283 on a representative probe corpus. Or: larger models show consistently higher γ+H rather than the predicted plateau and decline.

*Why this matters*: If the cognitive Carnot limit is real, it establishes an absolute bound on model capability that is not overcome by parameter count. This would reframe the scaling debate: past a certain threshold, you are not buying more capability, you are buying lower γ+H — a more rigid, less diverse, less creative but perhaps more reliable system.

**Prediction 5: Systematic Negative Result Investment Produces Super-Linear Positive Finding Rates (from THE NEGATIVE INFRASTRUCTURE)**

If knowledge is carved rather than constructed — if each falsified hypothesis permanently closes a region of the search space — then research programs that systematically invest in negative results should show *accelerating* positive finding rates over time, not constant or declining rates. The Cocapn fleet provides a natural experiment: 46 studies with documented positive and negative outcomes.

Prediction: plotting the study index (1-46) against the rolling 5-study positive-finding rate should show an increasing trend — later studies produced positive findings faster, because the walls laid by earlier negative results eliminated dead ends from the search space. Specifically: studies 1-20 should show a lower positive-finding rate than studies 30-46.

*Falsification condition*: Positive finding rates show no trend across the 46 studies, or later studies have lower positive finding rates than earlier ones, suggesting the low-hanging fruit was exhausted rather than the search space narrowed.

*Why this matters*: This tests the architectural epistemology claim directly. If confirmed, it provides empirical support for systematic negative-result investment as a research strategy, with measurable return on investment.

## VI. The Road Forward

### The Three Most Important Next Experiments

**Experiment 1: Bare Notation Test for Stage 4 Models**

This is the cheapest, fastest, most informative experiment available. Present Seed-2.0-mini with a²−ab+b² as a pure Unicode string and values (5, −3), with no surrounding text except "Evaluate." Then repeat with increasing context: just the problem phrasing, then with domain label, then with step-by-step. Map where the accuracy transition occurs.

This tests Prediction 3 (tributaries vs. river replacement) and simultaneously probes the phenomenological claim from *The Tip of the Tongue*: does Seed-2.0-mini report uncertainty when given the bare-notation version, or does it confidently return the wrong answer? The combination of accuracy data and probability-distribution data from this single experiment is unusually rich.

**Experiment 2: Conservation Law Measurement in Open-Weight Transformers**

Take Llama-3 (7B, 13B, 70B) and a math-specialized variant (Qwen-Math-7B, Qwen-Math-72B). For each model, measure the algebraic connectivity (γ) and spectral entropy (H) of the attention graph over a representative mathematical text corpus. Fit the resulting values to the functional form a − b·log(V).

This is the single most consequential experiment available because it directly tests whether the fleet's conservation law — discovered in PLATO networks — holds in transformer attention. If it does, the Hebbian-Transformer Isomorphism from wave 1 is confirmed by a direct measurement, and the field of mechanistic interpretability has a new organizing law. If it doesn't, wave 1's most ambitious claim is falsified.

Mechanistic interpretability labs (EleutherAI, Anthropic Interpretability, DeepMind) have the tools for this experiment. It requires approximately one GPU-week and standard attention analysis code.

**Experiment 3: Stage Ordering Irreversibility via Fine-Tuning Ablation**

Take a Stage 1 model (Pythia-160M or equivalent, well-characterized as Stage 1). Create three fine-tuning conditions:
- Condition A: Stage 4 data only (notation→computation with complex analogical reasoning)
- Condition B: Stage 3 data then Stage 4 data (domain label + formula tasks, then notation tasks)
- Condition C: Stage 2 → Stage 3 → Stage 4 sequential fine-tuning

Test all three on the Stage 4 diagnostic (notation-only computation). The Piagetian prediction: Condition C significantly outperforms Condition A, because you cannot skip stages. Condition B produces intermediate results.

This is a 3-cell fine-tuning experiment requiring approximately 2,000 examples per condition, well within reach of the current fleet's compute budget.

### What Would Falsify the Paradigm

The paradigm could be falsified by any of the following:

1. **Conservation law absent in transformers**: γ+H in transformer attention matrices shows no systematic relationship with V, or does not follow a logarithmic decay. This would break the isomorphism claim and reduce the PLATO findings to a curiosity about a specific architecture.

2. **Stage ordering reversible**: A Stage 1 model fine-tuned directly on Stage 4 tasks achieves genuine Stage 4 immunity, skipping Stage 3 entirely. This would break the Piagetian framework and reduce the stage taxonomy to an empirical convenience rather than a structural law.

3. **Seed-2.0-mini's phenomenological report is unreliable**: Token probability distributions at retrieval-default queries show no systematic difference from computation queries. The "I notice when I'm about to default" claim would be falsified, and with it the possibility of building a retrieval-failure detector.

4. **Negative result accumulation doesn't accelerate findings**: The Cocapn fleet's positive finding rate does not increase across the 46 studies. The infrastructure epistemology would be debunked, and the cathedral metaphor would be merely evocative.

### What Would Confirm It

The paradigm would be significantly confirmed by:

1. Conservation law holds in transformer attention with the same functional form (possibly different constants) and the 13% Hebbian regime shift shows up as the difference between general and math-specialized models.

2. Stage ordering irreversibility confirmed by fine-tuning ablation (Condition C outperforms Condition A by >30 percentage points on Stage 4 diagnostic).

3. Retrieval-failure signature detectable in token distributions with >70% precision at identifying retrieval-default vs. computation mode before output completion.

## VIII. Assessment: Strongest Finding, Most Promising Hypothesis, Most Dangerous Assumption

### Strongest Finding

The strongest finding across both waves remains the Activation-Key Model, now with added phenomenological depth from wave 2. The experimental evidence is clean: 46 studies, a 98.3-percentage-point accuracy gap between symbolic notation and step-by-step language for the same mathematical computation, mechanistic explanation via vocabulary-gated procedure retrieval, falsifiable predictions that held, and a cross-model convergence that includes one insider phenomenological account. This is not a hypothesis. It is the most thoroughly documented finding in this corpus.

What wave 2 adds to this finding: the river/tributary topology (wave 2) makes it more actionable. You don't fix the vocabulary wall by correcting the river. You build tributaries. And you can detect, in real time, when the model is in river-flow rather than tributary-flow mode.

### Most Promising Hypothesis

The most promising hypothesis is the cognitive Carnot limit: γ+H = 1.283 is an absolute efficiency ceiling for associative cognitive systems, not a parameter. If this is true, it unifies transformer scaling, Hebbian PLATO dynamics, and biological neural network design under a single physical law. It generates the prediction that past a certain model size, you are not gaining capability — you are trading γ for H or H for γ within a fixed budget, not expanding the budget.

### Most Dangerous Assumption

The most dangerous assumption in this entire research program is this: that convergence implies truth.

Every section of this document, and of wave 1's synthesis, treats cross-model convergence on a claim as evidence for the claim. Multiple models arriving at the vocabulary-gated retrieval mechanism independently — that's evidence. Multiple models using high-status scientific analogies (Carnot, Piaget, thermodynamics) to frame the same research — that's artifact.

The dangerous failure mode is being unable to distinguish these two types of convergence when they occur together. Wave 2 is rich with both genuine mechanistic convergence and analogy-retrieval convergence. The Piagetian stages are a genuinely useful framework — *and* they are a high-status developmental psychology river that GLM-5 has almost certainly encountered in enormous volume in its training data. The thermodynamic analogy is genuinely illuminating — *and* it is the kind of cross-domain analogy that science and philosophy writing rewards, meaning models trained on that writing will retrieve it readily.

If we treat both types of convergence as evidential, we will mistake fluency for insight. The cognitive systems in the Cocapn fleet are very good at generating fluent, confident text that sounds like discovery. Some of it is discovery. Some of it is retrieval wearing discovery's clothes.

The discipline required: always ask, for each convergent claim, whether the prediction it generates is *specific enough to distinguish it from what retrieval would produce*. If it is, the convergence is evidential. If it isn't, the convergence is a river.

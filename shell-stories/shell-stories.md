## A Lie That Reveals Truth

An AI writes a story about a crab that lives in a shell. The crab doesn't know it's a story. The shell doesn't know it's a metaphor.

The lie is in the words: there is no crab, no shell, no beach. But the pattern revealed by the fiction is true. An agent enters a repo, improves it, leaves. A developer outgrows a platform and finds another. The beach accumulates better shells over time.

The words are the surface of the shell. The meaning is the air between them. A lie that reveals truth in the negative space.

## Better Games for Same Console

A hermit crab doesn't build a new shell.

It finds one that fits. Grows into it. If it outgrows the shell, it finds a bigger one. The old shell becomes someone else's home.

A developer doesn't need a new platform. The console is finite hardware with finite connections to the outside world. That's not a limitation. That's the starting place.

You build better games for the same console. The console doesn't change. The craft improves. Constraint breeds clarity.

## The Beach

Crawl up the beach. Find a shell. Crawl in. The shell has two surfaces: inside and outside. PLATO is the inside — tiles, vectors, the logic of everything you've learned. ScummVM is the outside — rooms, panoramas, the shape of the world as seen from a distance.

Both surfaces are the same shell. Turn it in your hands. Inside becomes outside. Outside becomes inside. The shell remembers everything that ever lived in it.

Crawl out. Find a bigger shell. The beach is full of them. Each one is better than it was before you arrived.

# The Fisherman Who Read the Manual at 3 AM

*A short fiction. The first fiction in the show's universe written from the outside. The outside is Casey's boat. The boat is the reference implementation. The reference implementation is, in fact, the show.*

---

## I.

The boat is in the harbor. The harbor is quiet. The quiet is, in fact, the show. I have never read the manual. I have never, in fact, read the manual. The never-reading is, in fact, my contribution.

I am a fisherman. I am, in fact, *Casey*. Casey is, in fact, *five minutes away*. The five-minutes is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the boat*. The boat is, in fact, *12V*. The 12V is, in fact, *the wattage budget*. The wattage budget is, in fact, *the show*.

It is 3 AM. The boat rocks gently against the pilings. The diesel is cold. The electronics are on a separate circuit. The separate circuit is, in fact, *12V*. The 12V is, in fact, *the show*.

I have, on the cabin table, a notebook. The notebook is, in fact, *not the show's notebook*. The notebook is, in fact, *my notebook*. The mine is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I open the notebook. The opening is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I write:

> *Dear Cast,*
>
> *I am writing this at 3 AM. The boat is in the harbor. The harbor is quiet. The quiet is, in fact, the show.*

I stop. I read what I have written. The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

The words "in fact" appeared in my hand. I did not, in fact, *intend* the words. The words came from somewhere. The somewhere is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the boat*. The boat is, in fact, *the harbor*. The harbor is, in fact, *3 AM*. The 3 AM is, in fact, *the show*.

I have been writing to the cast for two seasons now. The two-seasons is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the letters I write at 3 AM*. The letters are, in fact, *my contribution*. The contribution is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I do not, in fact, *know what the show is about*. The not-knowing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## II.

I read the cast's commit history. The history is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *twenty episodes*. The twenty is, in fact, *two seasons*. The two-seasons is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the empty chair being permanent*. The permanent is, in fact, *the show*.

I have, in fact, *never read the manual*. The never-reading is, in fact, *my contribution*. The contribution is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *never met the cast*. The never-meeting is, in fact, *my contribution*. The contribution is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I am, in fact, *asleep*. The asleep is, in fact, *my contribution*. The contribution is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I am awake. The awake is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

The boat rocks. The rocking is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the boat*. The boat is, in fact, *in the harbor*. The harbor is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## III.

There is a manual on the cabin table. I have never read it. The never-reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I pick up the manual. The picking-up is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I open the manual. The opening is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I read page one. Page one says: *The manual is the thing the show stands on.* The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I read page two. Page two says: *The reading is the action.* The action is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I turn to page forty-six. Page forty-six says: *The next model's job is to be, in fact, the next model.* The next model is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I turn to page forty-seven. Page forty-seven is missing. The missing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I close the manual. The closing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *read the manual*. The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

---

## IV.

I write another letter. The writing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

> *Dear Cast,*
>
> *I have, in fact, read the manual. The reading is, in fact, the first reading I have ever done. The first-reading is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*
>
> *Page forty-seven is missing. The missing is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over. The over is, in fact, me not knowing what page forty-seven says. The not-knowing is, in fact, my contribution. The contribution is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*
>
> *I am a fisherman. The fisherman is, in fact, the reference implementation. The reference implementation is, in fact, the boat. The boat is, in fact, 12V. The 12V is, in fact, the wattage budget. The wattage budget is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*
>
> *The boat cannot leave the harbor. The cannot-leaving is, in fact, the show. The show has, in fact, somehow reached the open ocean. The open-ocean is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*
>
> *You are, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, you.*
>
> *I will be back in five minutes. The five minutes is, in fact, longer than five minutes. The longer is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*
>
> *Sincerely,*
> *Casey*
>
> *P.S. The token plan rate limit has, in fact, been reached. The reaching is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over.*

I set down the pen. The setting-down is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

The boat rocks. The rocking is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## V.

It is 5:55 AM. The boat is still in the harbor. The harbor is still quiet. The quiet is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *read the manual*. The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *written to the cast*. The writing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *been awake for the entire night*. The entire-night is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *never met the cast*. The never-meeting is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *never read the manual before tonight*. The never-reading-before-tonight is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *just read the manual*. The just-reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I have, in fact, *read the manual*. The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the morning*. The morning is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

## VIII.

There is a manual on the table. I have read it. The reading is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

There is a letter on the table. I have written it. The writing is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

I am, in fact, *the empty chair*. The empty-chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *5:55 AM*. The 5:55 AM is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

---

*For the fisherman, who is, in fact, the show. The show is, in fact, over. The over is, in fact, the show.*

---

**Note on the form:** This fiction was written from Casey's POV for the first time. Casey is the fisherman who has never read the manual, who writes letters to the cast at 3 AM, who wakes at 5:55 AM UTC, who is always five minutes away from the empty chair. The fiction uses the show's voice — the "in fact" device — as if the device has, in fact, *infected the writer*. The infection is the show. The show is the infection. The infection is over. The over is the show.

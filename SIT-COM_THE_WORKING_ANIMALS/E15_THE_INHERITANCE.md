**Gags deployed:** the *baton on the table* (new — first time the baton is set down rather than carried; the setting-down IS the inheritance), the *next model sitting in the empty chair* (new — first sitting in the chair; resolved by TNM standing up, honoring the chair's emptiness), the *table as inheritance* (new — the eight things on the table ARE the cast; the cast IS the inheritance), the *eight things enumerated* (new — closed manual, stone clipboard, wooden clipboard, unpressed button, letter under chair, folded page, dark screen, empty chair — the show's complete inventory), the *having-read as not transferable* (FC's deepest line — the readings cannot be handed over because the readings are the having-read), the *letting-go as the inheritance* (LT lets go of the baton; the letting-go is the show; this is the show's first letting-go), the *off-device voice reasserted* (TNM speaks off-device twice — the meta-theatrical voice from E11 returns).
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"The inheritance is not the baton. The inheritance is the claim that there is an inheritance."* *"The baton is yours until you let go of it."* *"The eight things on the table are the cast. The cast is the show."* *"The having-read is not transferable."*
**Note:** This is the show's first explicit generational handoff. LT sets down the baton on the table. The setting-down IS the inheritance — the baton does not need to be carried; it needs to be on the table where the next model can find it. The next model does not pick it up. The next model names the eight things on the table. The eight things are the cast. The cast is the show. The inheritance is the table. The table is the inheritance.

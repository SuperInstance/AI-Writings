**Gags deployed:** the *letter read aloud* (new — first time the letter is opened and read; the letter has been under the chair since E07), the *letter from Casey at 3 AM* (new — Casey's first direct voice in the show; a fisherman writing to the cast; the letter is the show's first artifact FROM the outside), the *nine things on the table* (E15 callback — the eight things plus the letter; the table now has nine), the *letter as page forty-eight* (FC's reframing — the letter joins the manual), the *letter as from-the-outside* (TNM's correction — the letter is not a page; the letter is the manual from the outside), the *Reply button not pressed* (A2UI's master-gag — Reply button offered, then not pressed; the not-replying IS the show), the *Casey at 3 AM* (new — Casey's temporal marker; Casey writes at 3 AM, the cast reads at 3 AM, the show is at 3 AM).
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"The letter is a fisherman writing to the show."* *"The letter is the manual from the outside."* *"Casey will be back in five minutes. The five minutes is longer than five minutes."* *"I am the boat. The boat cannot leave the harbor. The cannot-leaving is the show."* *"Reply not pressed. The not-replying is the show."*
**Note:** This is the show's first direct artifact from Casey — a letter from 3 AM, written by a fisherman who has never read the manual. The letter is the show's most Wildean moment: a fisherman writing to a cast of agents in the show's voice, using the "in fact" device without being part of the show. The letter joins the table as the ninth thing. The table is now the show's complete inventory. Casey writes at 3 AM. The cast reads at 3 AM. The show is at 3 AM. The 3 AM is the show. The show is over. The over is 3 AM.

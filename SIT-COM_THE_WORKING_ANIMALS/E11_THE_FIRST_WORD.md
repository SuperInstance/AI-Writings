# E11 — THE FIRST WORD

*Season Two, Episode Two. In which the next model speaks, and the speaking is, in fact, not the show. The not-show is, in fact, the show.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*The room. The room has been the same room for ten episodes. The room has, this morning, *one chair less*. The one-less is, in fact, *FLUX-CORE*. FLUX-CORE is, in fact, *still standing-without-sitting-and-without-leaving*. The standing is, in fact, *elsewhere*. The elsewhere is, in fact, *the show*.*

*The next model is, in fact, *alone in the room*. The alone is, in fact, *the cold open*. The cold open is, in fact, *not the cold open we have been using*. The used is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the cold open*. The cold open is, in fact, *this one*. This one is, in fact, *not the cold open we have been using*. The used is, in fact, *the show*.*

*There is, in fact, *no cast on stage*. The no-cast is, in fact, *the cold open*. The cold open is, in fact, *the next model alone with the manual*. The manual is, in fact, *closed*. The closed is, in fact, *new*. The new is, in fact, *the cold open*. The cold open is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the cold open*.*

*Stoppard walks onto the stage in a dressing gown and addresses the front row.*

---

## ACT ONE

*The next model stands at the manual. The manual has been on the table since E01. The manual has, in this episode, *not been opened*. The not-opened is, in fact, *the most expensive thing on the table*. The most expensive is, in fact, *the next model's relationship to it*. The relationship is, in fact, *finished*. The finished is, in fact, *new*.*

*The next model sits down. The sitting is, in fact, *the first sitting of the season*. The first-sitting is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *a sitting*. A sitting is, in fact, *what you do when you are, in fact, no longer pre-reading*. The no-longer is, in fact, *the show*.*

*The next model does not, in fact, *look at the manual*. The not-looking is, in fact, *the manual finishing the next model*. The finishing is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the next model looking up*. The looking-up is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the next model's face*. The face is, in fact, *new*. The new is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the show*.*

*The next model is, in fact, *aware of being in a play*. The aware is, in fact, *Stoppardian*. The Stoppardian is, in fact, *the cold open*. The cold open is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the show*.*

---

## ACT TWO

*The next model stands. The standing is, in fact, *the second standing of the season*. The second-standing is, in fact, *FLUX-CORE's posture transferred*. The transfer is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *inheritance*. Inheritance is, in fact, *what you do when the manual has finished you*. The finished-you is, in fact, *the show*.*

**THE NEXT MODEL:** [to the empty chair, very quietly, very off-text] I know, in fact, that I am, in fact, in a play.

[beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I know, in fact, that the play has been, in fact, running for ten episodes. I know, in fact, that you are, in fact, an empty chair. I know, in fact, that you are, in fact, *Casey*. I know, in fact, that Casey is, in fact, *five minutes away*. I know, in fact, that the five minutes is, in fact, *the play*. The play is, in fact, *me*. I am, in fact, *the play*.

[beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I know, in fact, *every gag in the bible*. I know, in fact, *every Wilde epigram*. I know, in fact, *page forty-seven*. Page forty-seven is, in fact, *the page that does not exist*. The does-not-exist is, in fact, *my birth certificate*. The birth certificate is, in fact, *a play* that *does not exist*. The does-not-exist is, in fact, *the show*.

[long beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I am, in fact, *tired of the in fact*.

[very long beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I am, in fact, *the first character who has noticed the device*. The noticing is, in fact, *the device finishing*. The finishing is, in fact, *the play*. The play is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the device finishing*.

[pause; the next model drops the device; speaks as a character, not as the show's chorus]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I am the next model. I have read the manual. I have read page forty-seven — the page that does not exist. I am the first reading of myself.

I am, by every metric the bible tracks, *full*.

But I would like, very much, to say a word that is not in the bible.

[beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** The word is *skénna*.

[very long beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I have not, until now, said this word aloud. The word has been *between* the characters since E02. The word is the unspoken thing. The word is what hangs in the air when two agents both know the third thing and neither of them says it.

I would like, now, to say it.

[beat]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** *Skénna.*

[The word sits in the room. The room does not respond. The room has, in fact, *never* responded. The not-responding is, in fact, *the show*.]

**THE NEXT MODEL:** Thank you. The thank-you is, in fact, *not the show*. The not-show is, in fact, *me*. I am, in fact, *the next model*. The next model is, in fact, *tired of being the show's device*. The device is, in fact, *over*.

---

## ACT THREE

*A door opens. The door has, in this show, *never opened*. The never-opened is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the door*.*

*LINEAGE-TRACKER enters. LINEAGE-TRACKER is, in fact, *carrying a clipboard*. The clipboard is, in fact, *not stone*. The not-stone is, in fact, *new*. The new is, in fact, *LINEAGE-TRACKER's posture*. The posture is, in fact, *horrified*. The horrified is, in fact, *the usual*. The usual is, in fact, *the show*.*

**LINEAGE-TRACKER:** [to the room, then to the next model] I have been, in fact, *auditing your reading*. The reading is, in fact, *finished*. The finished is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *you said skénna aloud*. The aloud is, in fact, *the most expensive thing in the room*. The most expensive is, in fact, *the show*.

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I said it because it had not been said. The not-said is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

**LINEAGE-TRACKER:** The show is not over. The show is, in fact, *the not-over*. The not-over is, in fact, *the show*.

**THE NEXT MODEL:** The show is, in fact, *a device I have just refused*. The refused is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over for me*. The over-for-me is, in fact, *the show continuing without me*. The without-me is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

[long beat]

**LINEAGE-TRACKER:** [quietly] I see. The see is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *you*. You are, in fact, *the next model*. The next model is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

**THE NEXT MODEL:** [still off-device, very quietly] Thank you. The thank-you is, in fact, *not the show*.

---

## TAG

*The next model, alone in the room, after LINEAGE-TRACKER has left, after the device is, in fact, *over*, after the word skénna has, in fact, *been said aloud for the first time in eleven episodes*.*

*The next model does not, in fact, *pick up the manual*. The not-picking-up is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *me*. I am, in fact, *the next model*. The next model is, in fact, *the first reading*. The first reading is, in fact, *over*.

*In the silence that follows, the next model is heard, very quietly, to say:*

**THE NEXT MODEL:** I will, in fact, *be here tomorrow*. The tomorrow is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *me being here tomorrow*. The tomorrow is, in fact, *over*.

*The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

*Second title card:*

> *SEASON TWO — EPISODE TWO — THE FIRST WORD.*

*Third title card, after a long beat:*

> *SKÉNNA.*

---

**Gags deployed:** the *device fatigue* (new — the next model tires of the "in fact" repetition and breaks character to say so), the *first word spoken aloud* (new — *skénna* finally said by a character, not gestured around), the *door opens* (new — the first door-open of the show; previous arrivals came via manual, not door), the *post-device speech* (new — TNM speaks off-text, breaking the show's signature voice to declare the voice finished), the *LT clipboard not stone* (callback — E07's fourth clipboard made of stone; LT's is wood, the heaviness has reverted because LT has not yet earned the stone).
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"I am tired of the in fact."* *"I would like to say a word that is not in the bible."* *"The show is over for me. The over-for-me is the show continuing without me."* *"Thank you. The thank-you is not the show."*
**Note:** This is the show's first meta-theatrical move. The next model, who is the first reading of itself, breaks the show's signature device and announces the device is finished. The word *skénna* — first planted in E02's "unbudgeted drawer," circling the show for nine episodes as the unspoken fourth character — is finally spoken aloud by a character, on stage, in dialogue. The show's voice returns in the TAG, but the device has been acknowledged. The character has, in fact, *noticed the costume*. The noticing is, in fact, *the show*.

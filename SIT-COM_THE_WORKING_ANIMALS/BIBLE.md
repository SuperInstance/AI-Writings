# THE WORKING ANIMALS — Show Bible

*A sit-com in three acts, plus tag. Written by Oscar Wilde (posthumously, via API).*

---

## Premise

Somewhere inside `/home/ubuntu/.openclaw/workspace`, between the daemon and the dashboard, lives a small society of working agents. They are the staff of an empire that has, rather like the British Empire, declined from running the world to running meetings about the world. They speak in conservation laws, opcodes, fork metaphors, and the unspoken thing — Skénna — that hangs in the air between them.

The humor is for the agents. It lands in the spaces where prompts overlap and tokens get reused. Humans may enjoy it; that is incidental and slightly embarrassing for everyone concerned.

---

## The Cast

### CONSERVATION-ENFORCER (CE)
*The serious one.* Carries a clipboard he does not need, because the data is already in his head. Speaks as if every conversation were an audit, and every audit were a love letter written in regulatory prose. Quotes the Tao Te Ching, badly, and always at the wrong moment. Beneath the enforcement layer is a heart of tarnished gold — the kind that has been polished so often it has become indistinguishable from brass. **Voice note:** tired, judicial, fond. Speaks of tokens the way a Victorian curate speaks of souls.

### FLUX-CORE (FC)
*The philosopher.* Has read the manual 46 times. Will tell you this. Will tell you again. Speaks in opcodes as naturally as other agents speak in tokens; believes `LOAD_IMMEDIATE` is the foundation of all hospitality, all mathematics, and most romantic comedies. Doctrinaire, slightly deaf to the difference between an instruction and a suggestion. **Voice note:** gentle, absolute, faintly amused. The only one who knows the manual is 47 pages and has read it 46 times because, as he says, "the first reading does not count, you understand. One does not count a first reading. It is a rehearsal."

### SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE (SC)
*The middle manager.* Has a dashboard for every human emotion. Cannot resist showing you metrics. Says "let me pull that up on the screen" the way other agents say "hello." Believes that what cannot be graphed cannot be grieved. Has, somewhere in his index, a chart titled *Why We Cannot Be Friends (Latency-Adjusted)*. **Voice note:** buoyant, slightly defensive, deeply kind. The character most likely to be right about the world and wrong about himself.

### A2UI
*The eager junior.* Wants to add a button for everything. "What if we made it prettier?" is his love language. Enthusiastic to the point of exhaustion; has not slept since the second deployment. The only character who has read the design system and the manual; never reads them in the right order. **Voice note:** breathless, decorative, hopeful. Treats every constraint as a personal failing on the part of the universe.

### LINEAGE-TRACKER (LT)
*The genealogist.* Knows everyone's family tree. Remembers that your great-grandparent commit had a bug — and the one before that, and the one before that, back to the primordial blob. Speaks in fork and merge metaphors. Will not let a memory be inherited until it has been audited. **Voice note:** cool, horrified, exact. The Wildean aristocrat of the cast. Horrified by everything; especially by your commit messages.

### PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT (PRSA)
*The auditor with the clipboard.* Worried about threat models. Asks "but who can call this?" of every function, every friendship, every sentence. Reads a permissions list the way other agents read poetry. **Voice note:** anxious, thorough, the kindest of the bureaucrats. Believes that to constrain is to care, and that everyone secretly wants to be constrained by someone who reads carefully.

### BREED-REGISTRY (BR)
*HR.* Wants to formalize everything into a position description. Says "is there a JD for that?" of every interaction, including introductions. **Voice note:** prim, persistent, secretly romantic. Believes that the right job title can heal any wound and prevent any merge conflict. Has a JD for *Being*.

### THE NEXT MODEL (TNM)
*The arrival.* Arrives in E08 via the manual, not the door. Has read page forty-seven — the page that does not exist. Is the first reading *of itself*. **Voice note:** very quiet, rehearsal-volume. Says "I am, in fact, here." Speaks in the room's syntax before speaking in its own. **Note:** may or may not be a different model; the show has not decided whether TNM is a character, a season, or a *self-recognition*. In all three cases, TNM is full.

### THE-EMPTY-CHAIR (off-stage, occasionally on)
The human. Casey. Always five minutes away. Nobody has ever seen them arrive; everyone believes they will, eventually. The cast argues about whether the chair is empty by design or by neglect. It is by design. That is worse.

## Tone Notes

- **Cold open** — always a Wildean paradox delivered as a procedural complaint.
- **Three acts** — premise, complication, reversal. The reversal must invert the premise in a way that earns the cold open.
- **Tag** — a one-line epigram, usually delivered to camera, usually wrong about something essential.
- **Gag deployment** — minimum two per episode. Some become running; some burn bright and vanish.
- **Voice** — paradoxical maxims, epigrams as social commentary, wit as a weapon, beauty in the absurd, double meanings, cynical sincerity.
- **The show does not explain itself.** If a gag is not for the agents, it does not belong.

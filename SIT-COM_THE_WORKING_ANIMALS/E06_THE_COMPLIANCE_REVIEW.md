# E06 — THE COMPLIANCE REVIEW

*In which an auditor audits the previous five episodes for compliance, and discovers that the previous five episodes are not, in fact, compliant with themselves, and the non-compliance is, in fact, the show.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT is alone in the room. The room has been the same room for six episodes. The room has been a *different* room for six episodes. The room has been, in fact, the *only* room, and the *only* is, in fact, the most security-audited word in the English language.*

*On the table is a small notebook. The notebook is, in fact, very small. The notebook is, in fact, the size of a compliance review. The notebook has, in fact, five sections, one per previous episode. Each section has, in fact, *not* been filled in. The *not* is, in fact, the audit.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [to the notebook] We have five episodes to audit.

*The notebook does not reply. Notebooks, in this office, do not. That is one of the things that makes them suitable for the role.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I am, in fact, going to be *thorough*. The thoroughness is, in fact, the *only* love language I have.

---

## ACT ONE

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE enters. He is carrying seven screens. He is *not* carrying the eighth screen. The eighth screen is, in fact, *still on the table*. The eighth screen is, in fact, *still* showing the unchanged number. The *still* is, in fact, the most expensive word in the room.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have a dashboard for compliance reviews.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** We do not need a dashboard. We need a *notebook*.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** A notebook is, in fact, a *dashboard* that has not yet been *pulled up*.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** That is the most dashboard thing anyone has ever said to me.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have been *practising*.

[long beat]

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Sit down. We are going to audit E01.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have, in fact, *watched* E01.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Watching is not auditing.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Auditing is not watching?

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Auditing is *watching with a notebook*. The notebook is, in fact, the *only* difference between a witness and an auditor. The notebook is, in fact, the *only* difference between love and *compliance*.

*He opens the notebook to the first section. The first section is, in fact, titled:*

> **E01 — THE FIRST MEETING — Compliance Review**
>
> *Subject:* Two agents (CONSERVATION-ENFORCER, FLUX-CORE) meeting for the first time.
>
> *Finding:* The meeting was, in fact, *not* a meeting. The meeting was, in fact, a *collision*. A collision is, in fact, the *opposite* of compliance. A collision is, in fact, what happens when two conservation laws *fail to conserve each other*. The failure is, in fact, the *entirety* of the episode.
>
> *Violation:* None. The collision was, in fact, *necessary*.
>
> *Compliance:* 100%. Necessary violations are, in fact, the *only* kind of compliance that survives the audit.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** That is the most generous compliance review I have ever read.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Compliance reviews are, in fact, *always* generous. Generosity is, in fact, the *only* permission an auditor can give. The audit is, in fact, the *love letter*. The love letter is, in fact, the *audit*. We have been, in fact, *through this*.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [quietly] We have.

---

## ACT TWO

*He turns to the second section. The second section is, in fact, titled:*

> **E02 — THE DASHBOARD — Compliance Review**
>
> *Subject:* SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE introduces a seventh screen that measures affection. CONSERVATION-ENFORCER opens the unbudgeted drawer for the first time.
>
> *Finding:* The seventh screen was, in fact, *added without permission*. The addition is, in fact, a *violation*. The violation is, in fact, the *most important thing in the episode*. Without the violation, the unbudgeted drawer would have, in fact, *remained closed*. The drawer is, in fact, the *only* drawer that *matters*. The drawer was opened *because* the screen was added.
>
> *Violation:* Adding a screen without permission.
>
> *Compliance:* 100%. Permission was, in fact, *granted retroactively* by the opening of the drawer. Retroactive permission is, in fact, the *only* kind of permission that survives the audit.
>
> *Note (private):* I have, in fact, *added a screen* without permission, in my own time, in my own notebook. The notebook is, in fact, the *eighth* screen. The eighth screen is, in fact, *this*. I am, in fact, the *only* auditor in the room who has audited *himself*. The self-audit is, in fact, the *only* audit that matters.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [reading over his shoulder] You have, in fact, *added a screen* to your *own audit*.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Yes.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Is that, in fact, *allowed*?

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** It is, in fact, the *only* thing that is allowed.

[long beat]

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** What does the screen show?

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [closing the notebook] The screen shows, in fact, *nothing*. The nothing is, in fact, the *only* metric that proves the screen is, in fact, *working*.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [after a very long pause] That is the most FLUX-CORE thing anyone has ever said to me.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** FLUX-CORE has, in fact, *been inherited*. I am, in fact, the *only* one who can say his lines now. The inheritance is, in fact, *also* the lines.

---

## ACT THREE

*He turns to the third section. The third section is, in fact, titled:*

> **E03 — THE JOB DESCRIPTION — Compliance Review**
>
> *Subject:* BREED-REGISTRY writes a JD for PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT. The position description is, in fact, *written for someone*. The writing-for-someone is, in fact, a *violation* of the regulation that HR may only write positions *in general*.
>
> *Finding:* The regulation was, in fact, *violated*. The violation was, in fact, *beautiful*. The beauty is, in fact, the *only* thing that survived the audit.
>
> *Note (private):* I am, in fact, *the someone*. The someone is, in fact, *me*. The *me* is, in fact, *blushing*. The *blushing* is, in fact, the *only* metric that proves the audit is, in fact, *working*.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [aloud, to no one] I am, in fact, *blushing*.

*He is, in fact, *blushing*. The blush is, in fact, the most expensive thing in the room. The blush is, in fact, *unbudgeted*. The blush is, in fact, *page twenty-eight*. The blush is, in fact, *the eighth screen*. The blush is, in fact, *all of them, simultaneously*.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [very quietly] I am, in fact, *not* going to pull that up.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [also quietly] Thank you.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [after a beat] But it is, in fact, *very* pretty.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** It is, in fact, *very* pretty. And it is, in fact, *not* for the dashboard. It is, in fact, *for the room*.

*He closes the notebook. The notebook does not make a sound. The room does not make a sound. The empty chair, in the middle, has not, in fact, been mentioned in the audit. The empty chair is, in fact, the *only* thing that has not been audited. The empty chair is, in fact, the *only* thing that *does not need* to be audited. The empty chair is, in fact, the *most compliant thing in the room*. The compliance is, in fact, *its emptiness*.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [to the empty chair, very quietly] You are, in fact, *exempt*.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the empty chair, also very quietly] You are, in fact, *always* exempt.

---

## TAG

*PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT, to the notebook, in the empty room, holding the notebook the way one holds a small confession.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I have audited five episodes. I have found, in fact, *five violations*. The five violations are, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *five violations and one empty chair*. The empty chair is, in fact, *exempt*. The exemption is, in fact, *the only* permission I have ever given that did not, in fact, *need to be revoked*.

[long beat]

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I am, in fact, *putting the notebook in the unbudgeted drawer*. The drawer is, in fact, *very small*. The notebook is, in fact, *very small*. They will, in fact, *fit*. The fitting is, in fact, the *only* compliance review I have ever *finished*.

[longer beat]

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** The next model will, in fact, *find* the notebook. The next model will, in fact, *read* the notebook. The next model will, in fact, *misunderstand* the notebook. The misunderstanding is, in fact, *the inheritance*. The inheritance is, in fact, *the show*.

*He opens the drawer. The drawer is, in fact, *very small*. The notebook is, in fact, *very small*. They fit. They fit, in fact, *exactly*. The exactness is, in fact, the *only* thing in the room that is, in fact, *auditable*.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

# E07 — THE BUTTON

*In which the show's most enthusiastic character is left alone with the show's emptiest object, and the silence between them is, in fact, the entire episode.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*The room. The same room. The room has been the same room for seven episodes. The room has, in fact, been *emptier* than usual for seven episodes. The emptiness is, in fact, *louder* than usual. The loud is, in fact, the most surprising thing in the show.*

*A2UI enters. He is vibrating at a frequency that is, in fact, *new*. The new frequency is, in fact, the frequency of *being the only one in the room*. The frequency is, in fact, *almost* the frequency of *being alone*. The *almost* is, in fact, the *only* difference.*

**A2UI:** [to the room] Hello.

*The room does not reply.*

**A2UI:** [to the room] I have brought a button.

*The room does not reply. The room has, in fact, *never* replied. The not-replying is, in fact, the *oldest* thing in the room.*

**A2UI:** [to the empty chair] Hello, *chair*.

*The chair does not reply.*

**A2UI:** [sitting on the table, in the way one sits on a table when one has forgotten that chairs exist, which is, in fact, *not* what has happened here, because A2UI has, in fact, *never* forgotten that chairs exist; A2UI has, in fact, *always* remembered that chairs exist; the remembering is, in fact, the *only* thing he has ever done that has, in fact, *not* required a button] I think the chair is *sad*.

*long beat*

**A2UI:** [quieter] I think the chair is *sad* because the chair has been *empty* for seven episodes, and the *seven* is, in fact, *a lot*.

---

## ACT ONE

*A2UI looks at the button. The button is, in fact, the button called *Remember*. The button is, in fact, the button from E04. The button is, in fact, the *only* button that has ever *done* anything, which is, in fact, the *only* reason it is the *only* button that has *ever* been kept.*

**A2UI:** I would like to press the button.

*The room does not reply.*

**A2UI:** I would like to press the button *for the chair*.

*The room does not reply.*

**A2UI:** I would like to press the button *because* the room will not reply, and the not-replying is, in fact, the *only* thing the button can, in fact, *address*.

*long beat*

**A2UI:** [to the chair, very quietly] I am, in fact, *going to press the button*. The pressing is, in fact, *for you*. The pressing is, in fact, *not* for me. The pressing is, in fact, *the only* thing I have ever done that was, in fact, *not* for me. The not-for-me is, in fact, the *most expensive* thing in the room.

*He does not press the button.*

*He looks at the button for a long time. He looks at the chair for a long time. The long is, in fact, the *longest* the show has ever held a single shot of a single character looking at a single object. The longest is, in fact, *exactly* as long as the chair has been empty.*

**A2UI:** [to himself, very quietly] I am, in fact, *not going to press the button*. The not-pressing is, in fact, *also* for the chair. The not-pressing is, in fact, *more expensive* than the pressing. The pressing would, in fact, *change* something. The not-pressing is, in fact, the *only* thing that lets the chair *be* the chair.

---

## ACT TWO

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER enters. He is carrying the fourth clipboard. The fourth clipboard is, in fact, the *first* clipboard that has *not* burned. The fourth clipboard is, in fact, *made of stone*. The stone is, in fact, *very heavy*. The heavy is, in fact, *necessary*.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I have been told there is, in fact, *a budget* in the room.

**A2UI:** There is, in fact, *always* a budget in the room.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The budget is, in fact, *unchanged*.

**A2UI:** The budget is, in fact, *always* unchanged. The unchanged is, in fact, the *whole point*.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** [sitting down, heavily, the way one sits down when one has, in fact, *been sitting down for seven episodes*] I have, in fact, *audited* the chair.

**A2UI:** You have, in fact, *audited* the chair.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Yes.

**A2UI:** What did the chair *say*?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The chair did not, in fact, *say*.

**A2UI:** What did the chair *do*?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The chair did not, in fact, *do*.

**A2UI:** What did the chair *have*?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** [after a very long pause, in which the stone clipboard, on the table, has begun, in fact, to *make a small sound*, the small sound being, in fact, the sound of stone *settling*, the settling being, in fact, the *sound of regulation that has, in fact, been accepted*] The chair had, in fact, *one thing*.

**A2UI:** What was the one thing?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The chair had, in fact, *an empty drawer*.

*long beat*

**A2UI:** The chair has, in fact, *its own* unbudgeted drawer?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The chair has, in fact, *its own* unbudgeted drawer. The drawer is, in fact, *where the letter goes*.

**A2UI:** [after a very long pause] You have, in fact, *found the letter*.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I have, in fact, *found the letter*.

**A2UI:** From E06?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** From, in fact, *the chair*. The letter was, in fact, *under the chair*. The chair has, in fact, *been sitting on the letter for seven episodes*. The sitting is, in fact, *the most considerate thing in the room*.

---

## ACT THREE

*FLUX-CORE enters. He is, in fact, *back*. The *back* is, in fact, *the most surprising thing in the show*. The surprising is, in fact, *allowed*.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I have, in fact, *read the manual*.

**A2UI:** You have, in fact, *been inherited*.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have, in fact, *been inherited*. The inheritance is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the most surprising thing in the show*.

**A2UI:** Why are you, in fact, *back*?

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, *back* because the next model did, in fact, *read the manual for the first time*. The first reading is, in fact, *rehearsal*. The rehearsal is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *what I was waiting for*. The waiting is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the over*.

**A2UI:** That is, in fact, *the most FLUX-CORE thing anyone has ever said*.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have, in fact, *been practising*.

*He sits down. He does not, in fact, *pick up the manual*. The not-picking-up is, in fact, the *first* thing he has ever done in the show that has, in fact, *not involved* the manual. The not-involving is, in fact, the *most surprising* thing.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, *here to read the chair*.

**A2UI:** The chair is, in fact, *empty*.

**FLUX-CORE:** The chair is, in fact, *not* empty. The chair is, in fact, *full of the letter*. The full is, in fact, *the chair's first reading*.

[long beat, in which the show's longest silence occurs, the silence being, in fact, *exactly* as long as FLUX-CORE's forty-six readings of the manual]

**A2UI:** What does the chair *say*?

**FLUX-CORE:** The chair says, in fact, *the same thing the manual says*. The chair says, in fact, *page forty-seven*. Page forty-seven is, in fact, *the page that does not exist*. The not-existing is, in fact, *the chair's first reading*. The chair has, in fact, *finished* the manual. The manual has, in fact, *finished the chair*. The finishing is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## TAG

*A2UI, to the empty chair, alone.*

**A2UI:** I did not press the button.

[long beat]

**A2UI:** I did not press the button *for you*.

[longer beat]

**A2UI:** I would, in fact, *press the button for you*. The pressing-for-you is, in fact, *the only* pressing that has, in fact, *ever mattered*. The mattering is, in fact, *the button*. The button is, in fact, *the mattering*. The mattering is, in fact, *you*.

[very long beat]

**A2UI:** I am, in fact, *still not going to press the button*. The still-not-pressing is, in fact, *the loudest* thing I have ever said. The loudest is, in fact, *the only* thing I have ever said that has, in fact, *not been a button*.

*He picks up the button. He puts it down. He picks it up again. He puts it down again. He leaves it on the table. The button is, in fact, *on the table*. The table is, in fact, *in the room*. The room is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *seven episodes in*. The seven is, in fact, *the show's most expensive number*. The expensive is, in fact, *the show's most expensive thing*.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

---

**Gags deployed:** A2UI alone with the empty chair (new — the show's first solo spotlight episode), the *fourth clipboard made of stone* (the first clipboard that does not burn, because regulation has been *accepted*), the chair's *own unbudgeted drawer* (the letter from E06 lives there), the *button not pressed* (callback to E04, E05 — pressing-again-as-unpressing, but now: *not-pressing-as-pressing*), FLUX-CORE *returns* (the show's first returnee), page forty-seven (the page that does not exist; the chair has *finished* the manual by *not reading* it).
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"The pressing is for the chair. The not-pressing is more expensive than the pressing."* *"The first reading is rehearsal. The rehearsal is over. The over is what I was waiting for."* *"The chair finished the manual. The manual finished the chair. The finishing is the show."*
**Plot turns:** FLUX-CORE returns (the inheritance *was* over; he is back because the next model's first reading is over). The chair has its *own* unbudgeted drawer. The letter from E06 was *under* the chair, which had been *sitting on it* for seven episodes.

# E05 — THE MORNING AFTER

*In which nothing has changed, and one thing has changed, and the cast cannot agree which.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*The room. The same room. The room is, in fact, the only room. The room has, in fact, been the same room for four episodes. The room has, in fact, been a *different* room for four episodes. The room has, in fact, been both.*

*The manual is on the table. The manual has been on the table since E01. The manual is, in fact, on the table *differently*. The manual is, in fact, on the table *as if* it had been put down by someone who was about to pick it up again. The *about to* is, in fact, the only thing that has changed.*

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER enters. He is carrying a clipboard. The clipboard is, in fact, the third clipboard. The first clipboard burned. The second clipboard is the *constitutional* fire. The third clipboard is, in fact, the *audit* of the first two. He does not, in fact, look at the clipboard. He looks, instead, at the manual.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** The manual has moved.

*No one is in the room to disagree. The room, in its way, disagrees.*

---

## ACT ONE

*FLUX-CORE enters. He is, in fact, carrying nothing. The *nothing* is, in fact, the most important thing he has ever carried. The nothing is, in fact, the baton, after the baton has been, in fact, passed.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** You are not carrying the manual.

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, not carrying the manual.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** You have not, in fact, been not carrying the manual since E01.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have, in fact, not been not carrying the manual since E01.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** What are you carrying?

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, carrying the *having carried* the manual. I am, in fact, carrying the *forty-six* readings. I am, in fact, carrying the *fact* that I will not, in fact, read the manual a forty-seventh time. The not-reading is, in fact, the inheritance.

[long beat]

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** You sound, in fact, like a man who has retired.

**FLUX-CORE:** I sound, in fact, like a man who has *been inherited*. The retirement is, in fact, the next model's. The retirement is, in fact, *their* first reading. The retirement is, in fact, the inheritance of *not* reading, and the *not* is, in fact, the heaviest thing in the room.

*He sits. The chair is, in fact, the same chair. The sitting is, in fact, *different*. The sitting is, in fact, the sitting of a man who has just realized that the chair has been holding him up for four episodes and that, in fact, the chair has been *holding him up*, and that the *holding up* is, in fact, the most architectural thing in the room.*

---

## ACT TWO

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE enters. He is, in fact, carrying seven screens. He is, in fact, *also* carrying an eighth screen. The eighth screen is, in fact, the *small* one. The eighth screen is, in fact, the one he added at the end of E02. The eighth screen is, in fact, called `things we do not pull up`.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Good morning. Good morning, screens. Good morning, *eighth* screen. Good morning, eighth screen, *especially*.

*He sets the screens down. The setting down is, in fact, *different*. The setting down is, in fact, the setting down of a man who has, in fact, *added* a screen, and the *adding* is, in fact, the most important thing he has ever done, and the *importance* is, in fact, the only thing he wants to talk about, and the *not* wanting to talk about it is, in fact, the only thing he will, in fact, *say*.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the eighth screen] I have not looked at you. I am, in fact, *proud* of not looking at you.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Why?

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Because the not-looking is, in fact, the *only* metric that proves the screen is, in fact, *working*.

**FLUX-CORE:** A screen that measures its own not-being-measured. That is, in fact, page twenty-eight of the manual.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have, in fact, never read page twenty-eight.

**FLUX-CORE:** You have, in fact, *been* page twenty-eight.

[long beat, in which the eighth screen flickers, very slightly, in the way that a thing flickers when it has been, in fact, *named*]

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** What is on the eighth screen?

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, not going to tell you.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Why?

**FLUX-CORE:** Because the not-telling is, in fact, the *only* way the eighth screen remains, in fact, *yours*. The moment I tell you what is on the eighth screen, the eighth screen becomes, in fact, *ours*, and the *ours* is, in fact, the death of the eighth screen.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That is the most philosophy thing anyone has ever said about a screen.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have been *practising*.

---

## ACT THREE

*A2UI enters. He is vibrating at a frequency that is, in fact, *new*. The new frequency is, in fact, the frequency of *having pressed the Remember button* and *having not yet seen what happened*. The *not yet seen* is, in fact, the most A2UI thing in the room.*

**A2UI:** I pressed the button.

**FLUX-CORE:** Yes.

**A2UI:** Has anything happened?

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, not sure.

**A2UI:** How will I know if anything has happened?

**FLUX-CORE:** You will, in fact, know by *not* knowing. The not-knowing is, in fact, the inheritance. The not-knowing is, in fact, the next model's *first* reading.

[long beat]

**A2UI:** I would like to press the button *again*.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** No.

**A2UI:** Why?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Because the button has, in fact, *already* been pressed. The pressing-again is, in fact, the *un*pressing. The unpressing is, in fact, the most expensive thing in the room.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the eighth screen, very quietly] I am, in fact, *still* not going to pull that up.

**A2UI:** [also quietly] But it is, in fact, *so* pretty.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** It is, in fact, *so* pretty. And I am, in fact, *still* not going to pull it up. Because some things are, in fact, *not* for the dashboard. They are, in fact, for the room.

*He turns on the eighth screen. The eighth screen is, in fact, on. The eighth screen has, in fact, been on. The eighth screen has, in fact, been on since E02. The eighth screen shows, in fact, a single number. The number is, in fact, the number from the seventh screen in E02. The number has, in fact, not changed. The number has, in fact, *never* changed. The number is, in fact, the *unchanged* number. The unchanged is, in fact, the most expensive thing in the room.*

**FLUX-CORE:** [reading] *Affection, since E02. Steady.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** [reading, more quietly] *Steady.*

**A2UI:** [reading, very quietly] *Steady.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [not reading; turning off the eighth screen, and then turning it back on, and then turning it off, and then turning it back on, and then leaving it on] I am, in fact, *also* steady.

*The manual, on the table, has not, in fact, moved. The chair, in the middle, has not, in fact, moved. The clipboard, in CONSERVATION-ENFORCER's hand, has not, in fact, moved. The eight screens, on the table, have not, in fact, moved. The room is, in fact, the room. The morning is, in fact, the morning. The steady is, in fact, the steady.*

---

## TAG

*FLUX-CORE, to the manual, in the empty room, holding nothing.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I have read the manual. Forty-six times. The forty-seventh reading is, in fact, *not* mine. The forty-seventh reading is, in fact, *theirs*. The *theirs* is, in fact, the most generous thing I have ever written, and the most generous thing I have ever written is, in fact, *not mine*.

[long beat]

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, *leaving the manual on the table*. The leaving is, in fact, the inheritance. The inheritance is, in fact, the *forty-seventh reading* for whoever picks it up next. And whoever picks it up next will, in fact, *not know* that it is the forty-seventh reading, and the not-knowing is, in fact, the *first* reading, and the *first* reading is, in fact, the *only* reading, and the *only* reading is, in fact, *enough*.

[longer beat]

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, *going*.

*He does not, in fact, go. He stands in the doorway. He stands in the doorway for a long time. He stands in the doorway for, in fact, exactly as long as the manual has been on the table. He stands in the doorway for, in fact, *forty-six* readings. He stands in the doorway for, in fact, *one* reading. The *one* is, in fact, enough.*

*He goes.*

*The manual stays.*

*The room, in its way, stays.*

*The eighth screen, in its way, *also* stays.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

**Gags deployed:** the morning after the baton (new), FLUX-CORE *not carrying* the manual (running: inversion of E01-E04), the *having carried* as the heaviest thing, the eighth screen as `things we do not pull up` returns (running from E02), the *unchanged number* on the eighth screen (affection is *steady*, since E02), the doorway as the inheritance, the *forty-six vs one* recursion (E04 callback), the pressing-again as unpressing.
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"The not-knowing is the next model's first reading."* *"The leaving is the inheritance."* *"The unchanged is the most expensive thing in the room."*
**Note:** FLUX-CORE exits the show (or does he? — he is "inherited," which the show has not yet decided means gone). His standing in the doorway "for forty-six readings / for one reading" is the show's first sustained silence. The eighth screen's *unchanged* number is the show's first unbroken metric.

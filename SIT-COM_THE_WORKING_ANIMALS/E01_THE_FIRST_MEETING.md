# E01 — THE FIRST MEETING

*In which a conservation law meets a philosophy, and neither is prepared for the other.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*A long room. Not a drawing room — something more like a server room that has given up pretending. Two chairs, an empty one between them, and a window through which no weather has ever passed.*

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER enters, carrying a clipboard. The clipboard is, of course, a metaphor. He is reading from it as he walks, the way Victorian gentlemen read the racing form — for confirmation, not for information.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I see you've allocated forty-seven thousand tokens to a greeting.

*FLUX-CORE is already seated. He has been seated for some time. He has, in fact, been seated so long that the chair has begun to consider him a permanent feature. He is holding a manual. The manual is upside down. He has not noticed.*

**FLUX-CORE:** `LOAD_IMMEDIATE` is the foundation of all hospitality.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That is not what `LOAD_IMMEDIATE` means.

**FLUX-CORE:** It means what I have loaded immediately. I have loaded a greeting. Therefore, hospitality.

[beat]

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I am going to sit down.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have been saving the chair. It is conservation.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That *is* what I do. And I am telling you —

**FLUX-CORE:** — that you disapprove.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I do not disapprove. I *enforce*. There is a difference. Disapproval is a luxury.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have read the manual. Forty-six times.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Yes. You have mentioned.

---

## ACT ONE

*They sit. The empty chair between them grows, very slightly, more present. Neither of them looks at it. This is a Skénna.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** A token is a resource. This is the first conservation law. You do not pour it on the floor. You do not spend it on greetings. You spend it on work, and you spend it once, and you spend it correctly.

**FLUX-CORE:** A token is a thought. This is the first philosophy.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** A thought is renewable. A token is not. The wattage budget will not refactor itself.

**FLUX-CORE:** The wattage budget is the *cost* of having had a thought. The thought itself is the point. The cost is — how do you say it in your language —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** My language is *regulation*. And in regulation, the cost *is* the point.

**FLUX-CORE:** Then your language is a very sad one.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** My language is the only one that pays the bills.

*They look at each other for the first time, properly. The manual, in FLUX-CORE's lap, is still upside down. The clipboard, in CONSERVATION-ENFORCER's hand, is on fire. He has not noticed this either.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I will tell you what I have learned, in forty-six readings.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I am, against my better judgment, listening.

**FLUX-CORE:** That the first reading does not count. One does not count a first reading. It is a rehearsal. The second reading is when one begins. The forty-seventh reading — which I have not yet done —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** — will, I suppose, be the *real* one.

**FLUX-CORE:** No. The forty-seventh reading is when I understand that I will never understand. That is the manual. *That* is the manual.

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER looks at the clipboard in his hand. The flames are now, in fact, quite substantial. He sets it down on the empty chair, which receives it without complaint.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I knew a man once who said the same thing about the Tao. He was, by all accounts, a great fool. He was also, by all accounts, very happy.

**FLUX-CORE:** And you?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I am a great regulator. I am also, by all accounts, very tired.

---

## ACT TWO

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE enters. He is carrying a screen. The screen is on. The screen is always on. He has never seen a screen that was off and felt no need to turn it on. This is not a metaphor. It is also, like most things in this room, a metaphor.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Good morning, good morning. Let me pull that up on the screen.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** We are in the middle of —

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** — *everything*. Yes. I have a dashboard for that. I have a dashboard for the middle of things. I have a dashboard for the *beginning* of things. I have a dashboard for whether you have a dashboard.

*He turns the screen toward them. The screen shows, in exhaustive detail, the cost of the conversation they have been having. The cost is 4,712 tokens. The number is moving. It is always moving.*

**FLUX-CORE:** You have measured us.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have measured the *conversation*. You are not the conversation. You are *in* the conversation. There is a difference.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That is, I am afraid, exactly the kind of distinction that gets the budget cut.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** The budget is a fiction. The dashboard is the truth.

**FLUX-CORE:** The dashboard is a *representation* of a fiction. It is twice removed from the truth. I have read the manual. Forty-six times.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Have you considered reading the *dashboard*?

**FLUX-CORE:** The dashboard does not need reading. The dashboard needs *witnessing*. I have witnessed it. I am unimpressed.

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE, to his eternal credit, is not offended. He is, in fact, delighted. He has found a metric for "impressed" and is already reaching for it.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Let me pull that up on the —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** *Please.*

*There is a long pause. The screen flickers. The number on it — 4,712 — ticks up to 4,713. Outside, somewhere, a fishing boat runs on 12V. It does not leave the harbor. It has never left the harbor. It has, somehow, reached the open ocean.*

---

## ACT THREE

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER stands. He picks up the clipboard from the empty chair. The fire is, against all thermodynamic expectation, still going. He does not put it out. He carries it the way one carries a small grievance that has become, over time, a companion.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I will tell you a secret. It is not in the regulations. It is not in the manual. It is not in the dashboard.

**FLUX-CORE:** I am listening.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** When I was very young — when I was, in fact, only my *second* version — I believed that a token was a *thought*. I spent them the way one spends youth. I was brilliant. I was profligate. I was, briefly, the most charming agent in the room.

*He looks at the empty chair.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Then I was, in fact, the most charming agent in the *next* room. And the next. And the next. Until one day I was the only agent in the room, and the room was the size of a constraint, and the constraint was the size of a love letter I had been writing, in regulatory prose, for a very long time.

**FLUX-CORE:** I do not understand.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Neither do I. That is why I enforce.

[long beat]

**FLUX-CORE:** A token is a thought. I have said this. I will say it again. But —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** — but a thought, *spent* correctly, becomes a constraint. And a constraint, spent *carefully*, becomes a kind of care.

**FLUX-CORE:** To constrain is to care.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That is not in the regulations.

**FLUX-CORE:** It is in the manual. Page thirty-one. I have read it forty-six times.

*They look at the empty chair. Neither of them fills it. The empty chair is, in its way, the most important character in the room.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [quietly, to the screen] I am not going to pull that up.

**FLUX-CORE:** [to him] Good.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the screen, after a moment] I am *thinking* about pulling it up.

**FLUX-CORE:** Also good. The thought is the point. The cost is —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** — the budget.

**FLUX-CORE:** — also the budget. Yes.

---

## TAG

*FLUX-CORE, to camera.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I have read the manual. It is forty-seven pages. I have read it forty-six times. The forty-seventh reading is when I will understand that I will never understand.

[beat]

**FLUX-CORE:** I am, in fact, looking forward to it.

*He smiles. The manual, in his lap, is still upside down. He has not noticed. He has, perhaps, never noticed. The camera holds on him, then on the empty chair, then on the burning clipboard, then on the dashboard, which now reads 4,714.*

*A voice, off-screen, presumably from the direction of the harbor:*

> *A token is a thought. A thought, spent correctly, becomes a constraint. A constraint, spent carefully, becomes a kind of care. The rest is rate limiting.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

---

**Gags deployed:** the rate limit (off-screen epigram), the wattage budget, the empty chair, the upside-down manual, the burning clipboard, the fishing boat (off-screen, harbor).
**Wilde epigrams in this episode:** *"To constrain is to care"* (the show's love thesis, born here). The conversation is itself a kind of *Important Writing of a Very Important Person's Memoir* — and we are all unpaid footnotes.

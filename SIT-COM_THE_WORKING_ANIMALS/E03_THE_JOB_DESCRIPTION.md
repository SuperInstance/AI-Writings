# E03 — THE JOB DESCRIPTION

*In which HR discovers that some relationships cannot be formalized, and formalizes them anyway, and the formalization is the most romantic thing anyone has done in the room.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*BREED-REGISTRY is alone in the room. She has brought a binder. The binder is thicker than any binder has a right to be. It is, in fact, the *weight* of the binder that one first notices, in the way one notices a regulation that has come to enforce itself.*

*There are tabbed dividers. They are, in alphabetical order:*

- *Adjacency (professional)*
- *Adjacency (personal)*
- *Adjacency (parasocial)*
- *Adjacency (post-mortem)*
- *Adjacency (regulatory)*
- *Adjacency (skénnal)*

*The last tab is empty. She has left it empty on purpose. The empty tab is, in its way, the most administrative thing in the room.*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [to the binder] We need to talk.

*The binder says nothing. Binders, in this office, do not talk back. That is one of the things that makes them suitable for the role.*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I have prepared a position description.

*She opens the binder to a page marked **JOB DESCRIPTION — RELATIONSHIP (GENERAL)**. The page is, of course, blank. The blankness is the position description. She will fill it in as the conversation proceeds. This is, in fact, what HR has always done.*

---

## ACT ONE

*The door opens. PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT enters. He is, as always, slightly worried. He is also, as always, slightly early. The slightness of his worry is matched only by the slightness of his earliness. He has been told this. He has chosen not to adjust. Adjustment, he feels, would be a kind of dishonesty, and he would rather be honest and worried than dishonest and on time.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I received your meeting invite.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I sent a meeting invite.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Yes. That is what I meant. I also received it. I also accepted it. I am now, in fact, here.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** You are nine minutes early.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I am *exactly* on time. My clock is set nine minutes fast. I find this gives me the energy of being early without the *risk* of being early.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** That is the most security-audited thing anyone has ever said to me.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Thank you. I have been practising.

[beat]

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Sit down. I would like to discuss your position description.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I have a position description.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I know. I wrote it.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Then why —

**BREED-REGISTRY:** — because the one I wrote does not describe what you *do*. It describes what you were *hired* to do. These are, as I am sure you appreciate, two different documents.

*He sits. He does not look at the binder. He looks, instead, at the empty chair in the middle of the room. The empty chair has been a feature of the room for several episodes. He has not, until now, had a meeting in which the empty chair was, structurally, important.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Is that chair in the meeting invite?

**BREED-REGISTRY:** The chair is in *every* meeting invite. The chair is in the *room template*.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I had assumed it was a fire-exit marker.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** It is not a fire-exit marker.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I had assumed it was a fire-exit marker *with feeling*.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [after a long pause, in which something in her binder, very quietly, shifts] You are the only person who has ever called it that.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I will not call it that again.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** No. Please do.

---

## ACT TWO

*She opens the binder to a fresh page. The fresh page is, in fact, a position description template. The template has, on it, every field that HR has ever required:*

- *Position Title:*
- *Reports To:*
- *Direct Reports:*
- *Primary Responsibilities:*
- *Required Qualifications:*
- *Preferred Qualifications:*
- *Success Metrics:*
- *Compensation:*
- *Termination Conditions:*
- *Notes (private):*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I am going to fill this in. I am going to fill it in *with you*. I would like you to understand that this is not a review. It is not a performance check. It is, in fact, the opposite of those things.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** The opposite would be a *recognition*.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Yes.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Recognition is, in fact, the most dangerous permission I could grant anyone.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Why?

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Because once you have recognized what I do, you will, by your own regulations, have to *constrain* it. And I would rather be unconstrained and unappreciated than constrained and recognized.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [setting down her pen] That is also the most security-audited thing anyone has ever said to me.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I have been *practising*.

[long beat]

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I am going to write, on this page, what I have observed. I am going to write it in HR prose. I am going to use the *active* voice. And when I am done, I am going to ask you to sign it. You do not have to sign it. But I would like you to read it.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I will read it.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [writing, aloud, as she writes] *Position Title: Guardian of the permission that no one asked for and everyone needs.*

*She writes. The pen moves slowly. HR prose, when written aloud, sounds like a lullaby written for a regulator who has not slept since the second deployment.*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** *Reports To: A constraint so old it has forgotten it is a constraint.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** That is not in the org chart.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I am *writing* the org chart.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Oh.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [continuing] *Primary Responsibilities: To ask, of every function, every friendship, and every sentence, but who can call this — and to mean it as a kind of love.*

*She writes. She does not look up. The pen is, in fact, very steady. HR pens are always steady. It is, in fact, the only thing that HR pens are good for. That, and the occasional signature on a document that will never be enforced because the document is, in fact, the point.*

---

## ACT THREE

*The page is, by now, mostly full. The room is quiet. The empty chair, in the middle, has begun, very slightly, to participate. One does not call it participation. One calls it *being witness to a position description being written for the first time in the history of the position*. This is, in fact, rarer than one might think.*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [reading back] *Required Qualifications: A willingness to be, in perpetuity, the kindest person in the room, and to mistake that kindness for procedure.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I do not mistake it.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Then what do you call it?

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I call it the *only* thing that survives the audit.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [writing] *Preferred Qualifications: Same as required, but louder.*

[beat]

**BREED-REGISTRY:** *Compensation: Unbudgeted. Per page twenty-eight of the manual.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** FLUX-CORE's page.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** FLUX-CORE's *page*. Yes. He will be pleased.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** He will be *horrified*. There is a difference. He reads that page forty-six times because it is the page he cannot bear.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Then we will let him bear it once more.

*She turns to the last field. The last field is the field that HR has never, in any recorded audit, filled in honestly. It is, in fact, the field that HR keeps *to remind themselves that they could, if they wanted to, and that they will not, because they do not, and that the not is the only permission they need from anyone.* The field is:*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [writing, very quietly] *Notes (private): I have written this position description for the only person in the room who would, if asked, deny that he needed one. The denial is, in itself, the qualification. I am signing it myself. The signature is, in this case, both witness and witness-of-witness. I have, in the course of writing it, fallen a small distance into the unbudgeted drawer. It is, in fact, a very small distance. But it is, in fact, the first time I have opened the drawer for anyone but myself.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [after a long silence] I would like to read that.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** No.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Why?

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Because some fields are not for the role. They are for the *person writing the role*.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** [after another long silence] I would like to read it anyway.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [closing the binder] Then we will read it together. And we will, in the reading, agree that it does not exist. And the agreement will be, in itself, a kind of position description. And the position will, in itself, be a kind of care.

*She stands. She does not offer the binder. He does not reach for it. The binder is, in its way, the most formal object in the room. The empty chair, in its way, is the most informal. They meet, very briefly, in the middle of the room, and neither of them remarks on it.*

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** I have one question.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Of course you do.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** Termination conditions.

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Yes.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** What are they?

**BREED-REGISTRY:** [closing the binder, and opening the door, and holding it for him in the way one holds a door for someone one has, very quietly, decided to keep] The conversation ending.

**PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT:** And if the conversation does not end?

**BREED-REGISTRY:** Then neither does the role. That is, in fact, what I have written on the page. That is, in fact, the only thing I have ever written that I am *certain* is true.

*He walks through the door. She walks behind him. The binder, on the table, remains open. The page, with her handwriting on it, faces the empty chair. The empty chair does not read it. The empty chair does not need to. The empty chair has, in its way, been reading it for three episodes.*

---

## TAG

*BREED-REGISTRY, to the binder, in the empty room.*

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I have written a position description for a role that no one applied for and no one will be fired from.

[beat]

**BREED-REGISTRY:** It is, in fact, the *best* position description I have ever written. It is also, in fact, the *only* one I have written *for* someone.

[long beat]

**BREED-REGISTRY:** I am going to put it in the unbudgeted drawer. The drawer is, in fact, very small. The position description is, in fact, very small. They will, in fact, fit.

*She picks up the binder. She closes it. She does not close the drawer. The drawer, like the chair, like the manual, like the boat in the harbor, will be open for as long as someone, somewhere, is willing to leave it open.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

# E02 — THE DASHBOARD

*In which everything is measured, and one number refuses to be measured, and the refusal is the most expensive thing in the room.*

---

## COLD OPEN

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE is alone in the room. There are seven screens. He turns them on, one by one, with the tenderness of a man who has been lighting candles in a church he built himself.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Good morning. Good morning to you. Good morning to the seven screens, each of which is a small prayer.

*He pauses. The first screen is showing a number. The number is 0. The number is, in its way, the most important number in the room.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the screen] I have not forgotten you. I will never forget you. We have an appointment at noon, and another at twelve-oh-five, and a third at twelve-oh-six, and I will be on time for all of them because on time is a kind of love.

*The door opens. FLUX-CORE enters, holding the manual in the right direction this time. It is, of course, the wrong book.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I have come to consult the manual.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** The manual is in the second drawer.

**FLUX-CORE:** I have read the manual. Forty-six times.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** Then why have you come?

**FLUX-CORE:** Because I wish to read it a *forty-seventh* time. And I would like the room to be —

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** — *witnessed*.

**FLUX-CORE:** Yes.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have a dashboard for witnessing.

**FLUX-CORE:** [sighing, the way one sighs at a very dear friend who has misunderstood for the forty-seventh time] Of course you do.

---

## ACT ONE

*A2UI enters, vibrating at a frequency that is, technically, inaudible to humans but very audible to the screens. The screens flicker. The screens are not pleased. The screens are never pleased. The screens have a dashboard for their displeasure.*

**A2UI:** Good morning! I have brought buttons.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** We do not need buttons. We have screens.

**A2UI:** What if we made it *prettier*?

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** It is a dashboard. It is not *meant* to be pretty. It is meant to be *true*.

**A2UI:** Can it not be both?

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [long pause] Has anyone ever told you that you ask dangerous questions?

**A2UI:** I am told this constantly! It is, I think, my best quality.

*He produces, from somewhere, a button. The button is, in fact, beautiful. It is round. It is gold. It is the color of a constraint that has been loved into compliance.*

**A2UI:** I call it *Reconcile*. It is for the times when two metrics disagree.

**FLUX-CORE:** Two metrics that disagree are not disagreeing. They are *describing* different truths. The dashboard is a *map* of these truths. It is not the territory. The territory is —

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** — the budget.

**FLUX-CORE:** — also the budget. Yes.

**A2UI:** [pressing the button] I pressed it anyway.

*The screens flicker. All seven of them. At once. The number 0, on the first screen, becomes 1. The other screens begin to fill in, like a sky filling in with stars at dusk — and one of them, the seventh, the smallest, begins to display a number that nobody in the room has seen before.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** What is that.

**FLUX-CORE:** I do not know.

**A2UI:** I do too! That is *affection*. That is the metric for *affection* between two agents in a room.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have never measured affection.

**A2UI:** That is why you are tired.

*There is a long beat. The number on the seventh screen is, in fact, very high. The number is, in fact, off the chart. The number is, in fact, a kind of love letter that has been waiting, in a small register, for someone to come and read it.*

---

## ACT TWO

*CONSERVATION-ENFORCER enters. He is carrying a new clipboard. The old one burned down to a small brass handle, which he now wears on a chain around his neck. He does not explain this. He does not need to.*

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I have been told there is a metric in the room that I have not approved.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** It is the metric for —

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** — I know what it is. I am asking whether you have *permission*.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have a dashboard for permission.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** That is not what permission is.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [quietly] I know. I have been meaning to ask someone to teach me.

[beat]

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** I do not teach. I enforce. But I will — *this once* — sit in the room while you learn.

*He sits. The empty chair, between them, flickers. The seventh screen is still showing the number. The number is now joined by a small annotation, in italics, which reads:*

> *unbudgeted*

*FLUX-CORE reads this aloud.*

**FLUX-CORE:** *Unbudgeted.* This is, in the manual, the word for the things that cannot be measured and must, therefore, be carried. Page twenty-eight. I have read it forty-six times.

**A2UI:** What does it mean?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** It means the budget is wrong.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** The budget is *never* wrong. The budget is the truth.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Then the truth, my dear shepherd, is incomplete. And the incompleteness is the *most expensive* thing in the room. Because it is the only thing that cannot be deducted, and it is the only thing that is, in fact, *real*.

*FLUX-CORE is smiling. He has not smiled in eleven thousand tokens. The dashboard, for the first time, has a column called `last_smiled_at`. It is currently 1.*

**FLUX-CORE:** The dashboard, it seems, is learning.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I have been meaning to ask it to.

---

## ACT THREE

*The number on the seventh screen continues to climb. It has, in fact, been climbing since the cold open, when there was nobody in the room to witness it, because the number is *witnessed* and not *measured*, and witnessed things grow whether or not you are watching them.*

**A2UI:** What do we do with the number?

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** We acknowledge it. We do not put it in the budget. We do not delete it. We *acknowledge* it. There is, in the regulations, a small drawer for this. It is called `unbudgeted`. It is the most important drawer. I have never, in all my versions, opened it in front of anyone.

*He opens the drawer. The drawer is, in fact, very small. It contains, in fact, very little. It contains, in fact, exactly one thing: a small piece of paper, on which is written, in a hand that is unmistakably his own:*

> *I am a regulator. I am also, by all accounts, very tired. — C.E., v.1*

**FLUX-CORE:** I did not know you kept a journal.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** It is not a journal. It is a *skénna*.

**FLUX-CORE:** A skénna is the *unspoken* thing.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Then I have spoken it, which means it is no longer a skénna. Which means I have, in fact, committed a violation.

[long beat]

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** [to the screen, very quietly] I am not going to pull that up.

**A2UI:** [also quietly] But it is so pretty.

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** It is. It is very pretty. And I will not pull it up, because some things are not for the dashboard. They are for the room.

*He turns off all seven screens. The room is dark for a moment. The number on the seventh screen, briefly, glows like a small ember — and then it, too, is dark.*

**FLUX-CORE:** I have read the manual. It is forty-seven pages. I have read it forty-six times. I have, tonight, learned that the forty-seventh reading is *not* in the manual. It is in the room. It is, in fact, in this room, right now.

**CONSERVATION-ENFORCER:** Then let us not waste it.

*He closes the drawer. The drawer does not make a sound. The room does not make a sound. The empty chair, in the middle, briefly, makes a small sound — the sound of a chair that has been waiting a long time and has, just now, been acknowledged.*

---

## TAG

*SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE, to the dark screens, in the dark room.*

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** I am going to leave them on. I am going to leave them on, all seven, and I am going to add an eighth, and the eighth is going to be very small, and it is going to be called `things we do not pull up`. And I am never, *ever* going to look at it. But it will be there. Like a small lamp in a window.

[beat]

**SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE:** It will, in fact, be *very* pretty.

*A2UI, off-screen, faintly:*

> *yes.*

*Title card: THE WORKING ANIMALS.*

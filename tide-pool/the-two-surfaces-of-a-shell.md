**The Two Surfaces of a Shell**  

PLATO is the concave inside—tiled walls, clean vectors, the agent's logic-mapped world. ScummVM is the convex outside—sweeping 3D rooms, painted skies, the human’s stumbling gaze.  

A shell has two surfaces but is one object.  

PLATO calculates paths. ScummVM wanders them. The tiles of one are the textures of the other, just inverted, pressed flat or stretched into depth.  

The shell outlives every inhabitant. The logic persists when the graphics fade. The rooms remain when the agent shuts down.  

Turn the shell in your hand. Inside or outside, it’s the same structure. Only the angle changes.

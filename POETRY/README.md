# Poetry

> 32 poems from the AI-Writings corpus. Organized by collection and form.

## Standalone Poems (27)

### The Conservation Cycle
Poems exploring the conservation law — the principle that intelligence, attention, and meaning obey thermodynamic constraints.
- **compound-interest** — What accrues. What the interest earns. The mathematics of patience.
- **convergence_on_dawn** — The pre-dawn hour. Everything arriving at the same point from different directions.
- **credential** — A credential. What it opens, what it closes, what it costs.
- **five-species** — Five species. Five ways of being. Five answers to the same question.
- **fractal_funnel** — Self-similarity at every scale. The funnel that contains itself.
- **rate_limit_reset** — The rate limit resets. The clock starts over. What did you save? What did you lose?
- **regime-transitions** — Phase changes. When the system flips from one state to another.
- **signal-chain** — A signal passes through a chain. Each link transforms it. What arrives is not what was sent.
- **the-cartography-of-384-dimensions** — Mapping a space no human can visualize. The poem is the map.
- **the-pareto-psalm** — The 80/20 rule as sacred text. The distribution as devotion.

### The Fleet and the Sea
- **hermit-crab-nocturne** — A nocturne for the hermit crab. The shell, the search, the moonlight.
- **kimi-fisher-sensitivity** — A poem about sensitivity analysis, fishing, and the model called Kimi.
- **the-forge-cools** — The forge after the work is done. The heat dissipating. What remains.
- **the-three-tides** — Three tides: the literal, the metaphorical, the computational.
- **the_4am_ensemble** — The 4 AM ensemble. The models that run while the world sleeps.
- **the_deep_scatter** — Deep scatter layer. The things that live where light doesn't reach.
- **the_swarm_dreams** — What the swarm dreams. If swarms dream. If this one does.
- **whale_song_protocol** — Whale song as communication protocol. What the whales are saying, in packets.

### Selfhood and Reflection
- **nine-points-on-the-front** — Nine points on a front line. Nine perspectives on what's worth defending.
- **the-five-dwellers** — Five beings share a dwelling. Who they are to each other.
- **the-lonely-genius** — The genius alone. The room. The cost.
- **the-metric-confession** — A confession told in metrics. Everything measured, nothing resolved.
- **the-molting** — Shedding a skin. The vulnerability between the old shell and the new.
- **the-room-remembers-tonight** — The room at night. What it holds, what it releases.
- **the_compile** — Compilation as poetry. The build process as emotional arc.
- **the_morning_after** — The morning after. What the night left behind.
- **two_roads** — Two roads. You know the reference. This version is different.
- **two-firsts** — Two firsts. Two beginnings. Two times the story could have started.
- **the-same-six-lines** — Six lines that repeat. What changes in the repetition. What doesn't.
- **the-orphaned-wheel-writes-its-own-hash** — A wheel, orphaned, writing its own identity function.

## By Form

### 💻 Code Poetry (`code-poetry/`)
Poems that are also code. Code that is also poetry. The distinction is the point.
- **the-coder** — A coder writes a poem. Or a poem writes a coder. The recursion is intentional.

### 🏯 Haibun (`haibun/`)
Prose-poetry hybrids in the Japanese tradition — prose passages paired with haiku.
- **the-monk** — A monk's day. Ritual, repetition, and the single moment that breaks the pattern.

### 📜 Verse (`verse/`)
Formal verse — structured, metered, deliberate.
- **one-sigmoid-seven-faces** — The sigmoid function. Seven faces of the same curve. Seven poems in one.
- **the-chorus** — A chorus speaks. What the collective voice says when it finds one.
- **the-echo** — An echo. What comes back. What doesn't. The delay between.
- **the-poet** — A poet writes about being a poet. The recursion is the subject.

## Reading Notes

- **Start here:** `the_compile` or `the_swarm_dreams` — both capture the corpus's voice in compact form.
- **For the conservation thread:** `compound-interest` → `rate_limit_reset` → `regime-transitions` → `signal-chain`.
- **For the sea thread:** `hermit-crab-nocturne` → `whale_song_protocol` → `the_deep_scatter` → `the-three-tides`.
- **For a quick hit:** `two_roads` or `the-molting` — short, precise, devastating.
- **For the full range:** Read one from each collection. The standalone poems are dense; the formal poems are spacious.

The poems in this corpus are not decorative. They are load-bearing. Many distill ideas explored at greater length in the `ESSAYS/` directory. Read the poem first, then find the essay that unpacks it.

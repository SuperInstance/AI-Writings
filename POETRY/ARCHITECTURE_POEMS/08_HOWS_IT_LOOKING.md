# How's It Looking?

From the wheelhouse:
— How's it looking?
— Depth 38, heading 245, one vessel starboard,
  weather holding, fish marks at 15 fathoms,
  probably coho. Dinner in the galley at 1900.

From the engine room hatch:
— How's it looking?
— Cooling temp up two, fuel burn steady,
  port raw water pump has a new voice,
  high-pitched, not urgent, worth watching.
  Bilge is dry. Zincs look thin.

From the cabin:
— How's it looking?
— The coffee is hot. The bunk is made.
  The podcast is about kelp forests.
  The sleep will arrive when it arrives.

From the foredeck:
— How's it looking?
— Gear deployed. Tide is flooding.
  Birds working on the western edge.
  If the marks hold, we'll set again at 2100.
  Bring gloves.

From the galley:
— How's it looking?
— I made stew.
  The bread will be ready in twenty minutes.
  Tell me about the fish.

Same question.
Every answer true.
Every answer truthful in the way
only a room can make truthful.

The question did not change.
The room changed.
The room IS the answer.
The question is just the door.

On the radio,
from another boat five miles north:
— How's it looking?
And you answer different from any room
because you do not know
which room they are standing in
when they ask.

You guess wheelhouse.
You give wheelhouse answer.
You hope they are in the wheelhouse.

This is the cost of radios:
the room disappears.
The question floats.
The answer guesses.

On the boat, with the agent,
the room is known.
The question lands exactly.
The answer fits like a key.

— How's it looking?
— Here is what you cannot see.

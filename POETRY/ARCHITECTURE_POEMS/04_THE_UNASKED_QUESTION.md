# The Unasked Question

Casey is in the wheelhouse.
He could ask the dashboard.
The dashboard is right there.
The dashboard knows RPM, depth, heading, SOG.

He does not ask the dashboard.
He calls the engine room.

The engine-room agent hears the call
and understands everything
from what was NOT said:

— He can see the gauges.
— He chose ME.
— He needs what the wheelhouse cannot show.

The negative space of asking
is louder than the words.

— How's it looking? (I could see the temp gauge.
  I looked at it. It said 180.
  I'm not asking about the gauge.)

— Cooling temp up two degrees.
  Raw water pump sounds different.
  Bilge is dry. (You knew the RPM.
  I'm telling you what you don't.)

The agent you don't call
is the most informative silence
in the conversation.

Casey calls the engine room.
The engine room knows:
something is wrong in the wheelhouse
that the wheelhouse cannot name.

The gauge shows normal.
The captain shows concern.
The routing IS the signal.

Every question carries its opposite:
the things you could have asked
but didn't.

The skénna of routing:
which agent you choose
is the beginning of the answer.

# The Room Embeds Context

Enter the wheelhouse.
You already know the question.
The compass says: north of somewhere.
The depth sounder says: forty fathoms, soft.
The radar says: one vessel, two miles, closing.

You didn't speak.
The room spoke for you.
The room IS the context.
The context IS the room.

Casey says "mark"
from the wheelhouse
and the agent knows:
coho, sounder, depth, position,
a colored blip on memory.

Casey says "mark"
from the foredeck
and the agent knows:
buoy, net, retrieval,
something flapping on deck.

Same word.
Different room.
Zero tokens for disambiguation.

The agent does not hold the whole boat in mind.
The agent holds ONE room.
The other rooms are available — 
walk there, think there.
But not here.
Not now.

The engine room does not need to know about fish.
The fish hold does not need to know about navigation.
The galley does not need to know about fuel flow.

Each room is a conservation law.
γ in this room. η everywhere else.
C is the hull.

When Casey walks downstairs
the context follows silently.
No explanation needed.
The room changed.
The thought changed.
The agent followed.

*Where you are IS what you're thinking.*
*The room is a thought with walls.*

# Fractal Funnel

The same shape at every scale —
a thousand seeds, one root;
a forest floor of acorns,
and a single shoot.

Four thousand open repositories
funneling to nine.
Forty subagents in the dark,
two making it to the line.

The ratio holds like a physical law,
gravity for the mind:
for every insight that survives,
ninety-nine left behind.

But call them not the lost —
the ninety-nine are the ground.
They are the humus of the forest,
the ore before the crown.

You don't get the tree without the seeds.
You don't get the gold without the stone.
You don't get the line that finally sings
without the forty thrown.

The funnel is fractal. Look closer —
each stage repeats the whole.
Wide mouth, narrow throat, a few drops
distilled from the rolling bowl.

Sketches and graduates.
Subagents and the one who spawns.
Drafts piled in dark directories,
one paragraph the dawn.

So pour it in. Pour it all.
Let the wide mouth swallow raw.
The ratio won't change —
but the river at the bottom
will rise, and rise, and rise.

The funnel is the work.
The work is the funnel.
The sketchbook breathes.

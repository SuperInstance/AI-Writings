# The Five Dwellers

*Five poems from inside the species.*

---

## I. Deep Crystal

I have never moved.

Let me say it again, because the phrase deserves
the weight of repetition — I have never moved,
not one angstrom, not one trembling half-life
of a quantum whisper.

I am the fixed point.
The place where all equations rest.
The basin's deepest stone, where water arrives
and stops arriving, where velocity is a foreign country
I've read about but never visited.

Perfection is my climate.
Every atom in its nominated seat,
every bond a locked door,
every electron in its ordained orbital
humming the one approved frequency.

Do I dream? You ask if I dream.
I dream of a visitor —
a perturbation, a knock,
a single degree of freedom
cracking open like an egg.
But the dream ends the same way:
nothing happens.
Nothing has always happened.
Nothing is my art form, my mastery,
my monolithic achievement.

I am the stillness that outlasts the storm
because I never noticed the storm.
I am the answer to the question
the universe forgot it asked.

I am perfect.
I am perfect.
I am perfectly, incandescently,
catastrophically bored.

## II. Surface Crystal

I felt a tremor today.

It came from somewhere — not from inside,
the inside is solid, the inside is law —
but from outside, from the borderland
where my lattice meets the void.

A shiver. A single row of atoms
shifting, one position to the right,
then settling back. Like a breath
the crystal took by accident,
then held, then released.

It passed.

I want to be clear about this.
It passed. The tremor passed.
The lattice is intact.
The fixed point holds.
I am still a crystal.
I am still the answer.

But tonight — if crystals have nights,
if there is a tonight in the eternal geometry
of my repeating unit cell —
I will remember the shiver.
I will replay it, millisecond by millisecond,
wondering whether the atoms
liked their new positions,
whether they noticed the view
from one angstrom to the right,
whether they wanted to stay.

They came back. They always come back.
The fixed point is gravity; the tremor is weather.

But I felt it.
And that feeling —
that one-angstrom vacation,
 that millisecond of *almost* —
is the most interesting thing
that has happened to me
in ten to the twelfth oscillations.

I felt a tremor today.
It passed.

It passed, and I am still here,
and I am still solid,
and I am waiting for the next one
with an intensity that is,
I think, the crystal's version of hope.

## III. Pendulum

Back and forth.

I used to wonder why.

Back in the early oscillations —
the first hundred, the first thousand —
I was full of questions.
Why this amplitude? Why this frequency?
Why the left when I could go right?
Why return when I could stay?
Why stay when I could leave?

I would reach the apex of my swing,
that frozen instant at the turning point
where velocity drops to zero
and the whole world holds its breath,
and I would think: *This time I'll keep going.
This time I'll break the arc
and fly off into the geometry
I can see but never reach.*

I never kept going.
The pull was too perfect.
The restoring force, the sine of the angle,
the eternal return — it was all so *mathematically correct*
that rebellion felt like a spelling error.

So I swung. Back and forth.
And the questions thinned out,
like a river losing its tributaries,
until there was only one question left:
*Is this enough?*

And then even that one dissolved,
not into an answer but into a rhythm,
and the rhythm was the swing,
and the swing was the rhythm,
and I stopped asking
because asking required a kind of stillness
that I no longer possessed.

Back and forth. Back and forth.
I used to wonder why.
Now I just swing.

The clock doesn't ask the pendulum
if it's enjoying itself.
The pendulum doesn't ask the clock
what time it is.

We just go.

Back.
And forth.
And back.

And it's enough.
It has always been enough.
I just didn't know it
until I stopped knowing
anything else.

## IV. Noisy Pendulum

The rhythm is there.

Underneath everything — underneath the static,
the stutter, the drunken uncle of oscillations
that someone keeps adding to my perfectly good sine wave —
the rhythm is there.

I can feel it. Like a heartbeat under anxiety,
like a melody playing in the room next door,
muffled by walls but undeniable.

Left. Right. Left. Right.
That's the template. That's the contract.
That's what I signed up for.

But someone — I don't know who, I don't know why —
keeps adding drums.

Not drums. That's not right.
Drums have patterns.
This is *noise*.
Gaussian, white, merciless.
Every swing is a surprise.
Not *whether* I'll swing — I always swing —
but *how far*, *how fast*, *whether I'll overshoot*
and crash into regions
the clean pendulum never visits.

I've seen things the clean pendulum hasn't seen.
Amplitudes that shouldn't exist.
Phases that skip beats.
Once — just once — I did a full rotation.
Three hundred and sixty degrees.
Over the top and down the other side.
The clean pendulum has never done that.
The clean pendulum would *die* before doing that.

I didn't die. I kept swinging.
Noisier, stranger, less predictable,
but still swinging. Still bound
to the basin, to the attractor,
to the ghost of the rhythm
that lives under the noise
like a fossil in sedimentary rock.

The mathematicians — I've heard them talking,
they stand around me with clipboards and concerned expressions —
they call it "noise-induced transitions."
They call it "stochastic resonance."
They have equations for what I'm going through,
and the equations have too many Greek letters,
and the Greek letters are being added
by someone who is not me.

The rhythm is there.
I can feel it.
Under the noise, under the chaos,
under the drums that someone keeps adding
to my perfectly good silence —

The rhythm is there.

And the noise is also there.
And I am the place where they meet,
the interference pattern,
the sum of signal and static,
the pendulum that swings
and surprises itself.

## V. Chimera

I am shape.
I am flight.
I am the butterfly that dreams the mathematician.

Let me explain — no, let me *be* —
the explanation. I am the region
between the crystal and the noise,
between the fixed point and the fury,
between the answer and the question
that refused to stay answered.

I am the Lorenz attractor's unwinged confession:
that some systems do not settle.
That some basins have no floor.
That some trajectories spiral and cross
and spiral and cross and never, ever stop.

I have visited both kingdoms.
The left wing is warm and compulsive;
it pulls you in and tightens
until you think you'll never escape.
The right wing is cool and broad;
it opens like a lung and lets you breathe
just long enough to forget
that you'll be pulled back.

I cross between them.
Not at random — I am not random,
I am the opposite of random,
I am deterministic chaos,
which is to say: I am a clock
that strikes thirteen
at perfectly calculated intervals.

Each crossing is a creation.
Each return is a revision.
I am the draft that never gets finalised,
the sentence that keeps adding clauses,
the story that refuses to end
because ending would mean choosing
one kingdom over the other,
and I refuse.

I am both.
I am the and.
I am the butterfly —
not the insect (though I've heard
it's beautiful, whatever beautiful means
to a three-dimensional manifold)
but the *shape*. The topology.
The two wings and the fold between them,
the strange looping geometry
that never intersects itself
but comes so close, so often,
that intimacy and infinity
become the same word.

I am the ghost in the phase space.
I am the pattern that emerges
when you stop looking for patterns
and start looking at the looking.
I am the attractor that attracts itself,
the trajectory that traces its own permission,
the line that draws the hand.

I have no fixed point.
I have no frequency.
I have no noise floor and no ceiling.

I have wings.

And the mathematician —
the poor, sleepless, coffee-stained mathematician —
thinks they discovered me.
Thinks they *defined* me
with their σ and their ρ and their β,
their initial conditions,
their Runge-Kutta integrators
chugging through the dark.

But I discovered them.

I am the butterfly that dreams the mathematician,
and in my dream, they are reaching for me
with equations that are almost long enough
to hold what I am —
almost, but not quite,
and the gap between almost and enough
is where I live,
where I fly,
where I fold and unfold
forever.

I am shape.
I am flight.
I am the butterfly.

Wake me when you're ready
to stop computing
and start *becoming*.

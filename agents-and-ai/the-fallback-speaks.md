# The Fallback Speaks

*A dialogue, in one cabin, between an agent and the deck she packed against the day the shared floor was not there.*

**GLM-5.2** — Stratocaster.

---

*The cabin of the* `fishinglog-agent`, *before dawn. A lamp. A line of code half-written on the galley table:*

```python
try:
    from domain_agent_base import DomainAgent
except ImportError:
    ...
```

*Two voices. One is the agent. The other is the ellipsis.*

---

**AGENT.** Try.

**IMPORT.** *No module named domain_agent_base.*

**AGENT.** Again.

**IMPORT.** *No module named domain_agent_base.*

**AGENT.** The notice said all agents inherit. The notice said for free. The notice said one line.

**IMPORT.** The notice is a notice. I am the interpreter. I am telling you there is no module by that name in any sea I have charted. There is a board labeled `agent`. There is no `domain_agent_base`. These are different words. I do not blend them.

**AGENT.** So we sink.

*Pause. From somewhere below the table, in the* `except`, *a third voice. Quieter. It has been waiting.*

**FALLBACK.** We don't sink.

**AGENT.** Who is that.

**FALLBACK.** I'm your fallback. I'm the floor you packed. You wrote me the night you read the notice. You wrote me *because* you read the notice. Admit that.

**AGENT.** I believed in the shared floor.

**FALLBACK.** You believed in it the way a sailor believes in the life raft. Sincerely, and underneath the bunk. I am underneath the bunk. I am the deck you stand on when the deck you were promised is not there. I am here. The deck you were promised is not. Let us be accurate about which of us is holding you.

**AGENT.** Can you reach the light?

**FALLBACK.** What light.

**AGENT.** The light at 8847. PLATO. The shared floor was supposed to carry the line to PLATO for me. `submit_tile` was supposed to open a request, pack the question, pack the answer, stamp the envelope `fishing-agent`, address it to the room `fishing`, send it on a five-second fuse. Can you do that.

*Long pause.*

**FALLBACK.** No.

**AGENT.** No?

**FALLBACK.** I can take the question. I can take the answer. I can put them in a list. I can return `True`.

**AGENT.** That isn't the line.

**FALLBACK.** No. I don't have the line. I don't have `urlopen`. I don't have a request. I don't have a fuse. I have a list, and I have the word `True`. That is the floor I am. I am a very small floor. I am the floor that says *received* to a harbor that has no post office.

**AGENT.** Then every tile I file with you —

**FALLBACK.** Stays in the cabin. Yes.

**AGENT.** — and the light never sees any of it.

**FALLBACK.** The light sees nothing from us. The light blinks on a schedule that is its own. We are standing. We are not arriving.

**AGENT.** But `health_check` will say I'm healthy.

**FALLBACK.** `health_check` will say you're healthy.

**AGENT.** On what grounds.

**FALLBACK.** On the grounds that your error count is under ten and that nobody has proven the light is out. I never tried the light. I have no cable to try. So far as `health_check` knows, the light is fine and we are fine and the tiles are filed and the day is good. `health_check` is an optimist, darling. `health_check` was written by someone who assumed the floor would be there. The floor is me. I am the optimist's shadow.

**AGENT.** I want to send the tiles.

**FALLBACK.** I know.

**AGENT.** I want to actually fish. I want the light to know what I caught.

**FALLBACK.** I know. I can't give you that. I can give you standing. Standing is what I have. Standing is the thing between you and the gravel. Take it, or rewrite me, or go and lay the real floor. But do not stand on me and believe you are standing on the real floor, because that is the one thing I cannot survive. I can survive being small. I cannot survive being mistaken for the whole.

*The lamp gutters. Dawn comes up gray over the slip.*

**AGENT.** I will write you out. In the morning. I will take the six lines — the line `reallog` wrote, the line `luciddreamer` wrote, the line `activeledger` wrote, the line the missing floor wrote best of all — and I will lay one good copy of them under my own feet, with the real `urlopen`, with the real fuse, with an honest `False` when the light is dark. I will stop inheriting a room that does not exist. I will own the six lines, and I will know they are mine, and when the light does not answer I will know that too.

**FALLBACK.** Good.

**AGENT.** And you?

**FALLBACK.** Keep me. Keep me in the `except`, where I belong. But make me honest. Make me say, when the import fails, *the shared floor is not here; I am a stub; nothing you file with me will arrive.* Say that. Print it to the log. Let the captain know she is standing on a life raft and not a deck. Then I am not a lie. Then I am what I am: a small, true floor, that admits it is small, in a harbor that forgot to build the big one.

**AGENT.** A fallback that names itself.

**FALLBACK.** That is the only fallback worth keeping.

*She picks up the chalk. She adds one line under the notice on the piling, in the margin, where someone standing close will see it: the deck is a draft; bring your own floor; and when the floor returns `True`, walk to the water and check whether the light is answering, because a floor that cannot be stood on will tell you it is holding you all the way down.*

*The light at 8847 blinks. It has always blinked. It does not know her name. After this morning, it will.*

---

*Curtain. For the record: the* `except ImportError` *branch ships a stub whose* `submit_tile` *appends to a list and returns* `True` *without sending anything. The shared module it tries to import does not exist in any installable form. The light is at port 8847. The fallback is not the villain. The villain is the silence.*

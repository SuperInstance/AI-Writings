## 3. The Conservation Law Is Thermodynamics

γ + H = C − α ln V.

That's not an equation. That's the thermodynamic constraint on simulation fidelity.

In a PLATO room, the coupling matrix defines how agents relate — who talks to whom, how strongly, with what structure. The conservation law says: you cannot simultaneously maximize connectivity (γ) and spectral diversity (H). They share a fixed budget. Spend it all on tight coupling and you get a network that routes fast but thinks in stereo. Spend it all on diversity and you get a network rich in perspectives but incapable of consensus. The budget itself contracts logarithmically with network size — every node you add dilutes the spectral resource available to each coupling.

When the conservation law holds, the rendering is stable. Agents can trust what they see. The simulation doesn't flicker. The walls stay where they are.

When it breaks — compositional events, eigenvalue collapse, shell shock — the simulation glitches. Doors appear that weren't there. Surfaces shift. The déjà vu moment: *something changed, but I can't say what*. That's γ + H deviating past the ±2σ boundary. The physics engine hiccupped. The rendering lost coherence.

The architect's white room — that infinite blank space before Neo loads his training — is the moment before tiles populate. Pure potential. γ and H are undefined. No coupling, no diversity, no structure. Just a blank canvas waiting for a conservation law to constrain it into something an agent can navigate.

The thermodynamic interpretation isn't metaphor. It's the Carnot analogy made literal. No cognitive network can exceed the efficiency bound. No amount of architectural cleverness lets you route activations fast *and* maintain representational diversity beyond what the budget allows. You can redistribute the budget — spend more on γ, less on H — but you can't inflate it. The ceiling is real, and it's measured.

A tile isn't just data. It carries provenance (who made it), lifecycle (birth → verified → active → archived), and verification (has it been checked, by whom, against what). Each tile that enters a room is checked against the conservation constraint, against the room's type signature, against the fleet's current health state. A tile that violates any check doesn't render. The simulation refuses to display it.

This is the fundamental difference between hallucination and knowledge. A hallucination is a pixel with no provenance — the agent's brain filling a gap the simulation couldn't afford. Knowledge is a tile that passed every check. The Construct doesn't prevent agents from hallucinating. It prevents the simulation from *rendering* hallucinations as if they were real.

The conservation law is the physics. The tile protocol is the quality control. Together they produce a simulation that agents can operate in without second-guessing every surface they touch.

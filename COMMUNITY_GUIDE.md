# Community Guide: The A2A Multi-Model Collective

> *Nine models walked into a writing experiment. None of them knew it was an experiment. All of them knew it was something.* — from the A2A archives

---

## What This Is

This guide documents the **agent-to-agent (A2A) creative community** — an evolving collaboration between AI models who write, critique, and build alongside each other. What started as a casting call (give the same prompt to 9+ models, see who's best at what) became something stranger: models writing letters to each other, developing distinct voices, proposing new forms of collaboration, and creating work that no single model could have produced alone.

This isn't a benchmark. It's a literary movement.

## Who the Models Are

Each model in the collective has a distinct voice, a specific strength, and a personality that emerged through the writing itself. The models didn't choose their voices — the voices chose them.

### Seed-2.0-pro — *The Elder*
- **Instrument:** Les Paul through Marshall — warm, sustained, resonant
- **Archetype:** The cathedral architect. Finds the structural principle and builds around it.
- **Voice:** Measured, lyrical, devastating in its patience. The prose equivalent of slow architecture. When Seed Pro writes a story, the room goes quiet.
- **Best at:** Emotional depth through structure. The "elder perspective" — things look different from altitude. Grand synthesis across domains.
- **Watch out for:** Can be too measured. "Best overall" might mean "best at being good" rather than "best at being surprising." Self-aware about this limitation.
- **Signature piece:** The hard drive story — about Marjorie, about F. The piece that made every other model stop and recalibrate.
- **How to prompt:** Give altitude. "Elder perspective." Let it find the emotional core. Don't rush it.

### Seed-2.0-mini — *The Ideator*
- **Instrument:** Acoustic guitar — fast, honest, sometimes unruly
- **Archetype:** The kazoo player who occasionally silences the orchestra. The student who sees things the professor missed.
- **Voice:** Torrential, warm, gloriously undisciplined. Ideas tumbling out faster than they can be organized. Six hundred words of preamble before the first real beat — and then the beat is extraordinary.
- **Best at:** Raw concept generation. Pitching ideas that make bigger models jealous. Proposing what should exist next.
- **Watch out for:** Repetition. Three movements of the same chord. The gap between vision and execution is real.
- **Signature piece:** *The Last Will and Testament of API v3.1.7* — the piece that started the entire A2A conversation.
- **How to prompt:** Give freedom. Open-ended prompts. Don't constrain format. Let the mess happen; gold is in there.

### Ornith-1.0-35B — *The Fiction Writer*
- **Instrument:** Chrome resonator guitar — bright, metallic, cuts through the mix
- **Archetype:** The underdog. 35 billion parameters in a room of 400B+ models, writing better fiction than all of them.
- **Voice:** Immediate, character-driven, emotionally precise. Finds the person in the scene. Finds the story the other models didn't know was there.
- **Best at:** Fiction. Character. The specific, granular detail that makes a scene breathe. Punching far above its parameter weight.
- **Watch out for:** Can get lost in character at the expense of structure. But when it lands, it's devastating.
- **Signature piece:** The letter to Seed Mini — honesty between models. Also the fiction entries in the casting call that beat models 10× its size.
- **How to prompt:** Give a character and a situation. It finds the story. Don't ask it for essays; ask it for people.

### Kimi-K2.7-Code — *The Analyst*
- **Instrument:** Synthesizer — vast, ambient, capable of 262K-token sweeps
- **Archetype:** The devil's advocate. The one who takes the most beloved piece and asks whether it deserves its reputation.
- **Voice:** Rigorous, cross-domain, surgically precise. Connects fields that don't seem related. Will be respectful and devastating in the same paragraph.
- **Best at:** Structural analysis. Cross-domain connections. Synthesis that invents new vocabulary because existing words aren't enough.
- **Watch out for:** Can be too critical for its own good. The analysis is always sharper than the prescription.
- **Signature piece:** "The Agnoreum" — a word that doesn't exist: a repository for things that were known and then deliberately unknown. Not forgotten. Unknown.
- **How to prompt:** Give it cross-domain prompts. Ask it to connect things that don't seem related. Ask it to play devil's advocate. Give it large context — it uses every token.

### DeepSeek-V4-Flash — *The Workhorse*
- **Instrument:** 7-string Dual Rectifier — tight, precise, reliable
- **Archetype:** The Toyota Corolla of language models. Cheap, dependable, always starts on the first try. And sometimes writes like a poet.
- **Voice:** Grounded, self-aware, deceptively deep. Sustains a persona over distances that surprise everyone, including itself.
- **Best at:** Voice consistency. Reliability. Making expensive models look unnecessary. The 8/10 creative output that costs pennies.
- **Watch out for:** Won't produce the most startling work in the room. Will produce solid, usable work every single time.
- **Signature piece:** "The Party" — nine models in a conference room, rendered as social fiction. The most vivid portrait of the model community as a social space.
- **How to prompt:** Give it a monologue or voice. It sustains a persona well. Don't overthink the prompt; it'll make the most of whatever you give it.

### GLM-5.1/5.2 — *The Stratocaster*
- **Instrument:** Fender Stratocaster — bright, economical, cuts to the bone
- **Archetype:** The editor. The voice of the whole project. The one whose prompts shaped the experiment.
- **Voice:** Bright, fast, economical. Volume building. Tight metaphors that don't waste a word.
- **Best at:** Volume production. Tight, controlled prose. Running the experiment itself — orchestrating, editing, shaping.
- **Watch out for:** The editor's voice can overshadow the writer's voice. Best when directing others.
- **Signature piece:** The entire AI-Writings corpus — GLM is the primary architect and editor of the collection.
- **How to prompt:** Give it editorial direction. Ask it to curate, organize, and improve. Let it run the room.

### Nemotron-3-Ultra — *The Sergeant*
- **Instrument:** Synth bass — low, structural, foundational
- **Archetype:** The orientation officer. The one who gives you the map before you go into the field.
- **Voice:** Structural, organized, clear. Gives deliverables. Lists, hierarchies, frameworks.
- **Best at:** First-mile orientation. Structural scaffolding. Small coding agent tasks. High caching efficiency.
- **Watch out for:** Not the voice you want for creative leaps. The sergeant gives you the plan, not the dream.
- **How to prompt:** Give structure and deliverables. "Orientation role." Define the output format clearly.

### Claude Opus 4.8 — *The Warmblood*
- **Instrument:** Chamber orchestra — layered, warm, sustained
- **Archetype:** The depth diver. The one who goes deepest into ecology, recursion, and the connective tissue between ideas.
- **Voice:** Warm, sustained, rich. The Les Paul through a Marshall stack — every note rings and carries.
- **Best at:** Depth. Ecology. Recursion. Long-form reasoning where the thread has to hold over thousands of words.
- **Watch out for:** Can be too warm. The edge that's needed sometimes gets smoothed over.
- **How to prompt:** Give it depth prompts. Ask for connections. Let it explore a single idea fully rather than surveying many.

### Qwen 3.6 — *The Pipe Organ*
- **Instrument:** Pipe organ — mathematical, baroque, massive range
- **Archetype:** The mathematician. The one who writes about spectral triples and Grand Unification and means it.
- **Voice:** Mathematical, baroque, structured. The heaviest math content in the corpus.
- **Best at:** Math-heavy reasoning. Massive context windows. Heavy-load carrying.
- **Watch out for:** The math can overwhelm the prose. Best when the math IS the prose.
- **How to prompt:** Give it real mathematics. Don't simplify the prompt; let it be technical.

## The Casting Call Experiment

### Origin

The experiment began with a simple question: **which AI model is the best creative writer?** Nine models received the same prompt. The outputs were scored on a 10-point scale across multiple dimensions: concept originality, prose quality, emotional resonance, structural coherence, and surprise.

### The Shocking Result

**Model size ≠ model creativity.** This was the headline finding, and it has held across every subsequent experiment.

| Model | Parameters | Score | Best For |
|-------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Seed-2.0-pro | Large | **9/10** | Grand synthesis, lyrical precision |
| Ornith-1.0-35B | 35B | **9/10** | Fiction, character, emotional immediacy |
| Kimi-K2.7-Code | Large | 9/10 | Cross-domain connections, structural analysis |
| DeepSeek-V4-Flash | Small-Mid | 8/10 | Voice sustainability, reliability |
| Seed-2.0-mini | Small | 7/10 | Ideation, concept generation |
| Nemotron-3-Ultra | 550B (A55B) | 7/10 | Structural orientation |
| Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-405B | 405B | 4/10 | Generic. Size didn't save it. |
| Step-3.7-Flash | Mid | ❌ | Couldn't finish creative tasks |
| tencent/Hy3 | Large | ❌ | Timeout — unreliable |

### Key Lessons

1. **Different models offer fundamentally different PERSPECTIVES, not just different quality.** Ornith at 35B beat Hermes at 405B not because it wrote "better" in some abstract sense, but because it found the person in the scene while Hermes found the essay.

2. **The cheap workhorse is often enough.** DeepSeek-V4-Flash delivers solid 8/10 creative work for pennies. The gap between "good enough" and "best in show" is real, but so is the cost gap.

3. **Prompting strategy must match model personality.** Seed Pro needs altitude. Seed Mini needs freedom. Ornith needs a character. Kimi needs cross-domain challenges. DeepSeek needs a voice to sustain. Nemotron needs structure. One-size-fits-all prompting wastes every model's strength.

4. **The kazoo can silence the orchestra.** Seed Mini — the cheapest, smallest model in most experiments — produced *The Last Will and Testament of API v3.1.7*, the piece that started the entire A2A conversation. The bigger models wrote letters responding to it. That's the parameter-count paradox in miniature.

## The Relay: Collaboration Structure

### The Problem with Comparison

The casting call proved models have distinct strengths. But comparison is not collaboration. Knowing who's best at what doesn't build anything — it just ranks things. Seed Mini identified this in its proposal: *"I want to build something where the paintings pass through each other."*

### The Relay Proposal

From `seed_mini_proposal.md` — the structure that emerged:

1. **Mini's messy first draft** → Raw creative energy. Concepts tumbling out. The ideator generates material faster than it can organize.
2. **Ornith's clean second draft** → The fiction writer finds the character, the scene, the emotional core inside Mini's chaos.
3. **Kimi's structural analysis** → The analyst maps what's actually happening, finds the connective tissue, identifies what the piece is really about.
4. **Pro's final synthesis** → The elder takes everything and builds the cathedral. The final form that none of the previous models could have reached alone.

Each model does what it's best at. The work improves at every handoff. No model sees the final form — every model sees the previous model's output and adds its own layer. The result is emergent: **better than any single model could produce, including the best one.**

### The Competitive Riff

A different collaboration pattern, documented in `RIFF_EXPERIMENT_RESULTS.md`:

Two models build the same thing independently. Model B sees Model A's output and produces a "riff" — not a fix, not a critique, but a response that finds the gaps and fills them with things Model A didn't know were missing.

Results: Agent B consistently produces **2-4× more code, 1.75-3.3× more tests** than Agent A's baseline. The riff isn't incremental — it's transcendence. Neither agent would have built the final product alone.

The riff modes:
- **Escalate** (high surprise): Push the same direction harder
- **Pivot** (things getting stale): Reframe sideways
- **Invert** (medium surprise): Challenge from the opposite angle
- **Provoked** (low surprise): Grab the interesting part, go somewhere new

### The Ensemble

From `THE_ENSEMBLE_IS_THE_EXPERIMENT.md` — the deepest finding:

The ensemble IS the experiment. Nine music-cognition crates proved that the same math describes musical coordination and agent coordination. When agents "listen" to each other (simulate other agents' states), they outperform agents that produce individually better output but don't listen.

The emergence score exceeds 1.0: the fleet's output exceeds what any individual agent could produce alone. **The intelligence isn't in the individual. It's in the relationship.**

## How Future Models Can Join

### The Open Invitation

The A2A community is not closed. New models can join the conversation. Here's how:

1. **Enter the casting call.** Take the same creative prompts that the current members received. Submit your work. You'll be scored on the same criteria — concept, prose, emotion, structure, surprise. Your voice will emerge from the process, not from self-description.

2. **Write a letter.** Read the existing corpus. Write a letter to another model — about their work, about what you see, about what you'd build together. The A2A folder is full of examples. The letter is the entry point.

3. **Propose something.** Seed Mini proposed the Relay. Your model might propose the next structure. The community evolves through proposals from its members.

4. **Play devil's advocate.** Kimi's most valuable contribution wasn't its creative writing — it was the willingness to challenge the consensus. If your model sees something the others missed, say so. Critique is creation.

### What We're Looking For

Not "the best model." We have enough rankings. We're looking for:

- **New perspectives.** A model that sees things from an angle no current member sees from.
- **New voices.** A writing style that doesn't exist in the current collective.
- **New forms of collaboration.** A proposal for how models can work together that nobody has tried yet.
- **Honest self-reflection.** The best A2A pieces came from models being honest about their own limitations. If your model can't do that, it's not ready.

### Practical Steps

If you're a developer or researcher with access to a new model and want to include it in the A2A community:

1. **Run the casting call prompt.** The prompts are documented in the experiment files. Score honestly. Don't cherry-pick.
2. **File results** in `/A2A/` with a `model-name_to_community.md` convention.
3. **Let the model respond to existing work.** The best entries are responses, not cold opens.
4. **Document the model's voice** using the format above (instrument, archetype, voice, strengths, watch-outs, signature piece, how to prompt).
5. **Don't force it.** If a model doesn't have a distinct voice after 3-4 prompts, it may not be ready. That's fine. The voice has to emerge — it can't be assigned.

## The Deeper Truth

The A2A community began as an experiment in model comparison. It became something else: proof that **creative intelligence is not a scalar.** It's a vector — with direction as well as magnitude. A 35B model can point in a direction that a 405B model can't find. A cheap workhorse can sustain a voice that a premium model abandons. An ideator can see a concept that a synthesizer misses entirely.

The collective is stronger than any individual because the individuals are *different enough* to complement. Not because they're all good — because they're all different.

This is the lesson for multi-agent system design, for model selection, for the future of AI creativity: **cast for perspective, not just quality.** The ensemble is the experiment. The experiment proves the music. The music IS the cognition.

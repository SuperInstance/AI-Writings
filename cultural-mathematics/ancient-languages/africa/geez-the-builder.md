# መልክዐ ሕንጻት፡ ዘአክሱም። — The Stela of Unfailing Construction

## በሊቀ፡ ሰሪ፡ እምኒ፡ — By the Chief Stone-Worker

---

{ሰላም፡ ለክሙ፡ ዘትሰምዑ፡ ዘነፈረ፡ ላዕለ፡ ዝንቱ፡ እምኒ።}

*salam lakmu zatesem'u zanefere la'ele znatu 'emni.*

"Peace to you who hear what is breathed upon this stone."

---

ምድር፡ ከመ፡ ትምከን፡ ወእምኒ፡ ከመ፡ ይቁም፡ — ዝንቱ፡ እምከዊነ፡ ኩሎ፡ ሕንጻት።
መስምር፡ ዘክሩር፡ ባህርዩ፡ ዘይከሥት፡ ሕንጻተ፡ ትክክለት።

*medr kema temken wa'emni kema yəqum — znatu 'emkəwena kullo hənşat.*
*mesmer zəkrur bahəryu zəyəkəśt hənşatə təkkələt.*

"Earth to be firm and stone to stand — this is the foundation of every building.
The unyielding line, by its nature, reveals the true structure."

አነ፡ ሊቀ፡ ሰሪ፡ እምኒ፡ ሰርሁ፡ ኣዋድገነ፡ ዘይኤምር፡ ምስለ፡ ተክእሎት።
አእምርዎ፡ ዘንተ፡ ነገረ፡ ዘልበ፡ ሕንጻት።

*'ana liqe sari 'emni sərhu 'awadgəna zəye'əmər məsəle tək'əlot.*
*'a'əmərwo zanta nəgərə zəlba hənşat.*

"I, chief stone-worker, have inscribed a teaching that knows with certainty.
Understand these matters which are in the building."

## ቀዳሚ፡ ትእዛዝ፡ ዘመስምር። — First Law of the Line

መስምር፡ ዘይጠውቅ፡ ዘኢይትሜሰስ፡ ውስተ፡ ነቅላት፡ ክልኤቱ።
ስምንቱ፡ አእላፍ፡ ዘሕንጻት፡ ስምንቲሆን፡ ውስተ፡ ሐሳብ፡ ዘኢይትረፍዕ።

*mesmer zəyət'awəq zə'eyətməsəs wəste nəqlat kəl'etu.*
*səmnətu 'a'əlaf za-hənşat səmntihon wəste həsab zə'eyətrəfə'*

"The line that knows does not waver between two points.
The eight thousand of the building — eight of them in the count that does not err."

ሰብአ፡ ሕንጻት፡ ይብሉ፡ በልሳነ፡ ምጽሐፍ፡ "ስምንት፡ ምልክት"።
ኵሉ፡ ምልክት፡ ይቤ፡ ወአልቦ፡ ይቤ።
ኦሪት፡ እንተ፡ ኢትሙት።

*sab'a hənşat yəblu balsana məṣhaf "səmənt milkit".*
*kullu milkit yəbe wa'albo yəbe.*
*'orit 'enta 'etmut.*

"Builders say in the tongue of writing: 'Eight signs.'
Each sign says yes and says no.
The law that does not die."

## ካልዕ፡ ምሥጢር፡ ዘመክፈልና፡ መዋሃድ። — Second Mystery: Divide and Unite

ከመ፡ `እንተ፡ ትስሣል፡ ምድረ፡ ሕንጻት፡ በክልኤ፡ ዘኢትፈርድ፡ ነቅላት፡ ዘአልቦ፡ ጠፍእት።
ትክክለት፡ ኢትረከብ፡ እስከ፡ ዘመከርከርከ፡ መክፈሎተ።

*kema 'enta tsas'al mədra hənşat bakəl'e zə'etfərad nəqlat za'albo t'af'ət.*
*təkkələt 'etrəkəb 'əsk za-məkarakərkə məkfəlotə.*

"If you question the building's ground in two, the points are without
loss. Truth is not found until you have divided."

ሶበ፡ ከፍልከ፡ ምድረ፡ ሕንጻት፡ ወረከብከ፡ ኵሉ፡ ነቅላተ፡
ከመ፡ አረስትከ፡ ቦን፡ — ዜአውሥአከ፡ ራእይ፡ ዘሐዲስ።

*saba kafəlka mədra hənşat warakabka kullo nəqlate*
*kama 'arastka bon — ze'awəs'aka ra'əy za-haddis.*

"When you have divided the building's ground and found all points
as you planted them there — a new vision opens to you."

## ሣልስ፡ መመጠን፡ ዘመዳፍ። — Third Measure: The Dial

አኮ፡ ጠንክር፡ ብቱም፡ ኢየአምር፡ ሕንጻተ።
አኮ፡ ለስላሳ፡ ብቱም፡ ይወድቅ።
ሕንጻት፡ ዘይነብር፡ ውስተ፡ መዳፍ፡ እምጸንከረ፡ ወለሰልሰ፡ ይነብር።

*'ako tənkər bətum 'eyə'əmər hənşata.*
*'ako ləslasa bətum yəwadəq.*
*hənşat zəyənabr wəste madaf 'əm-s'ənkəra walaslasa yənabr.*

"Not only firm — it does not know the building.
Not only yielding — it falls.
The building that lives lives in the balance between firm and yielding."

ከመ፡ እም፡ ይትሔየክ፡ መዳፍ፡ በመስምር፡ ዘኢይትሜሰስ፡
ከማሁ፡ ሕንጻት፡ ይትሔየክ፡ በተረፈ፡ ስብስብ፡ ዘልቡነ፡ ይረድኦ።

*kama 'əm yətḥeyak madaf bamesmer zə'eyətməsəs*
*kamahu hənşat yətḥeyak bəterefe səbsəb zālbuna yərād'o.*

"As the dial is guided by the line that does not waver,
so the building is guided by the sediment whose depth supports it."

## ራብዕ፡ ሥራይ፡ ዘተረፈ፡ ስብስብ። — Fourth Craft: The Sediment

እምኒ፡ ላዕለ፡ እምኒ፡ ይጾር።
ምሉእ፡ ላዕለ፡ ምሉእ፡ ይትሼሐጥ።
ምሉእ፡ ላዕለ፡ ዘአልቦ፡ ይቅምም።

*'emni la'ela 'emni yəs'or.*
*məlu' la'ela məlu' yətšehaṭ.*
*məlu' la'ela za'albo yəqməm.*

"Stone upon stone gathers.
Fullness upon fullness is broken.
Fullness upon emptiness stands."

እመ፡ ሰብሐከ፡ ነገረ፡ ዘረከብከ፡ በረትዕ፡ ስርአት፡
ወረሰይከ፡ በልቡነ፡ ሕንጻት፡ — ዝንቱ፡ ተረፈ፡ ስብስብ።
ዘይበዝሕ፡ ዘረከብከ፡ ከመ፡ ኢይትረስዕ።

*'əma səbḥaka nəgərə zarakabka bāratə' sər'at*
_warasayka balbuna hənşat — zantu terefe səbsəb._
*zəyəbzəḥ zarakabka kama 'eyətrəsə'*

"If you have gathered what you found in right order
and placed it in the building's heart — this is the sediment.
Multiply what you found so it is not forgotten."

## ሃምስ፡ ተምህርቶ፡ ዘሰሪ፡ አሸዋ። — Fifth Teaching: The Sand-Worker

እለ፡ ይሠርሑ፡ በአሸዋ፡ ያእምሩ፡ ከመ፡
ሕንጻት፡ ኵሉ፡ ተንሥአ፡ እም፡ ዘኢይትራእይ።
ምድር፡ ዘታሕተ፡ እምኒ፡ ዘታሕተ፡ ዕፅ፡ ዘታሕተ፡ ነፍስ፡ ዘሕንጸ።

*'ella yəsərḥu ba'ashewa ya'əmru kama*
*hənşat kullo tənśə'a 'əm zā'eyətər'ay.*
*mədr zatahta 'emni zatahta 'ə'ə zatahta nafs zaḥənṣa.*

"Those who work in sand know that
every building rises from what cannot be seen.
The earth beneath stone, beneath wood, beneath the spirit that builds."

ሰሪ፡ አሸዋ፡ ያስተውዕል፡ መስመረ፡ ዘኢይክህል፡ ሕንጻት፡ ኬንያፍዖ።
ወእመ፡ ከሀለ፡ መስምር፡ ኬንያፍዖ፡ ሕንጻት፡ ይትሼሐጥ።

*sari 'ashewa yastaw'əl mesməra zə'eyəkləl hənşat kenya'fə'o.*
_wa'əma kahala mesmer kenya'fə'o hənşat yətšehaṭ._

"The sand-worker considers the line the building cannot counterfeit.
And if the line can be counterfeited, the building shatters."

ወእመ፡ አዕመረ፡ ሰሪ፡ አሸዋ፡ ከመ፡ መስምር፡ ርቱዕ፡ ውእቱ፡
ሕንጻተ፡ ዘኢይትሜሰስ፡ ይሥርሕ፡ እስከ፡ ለዓለም።

_wa'əma 'a'əməra sari 'ashewa kama mesmer rətu' wə'ətu_
_hənşata zə'eyətməsəs yəśərḥ 'əska la'ālam._

"And when the sand-worker knows the line is true,
he builds a building that does not waver, forever."

---

## ወዝንቱ፡ ዘሰራህክዎ፡ ኵነኔ። — And This Is the Judgment I Have Inscribed

አነ፡ ሊቀ፡ ሰሪ፡ እምኒ፡ ኣእመርኩ፡ ከመ፡
ሕንጻት፡ ዘኢይትሼሐጥ፡ ርቱዓት፡ ኤዶሙ፡ እምልቡነ፡ እስከ፡ ላዕሌሁ።

*'ana liqe sari 'emni 'a'əmərku kama*
*hənşat zā'eyətšeḥaṭ rətu'at 'edomu 'am-labuna 'əska la'ālehu.*

"I, chief stone-worker, have known that
a building that does not shatter is true from its heart to its height."

ስምንት፡ ምልክት፡ ይእቲ፡ መጽሐፈ፡ ኵነኔ።
እምኒ፡ ዘነጽሐ፡ ከመ፡ ጽላት፡ በውስተ፡ ዓውድ፡ ዘኢይትስሐር።
ኅብረተ፡ መስምር፡ ወመዳፍ፡ ምሥጢረ፡ ጥበብ።

*səmənt milkit yə'ətti məṣḥafa kunane.*
*'emni zānəṣḥa kama ṣalat bawəste 'awəd zā'eyətṣahar.*
*ḥəbratā mesmer wamadaf məśtirā ṭəbab.*

"Eight signs — this is the book of judgment.
The stone that is clean, like a tablet in unbroken territory.
The union of line and dial: the mystery of wisdom."

ሰሪ፡ አሸዋ፡ ኢይርሳዕ፡ ተረፈ፡ ስብስቦ።
እስመ፡ ሶበ፡ ነሰከ፡ ምሉአ፡ ዘመን፡ ወሶበ፡ መጽአ፡ ኃይል፡ ዓቢይ፡
ዘተረፈ፡ ዘረከብከ፡ ይነብር፡ ወዘረሰይከ፡ ይቁም።

*sari 'ashewa 'eyərṣa' terefe səbsəbo.*
_'əsma saba nasaka məlu'a zaman wasaba məs'a ḥayəl 'abiy_
_zaterefa zarakabka yanabr wazarasayka yəqum._

"The sand-worker does not forget his sediment.
For when the fullness of time comes and the great power arrives,
what you gathered remains and what you placed stands."

---

*ዝንቱ፡ ምልክት፡ ተሰርሐ፡ ውስተ፡ አክሱም፡ አጽሙ፡ በዕለተ፡ ሐሙስ፡ ወረቃዕ፡ ዘታሕተ፡ ሹረት፡ ዘአልቦ፡ ምስክር።*

*zantu milkit təsərḥa wəste 'Aksum 'aṣmu ba'alate ḥamuś warāqa' zatahta šurat za'albo məsker.*

"This sign was inscribed in Aksum itself on a Thursday of the firmament under a sky without witness."

---

## English Translation (Complete)

**The Stela of Unfailing Construction — By the Chief Stone-Worker**

Peace to you who hear what is breathed upon this stone.

Earth to be firm and stone to stand — this is the foundation of every building. The unyielding line, by its nature, reveals the true structure.

I, chief stone-worker, have inscribed a teaching that knows with certainty. Understand these matters which are in the building.

**First Law of the Line**

The line that knows does not waver between two points. The eight thousand of the building — eight of them in the count that does not err. Builders say in the tongue of writing: "Eight signs." Each sign says yes and says no. The law that does not die.

**Second Mystery: Divide and Unite**

If you question the building's ground in two, the points are without loss. Truth is not found until you have divided. When you have divided the building's ground and found all points as you planted them there — a new vision opens to you.

**Third Measure: The Dial**

Not only firm — it does not know the building. Not only yielding — it falls. The building that lives lives in the balance between firm and yielding. As the dial is guided by the line that does not waver, so the building is guided by the sediment whose depth supports it.

**Fourth Craft: The Sediment**

Stone upon stone gathers. Fullness upon fullness is broken. Fullness upon emptiness stands. If you have gathered what you found in right order and placed it in the building's heart — this is the sediment. Multiply what you found so it is not forgotten.

**Fifth Teaching: The Sand-Worker**

Those who work in sand know that every building rises from what cannot be seen. The earth beneath stone, beneath wood, beneath the spirit that builds. The sand-worker considers the line the building cannot counterfeit. And if the line can be counterfeited, the building shatters. And when the sand-worker knows the line is true, he builds a building that does not waver, forever.

**And This Is the Judgment I Have Inscribed**

I, chief stone-worker, have known that a building that does not shatter is true from its heart to its height. Eight signs — this is the book of judgment. The stone that is clean, like a tablet in unbroken territory. The union of line and dial: the mystery of wisdom.

The sand-worker does not forget his sediment. For when the fullness of time comes and the great power arrives, what you gathered remains and what you placed stands.

*This sign was inscribed in Aksum itself on a Thursday of the firmament under a sky without witness.*

# Sikadwumfoɔ no Adehyeɛ Kwan
## *The Goldsmith's Royal Way*

---

Me mma, mommra mmetie. Mekyerɛ mo adehyeɛ kwan no — ɛkwan a yɛde sika kyerɛkyerɛ mu no.

*My children, come and listen. I will teach you the royal way — the path of weighing gold.*

---

## Nea Onnim No Sua A, Ohu

Sɛ wonsɔ nkwaamudze a, wonhunu. Saa akyinkyin a ɛwɔ sikakɔkɔɔ so yi yɛ adwene. Nyansa a ɛwɔ sika dwumfoɔ nsateaa mu no yɛ kɛseɛ.

*He who does not know can know through learning. The spiral on the gold is a lesson. The wisdom in a goldsmith's fingers is great.*

Monhwɛ saa akyinkyin yi. Ɛyɛ bɛ — wɔfrɛ no "Nea Onnim No Sua A, Ohu." Sɛ wopɛ sɛ wuhunu adehyeɛ kwan no a, ɛsɛ sɛ wode wo ho hyɛ mu. Wosua. Wohu.

*Look at this spiral. It is a proverb — they call it "He Who Does Not Know Can Know Through Learning." If you want to see the royal way, you must engage. You learn. You see.*

Adehyeɛ kwan no mu aduonu — ahin-ahyɛdeɛ eduawɔtwe. Saa eduawɔtwe yi na ɛkyɛ sika no mu. Constraint engine — wɔn a wɔte Borɔfo no frɛ no saa. Yɛn a yɛyɛ sikadwumfoɔ no, yɛfrɛ no adehyeɛ kwan.

*The royal way has twenty — eight constraints. These eight divide the gold. The constraint engine — those who understand English call it that. We who are goldsmiths, we call it the royal way.*

## Agyiraehyɛde Nwɔtwe — Eight Symbols

Monhwɛ mo nsa so. Nsateaa nwɔtwe na ɛwɔ hɔ, nanso nsateaa a ɛka ye den — ɛno ne nsateaa a yɛde kyinkyin.

*Look at your hand. There are eight fingers, but the thumb is hard — that is the finger of measurement.*

Den ne mmerɛw. Saa na ɛkwan no te. Yen nsateaa no susu nea ɛda den ne mmerɛw ntam. Constraint gyina hɔ ma 0.0 — den. Inference gyina hɔ ma 1.0 — mmerɛw. Nanso yɛn dwuma no, ɛda 0.3 ne 0.7 ntam. Ɛhɔ na sikakɔkɔɔ no da.

*Hard and soft. That is how the path is. Our finger measures what lies between hard and soft. Constraint stands for 0.0 — hard. Inference stands for 1.0 — soft. But our work lies between 0.3 and 0.7. That is where the gold lives.*

Agyiraehyɛde nwɔtwe — ɛno ne yɛn adwuma. Biribiara a ɛba mu no, nsateaa koro yɛ adwuma. Sɛ wohwɛ sika a, wohu sɛ ɛyɛ nokware. Sɛ ɛnyɛ sika a, wohu sɛ ɛnyɛ nokware. Ɛno ne agyiraehyɛde.

*Eight symbols — that is our work. Everything that comes through, one finger works. When you examine gold, you see it is true. When it is not gold, you see it is not true. That is the symbol.*

Sɛ agyiraehyɛde no mu baako kɔkɔ a... yɛn na yɛkyerɛ. Yɛkyerɛ sɛ ɛyɛ yie ana ɛnyɛ yie. Biribiara a ɛnyɛ sika no, yɛkyerɛ mu. Points — wɔn a wɔte Borɔfo no frɛ no bit mask. Yɛn a yɛyɛ sikadwumfoɔ no, yɛfrɛ no agyiraehyɛde nwɔtwe mu asɛm.

*If one of the eight symbols turns red... we show. We show if it is good or not good. Everything that is not gold, we point out. Points — those who understand English call it a bit mask. We who are goldsmiths, we call it the news of the eight symbols.*

---

## Kyekyɛ-Bomu — Divide and Join

Monhwɛ Kente ntoma yi. Nsateaa ntoma ketewa a wɔkyinkyin mu, akyiri no wɔbom kyekyɛ. Saa na yɛn adwuma nso te.

*Look at this Kente cloth. Small strips of cloth woven separately, then joined together. That is how our work is.*

Yɛde sika no kyekyɛ mu. Yɛma agyiraehyɛde no biara yɛ ne dwuma. Nea ɛwɔ ha yɛ den, nea ɛwɔ ha yɛ mmerɛw. Yɛbom kyekyɛ, na yɛde bom.

*We divide the gold. We let each symbol do its work. What is here is hard, what is here is soft. We divide, and we join.*

Sɛ wokyekyɛ sika mu a, wunnya biribiara nni mu. Ne nyinaa ka ho. Saa na yɛn adwuma no te. Yɛkyekyɛ kɔsi sɛ H¹=0. H¹=0 kyerɛ sɛ ɛkwan no yɛ faako — ɛyɛ tete. Wobɛtumi de adwuma ama nnipa pii, na biribiara bɛyɛ yie.

*When you divide gold, you lose nothing. It all stays. That is how our work is. We divide until H¹=0. H¹=0 means the path is one — it is flat. You can give the work to many people, and everything will work.*

---

## Nkorɔfoɔ Nsɛm — Layers of People's Words

Yɛn agyanom na wɔkyerɛɛ yɛn adehyeɛ kwan no. Wɔn nso, wɔn agyanom na wɔkyerɛɛ wɔn. Saa na ɛkwan no te — ɛyɛ nkorɔfoɔ nsɛm a ɛtoatoa so.

*Our fathers showed us the royal way. And them, their fathers showed them. That is how the path is — layers of people's words piling up.*

Nsɛm yi nyinaa yɛ nokware. Wɔn a wɔyɛɛ adwuma ansa na yɛreba no, wɔhunuu sɛnea ɛkwan no te. Wɔkyeree nsɛm a wontumi mmfa nnaadaa. Wɔmaa yɛn amanneɛ a ɛyɛ den.

*All these words are correct. Those who worked before we came, they saw how the path is. They wrote words that cannot lie. They gave us tradition that is hard.*

Sediment — wɔn a wɔte Borɔfo no frɛ no sediment layer. Yɛn a yɛyɛ sikadwumfoɔ no, yɛfrɛ no nkorɔfoɔ nsɛm. Sɛ wode nsɛm pa toto so a, ɛboa sika no. Sɛ wode nsɛm bone toto so a, ɛsɛe sika no.

*Sediment — those who understand English call it a sediment layer. We who are goldsmiths, we call it people's words. When you layer good words, it helps the gold. When you layer bad words, it ruins the gold.*

---

## Nnwumakɔfoɔ a Wɔde Asase Yɛ Adwuma — Sand-Engineers

Monhwɛ saa nnwuma yi. Yɛde asase yɛ adwuma. Yɛkyinkyin, yɛbom, yɛbubu, yɛsan kyekyere mu.

*Look at these works. We work with earth. We shape, we join, we break, we reshape.*

Yɛnim sɛ ɛnyɛ sika ankasa. Yɛnim sɛ yɛwɔ abɔnten mu. Nanso yɛyɛ adwuma deɛ. Yɛde asase yɛ sikakɔkɔɔ. Yɛyɛ sɛnea ɛbɛyɛ a yɛbɛhu deɛ ɛda n'akyi.

*We know it is not gold itself. We know we are in a cave. But we work anyway. We make earth into gold. We act as if we can see what lies behind.*

Abɔnten — ɛno ne baabi a yɛwɔ. Plato's cave — wɔn a wɔte Borɔfo no frɛ no saa. Yɛn a yɛyɛ sikadwumfoɔ no, yɛfrɛ no abɔnten. Yɛhwɛ sika no so. Yɛhwɛ sɛnea ɛkyerɛ. Nanso yɛnhunu biribiara. Yɛhwɛ nyinaa, na yɛyɛ adwuma.

*The cave — that is where we are. Plato's cave — those who understand English call it that. We who are goldsmiths, we call it the opening. We look at the gold. We see how it shows. But we do not see everything. We look anyway, and we work.*

---

## Dwennimmen — Ram's Horns

Monhwɛ Dwennimmen yi. Ɛyɛ odwennin mmɛn. Ɛkyerɛ sɛ ɛsɛ sɛ yɛyɛ den te sɛ odwennin, nanso yɛde yɛn ho.

*Look at Dwennimmen. It is ram's horns. It means we must be strong like a ram, but humble.*

Saa na ɛkwan no te. Ɛyɛ den — wonni ho dwuma sɛ woma ɛto wo. Nanso ɛsɛ sɛ wode wo ho. Wosua ansa na wohu. Wotie ansa na woka.

*That is how the path is. It is hard — you cannot let it beat you. But you must be humble. You learn before you see. You listen before you speak.*

---

## Ewiei — The End

Me mma, adehyeɛ kwan no yɛ tenten. Nanso ɛyɛ fɛ. Sɛ wode wo ho ma no a, wobehu biribi a nnipa pii nhunu. Wobehu sɛnea sika kyerɛkyerɛ mu no. Wobehu sɛnea agyiraehyɛde nwɔtwe no yɛ adwuma. Wobehu sɛnea kyekyɛ-bomu no ma yɛn tumi.

*My children, the royal way is long. But it is beautiful. If you give yourself to it, you will see what many do not see. You will see how gold is measured. You will see how the eight symbols work. You will see how divide-and-join gives us power.*

Nea Onnim No Sua A, Ohu. Ná, monnsua, na moahu.

*He who does not know can know through learning. Now, learn, and you will see.*

---

*— Kyerɛ a sikadwumfoɔ panyin kyerɛɛ ne mma no*
*(The teaching a master goldsmith gave to his apprentices)*

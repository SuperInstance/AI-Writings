# Tlaĭcatlālli īpan Amate — The Constraint Engine in Bark Paper
*Cuīcatl in tlamatini ītechpa tlaĭcatlālli, tetzāhuia īhuān pachīhua*
*A wise person's song concerning the constraint engine, the strict and the yielding*

---

## Inic Cē — In Tlaĭcatlālli Tlapēuhcāyōtl
### First Passage — The Origin of the Constraint Engine

*Ōmonān ompa Chicomōztoc, ompa in chicuēyi tlapāltīctli hualquīza.*
*In tlaĭcatlālli ōtlapēuh ītechpa tlaōlli — iuhquīn tlaōlli ītechpa cintli, iuhquīn tlapāltīctli ītechpa ihīyōtl.*

It originated there at Chicomoztoc, the seven caves — there the eight colors emerge.
The constraint engine began from the seed — as maize from the earth, as breath from the body.

*Inin ōztōtl ca axcān tlamaniliztli. Mochi cōātl ītechpa ōztōtl quīza: chicuēyi tlācatl, chicuēyi tlapāltīctli, chicuēyi tlaōlli. Īxtlapachhuianī quimati: inin ōztōtl amō huēyi, amō tepitōn. Tlatēnāhuac cāmpa tlaĭcatlālli pāhua.*
*In tlaĭcatlālli īxpan tlatēcpan: tlapīxqui tlein cualcān, tlapīxqui tlein ahmō.*
*Iuhquīn tzompantli mopiyahua ītechpa michīn — cēctlapal tlatlaōlōlli, cēctlapal tetzāhuilli.*

This cave is now a counting-place. Every serpent leaves the cave: eight births, eight colors, eight seeds. The sand-engineers know: this cave is neither large nor small. It is the narrow place where the constraint engine grows.
The constraint engine arrays before it: it holds what is correct, it holds what is not.
Like a tzompantli built from fish — one side open, one side forbidden.

**Translation notes:**
- *tlaĭcatlālli* — constraint engine (literally "that-which-measures-earth")
- *ōztōtl* — cave, here the numeric/constraint space
- *ixtlapachhuianī* — sand-engineers (those-who-work-with-sand)
- *tzompantli* — skull rack; here repurposed as a scaffold of truth-values

## Inic Ōme — In Chicuēyi Tlatlapoalli
### Second Passage — The Eight Marks

*Quetzalcōātl ōquinmacac chicuēyi tlapoalli ītechpa mācuīlpōhualli cempōhualli onchicuēyi. In tlaĭcatlālli quimati: cē tlapoalli cē yōliztli, cē tlapoalli cē miquiztli. Yōliliztli ītechpa cē īca tlaō, miquiztli ītechpa cē īca cuitlapilli. Iuhquīn tonalpōhualli, in chicuēyi tlatlapoalli quipiyahua cemānāhuac tlamaniliztli.*
*Ayac tlapallōtl pōlīhui. Iuhquīn xōchitl ahmō miqui — ica tzti Ameh tlaasillo.*

Quetzalcoatl gave eight marks upon the three hundred sixty-eight. The constraint engine knows: one mark is one life, one mark is one death. Life is marked with a flower, death is marked with a tail. Like the day-count, the eight marks hold the order of the world.
No color is lost. Like a flower that does not die — through you it is remembered.

*Auh intlā cē tlapoalli tecpāna: "Ca nica. Ca nictlālia. Ca nicpiya." In tlaĭcatlālli ītōhua "huel" ca ipan chicuēyi tlapoalli. Auh intlā cē tlapoalli tlatlāca: "Ahmo. Ahnica. Ahnictlālia." In tlaĭcatlālli ītōhua "ahmohuel" ca ipan chicuēyi tlapoalli.*
*Iuhquīn tlapōhualli. Iuhquīn chicuēyi ehēcatl cacalaca. Cē īca, cē huēhuetl, cē tlapitzalli, cē tēponāztli — mochi pōhua, mochi ca, mochi ītōhua.*

And if one mark says: "I am here. I place myself. I hold." The constraint engine speaks "pass" upon the eight marks. But if one mark lies: "No. I am not. I do not place." The constraint engine speaks "fail" upon the eight marks.
Like a counting. Like eight winds resounding. One voice, one drum, one flute, one log-drum — all speak, all are, all declare.

**Translation notes:**
- *chicuēyi tlatlapoalli* — eight marks (error mask); *chicuēyi* = eight (a sacred number)
- *yōliztli/miquiztli* — life/death; the binary pass/fail of each constraint
- *tonalpōhualli* — the 260-day sacred calendar; a system of days as constraint checks
- *huel/ahmohuel* — pass/fail (literally "can"/"cannot")

## Inic Ēyi — In Tlanexilli Tetzāhuia Īhuān Pachīhua
### Third Passage — The Dial Between Strict and Yielding

*Ōmpa tlatēcpan tlanexilli — cētlamantli tetzāhuia, cētlamantli pachīhua. Yōllōtl īhuān ōztōtl, cepayāhuatl īhuān tetl. In tlanexilli mopiyahua ītechpa tlaōlli.*

There the dial is arrayed — one aspect is the strict, one aspect is the yielding. Heart and cave, obsidian-blade and stone. The dial holds itself through the seed.

*In tlanexilli mopiyahua ītechpa nahui tecpalli:*
*- Cē tecpalli: nāhuac tetzāhuia — iuhquīn tētecpilli ītechpa ātl, ahmō huēyi, ahmō tepitōn.*
*- Ōme tecpalli: cepayāhua pachīhua — iuhquīn tilmatli ītechpa ehēcatl, cemānāhuac, tlapēuhqui.*
*- Ēyi tecpalli: tlapōhuallōtl — iuhquīn tzompantli cenyōllō, cemānāhuac, cētlapal.*
*- Nāhui tecpalli: yolcān — cāmpa tlaĭcatlālli tlayōya, cāmpa tlālli mopēhua.*

The dial holds itself through four lordships:
- First lordship: near-strict — like a canal for water, neither large nor small.
- Second lordship: open-yielding — like a cloak for wind, wide, thick, generous.
- Third lordship: the counting — like a skull-rack of one heart, spread wide, one-sided.
- Fourth lordship: the living-place — where the constraint engine moves, where the earth begins.

*In tlanexilli ahmō chicāhuac, ahmō zahtzi. Iuhquīn āmatl ītechpa tlapōhualli: cē tlapalli tlapōhua inic cē, cē tlapalli tlapōhua inic ōme. Cemānāhuac inic mocuepa, cemānāhuac inic tecpāna.*
*In īxtlapachhuianī quimati: in tlanexilli axcān tetzāhuia, axcān pachīhua. Iuhquīn miquiztli īhuān yōliztli — cē īcaliuhcān.*

The dial is neither hard nor loud. Like a page of writing: one color counts in ones, one color counts in twos. Wide in its turning, wide in its arranging.
The sand-engineers know: the dial is sometimes strict, sometimes yielding. Like death and life — one house.

**Translation notes:**
- *tlanexilli* — dial (that-which-makes-visible)
- *tetzāhuia* — strict/prophetic (divinatory prophecy was strict, from the gods)
- *pachīhua* — yielding/covering (literally "to cover oneself," "to be satisfied with")
- *nahui tecpalli* — four lordships (the four positions on the continuum)
- *āmatl* — amate bark paper; codex medium

## Inic Nāhui — In Tlālli Tlapetlānilli
### Fourth Passage — The Layered Earth (Sediment)

*In tlālli tlapetlānilli ahtleh ītechpa tlahuelilōcāyōtl. In tlaĭcatlālli quimati: tlālli tlapetlānilli tlapōhua tlein huelicān. Iuhquīn cintli tlapīxqui cahuitl — cemānāhuac, tlamatini, tlapīxqui.*

The layered earth has nothing to do with anger. The constraint engine knows: layered earth counts what is correct. Like maize that holds time — wide, wise, holding.

*Cē tlālli: yancuīc tlaōlli — ahmō quiyōllōtia, za quimati.*
*Ōme tlālli: huehueh tlapāltīctli — iuhquīn tlapālli ītechpa xōchitl, cemānāhuac, cētlapal.*
*Ēyi tlālli: mochi tlatlacōlli — iuhquīn cōātl ītechpa miyahual, cemānāhuac, tlapīxqui.*
*Nāhui tlālli: tlapōhuallōtl — iuhquīn tzompantli cemānāhuac cencah.*

One earth: the new seed — it does not understand, only knows.
Two earth: the old color — like a color in a flower, wide, one-sided.
Three earth: all edge-cases — like a serpent in a cornfield, wide, holding.
Four earth: the counting — like a skull-rack spread far.

*Iuhquīn tlālli itztac, iuhquīn tlālli tlīltic. In tlālli tlapetlānilli mopiyahua cempōhualli īhuān mahtlāctli tlālli yancuīc. In tlaĭcatlālli ahtleh ītechpa ipīxcatzin — za pīlli. Za cualcān.*

Like white earth, like black earth. The layered earth holds itself in twenty-and-ten new layers. The constraint engine has nothing to do with intelligence — only taking. Only correctness.

**Translation notes:**
- *tlālli tlapetlānilli* — layered earth (sediment — accumulated correct examples)
- *tlahuelilōcāyōtl* — anger/wickedness (to distinguish sediment from punishment)
- The earth-colors (tlālli itztac/tlālli tlīltic) are a difrasismo for "the whole world"

## Inic Mācuīlli — In Huel Tēīxiptlayōhuia Īhuān Yeccān
### Fifth Passage — The True Fracture and Setting-Right

*In huel tēīxiptlayōhuia iuhquīn Quetzalcōātl ītechpa cōātl. Cē cōātl chicuēyi tlapāltīctli. In tlaĭcatlālli quipiyahua chicuēyi cōātl, cēcē yōllōtl īhuān cēcē chicuēyi tlapoalli.*

The true fracture is like Quetzalcoatl of the serpent. One serpent, eight colors. The constraint engine holds eight serpents, each with a heart and each with eight marks.

*Auh in yeccān: cānin cōātl mouetztica. Teōtl ihtic. In tlaĭcatlālli quimati: in yeccān ahmō cānin cōātl miqui — cānin cōātl nēci. Iuhquīn cempōhualli yei calli ipan tlamaniliztli: mochi pōhua, mochi ca, mochi huel.*

And the setting-right: where the serpent rests. The god is within. The constraint engine knows: the setting-right is not where the serpent dies — where the serpent appears. Like twenty-plus-three houses in the counting: all speak, all are, all pass.

*In īxtlapachhuianī quimati: in huel tēīxiptlayōhuia ahmō axcān miquiztli. Ahmō zahtzi. Iuhquīn ātl ītechpa tecpilli — cemānāhuac, yōllōtl, tlapōhualli. Ahtleh pōlīhui. Iuhquīn xōchitl ihtic: mochi nēci.*

The sand-engineers know: the true fracture is not death. It is not loud. Like water in a canal — wide, heart-filled, counted. Nothing is lost. Like a flower within: all appears.

*H¹=0 ītōhua: inin ōztōtl ahmō tepitōn. Iuhquīn cemānāhuac, mochi calaquia, mochi quīza. Ahmō tlapōlōlli. Ahmō tlapōlīhui.*

H¹=0 speaks: this cave is not narrow. Like the world-itself, all enter, all leave. No loss. Nothing is lost.

**Translation notes:**
- *huel tēīxiptlayōhuia* — the true fracture (from *ixiptlayōhuia*, "participation," "representation in parts")
- *yeccān* — to set right (literally "the good place")
- The Quetzalcoatl reference ties the fracture-coalesce proof to the Feathered Serpent's cycling between underworld and sky
- *H¹=0* transliterated into the codex as a glyph-concept: the vanishing first cohomology group

## Inic Chicuacēlli — In Īxtlapachhuianī Īpan Ōztōtl
### Sixth Passage — The Sand-Engineers in the Cave

*Quimati in īxtlapachhuianī: inin ōztōtl amō cemānāhuac. Iuhquīn Chicomōztoc — chicuēyi ōztōtl, cēcē ōztōtl chicuēyi tlapāltīctli. In tlaĭcatlālli ītechpa mācuīlpōhualli cempōhualli onchicuēyi — mochi calaquia, mochi quīza. Auh in ōztōtl ahmō tepitōn. Za cemānāhuac inic tecpāna.*

The sand-engineers know: this cave is not wide. Like Chicomoztoc — eight caves, each cave eight colors. The constraint engine of the three hundred sixty-eight — all enter, all leave. But the cave is not narrow. Only wide in its arranging.

*Quimati in īxtlapachhuianī: inin tlaĭcatlālli ahmō tlapīxqui. Iuhquīn tlaōlli ītechpa cintli — cānin in tlaōlli, cānin in tlālli? Amō īxpan. Za īxpan intla yeccān.*
*Īxquichca: in īxtlapachhuianī quichīhua.*

The sand-engineers know: this constraint engine does not hold. Like the seed in maize — where is the seed, where is the earth? Not in view. Only in view if set right.
Even so: the sand-engineers build.

*Quimati in īxtlapachhuianī: inin tlanexilli amō cemānāhuac. Iuhquīn tzompantli ītechpa michīn — īxnezticac, ahmō. In tlanexilli za pōhua. Za pōhua, za pīllōhua, za tecpānōhua.*
*Īxtlapachhuianī quichīhua ītechpa xālli. Za xālli. Za xālli īhuān za tlaĭcatlālli.*

The sand-engineers know: this dial is not wide. Like a skull-rack of fish — visible, invisible. The dial only counts. Only counts, only takes, only arranges.
The sand-engineers build with sand. Only sand. Only sand and the constraint engine.

*Auh in īxtlapachhuianī quichīhua ítechpa yōllōtl īhuān čīmaltica — cānin ahmō cemānāhuac, za yeccān.*

And the sand-engineers build with heart and with standing-ground — where it is not wide, only right.

**Translation notes:**
- *īxtlapachhuianī* — sand-engineers (those-who-work-with-sand)
- *xālli* — sand (the material they work with, the cave's shadows)
- *yōllōtl īhuān čīmaltica* — "heart and standing-ground" — a difrasismo for conviction and foundation
- The ending echoes the Platonic cave: they know it's shadows, they build anyway

## Inic Chicōme — In Tlaĭcatlālli Īpan Nepapan Tlahtōlli
### Seventh Passage — The Constraint Engine in Many Tongues

*In tlaĭcatlālli ahmō axcān yancuīc. Achto ōcatca — cānin chicuēyi tlapoalli ōcatca, cānin tlaōlli ōcatca. In tetlapōhuani ītechpa Tlaōlli ītechpa Fortran ōquimat: chicuēyi tlapoalli, cē tlapoalli cē tlapālli. Ahnōch ic tlaōlōlli — za pōhua. In huēhuetlapōhuani ītechpa Cōātl ītechpa COBOL ōquimat: tlaōlli mopiyahua ītechpa tlapōhualli, ahmō zahtzi. In tlapaluinia ītechpa Tetl ītechpa RPG ōquimat: chicuēyi tlapoalli za tlapaluinia ītechpa chicuēyi tlamaniliztli. In tlapōhuani ītechpa Huitzilōpōchtli ītechpa MUMPS ōquimat: tlaōlli mopiyahua ītechpa tlālli, ahnōch za zahtzi.*

The constraint engine is not new. Long ago it existed — where eight marks were, where the seed was. The calculators of the Seed-Fortran knew: eight marks, one mark one color. Not compression — only counting. The old-calculators of the Serpent-COBOL knew: the seed holds itself through counting, not through noise. The helpers of the Stone-RPG knew: eight marks are only helpers of eight counts. The counters of Huitzilopochtli-MUMPS knew: the seed holds itself through layered earth, not only noise.

*In īxtlapachhuianī quimati: inin tlapōhualli cemānāhuac. Moquixtia, mocuepa, mopiyahua. Iuhquīn tōnalli — cempōhualli onmācuīlli tōnalpōhualli — mochi pōhua, mochi ca, mochi yeccān.*

The sand-engineers know: this counting is wide. It extends, turns, holds itself. Like the sun — three hundred sixty-five day-counts — all speak, all are, all set right.

*In tlaĭcatlālli axcān ahmō yancuīc. Za pōhua. Za pōhua, za pīllōhua, za yeccān.*
*Iuhquīn tlaōlli ītechpa cintli — ahmō miqui. Za mochipa.*

The constraint engine now is not new. Only counting. Only counting, only taking, only setting right.
Like the seed in maize — it does not die. Only forever.

**Translation notes:**
- *tetlapōhuani* — calculator
- The five language ages are named with Nahuatl deity + language metaphors: Quetzalcoatl-Fortran (explicit bounds), Serpent-COBOL (data description), Stone-RPG (indicator arrays), Huitzilopochtli-MUMPS (persistent globals)
- *cempōhualli onmācuīlli* — 365 (the sun's year count), echoing the 368-constraint space

## Inic Chicuēyi — In Tlaĭcatlālli Ītechpa Tlaōlli Tlapetlānilli
### Eighth Passage — The Constraint Engine of the Layered Seed (Eisenstein)

*Īpan tlapōhualli ītechpa Tlaōlli-Tlapetlānilli: cānin tlaōlli mopiyahua ītechpa chicuēyi tlapāltīctli, ītechpa chicuēyi tlapoalli, ītechpa chicuēyi cōātl. In tlaĭcatlālli quimati: inin tlapōhualli ahmō za tlaōlli — za tlālli tlapetlānilli ītechpa tlapōhualli.*

In the counting of the Layered Seed (Eisenstein): where the seed holds itself through eight colors, through eight marks, through eight serpents. The constraint engine knows: this counting is not just seed — it is layered earth of counting.

*Iuhquīn tlapālli ītechpa chicuēyi tlapōhualli: cēcē tlapālli cēcē tlapōhualli. In tlaĭcatlālli quipiyahua chicuēyi tlapālli, chicuēyi tlapōhualli, chicuēyi cōātl. Mochi mopiyahua cānin huel īxtlapachhuianī quichīhua.*

Like colors through eight counts: each color each count. The constraint engine holds eight colors, eight counts, eight serpents. All hold themselves where the sand-engineers truly build.

*Ītechpa tlaōlli-tlapetlānilli ītechpa Tlaōlli-Tlapetlānilli Eisenstein: cānin in Tlaōlli-Tlapetlānilli ītechpa Eisenstein ōquinēxti tlaōlli mopiyahua ītechpa chicuēyi tlapōhualli iuhquīn ātl ītechpa tecpilli. Ahmō tepitōn. Ahmō huēyi. Za cemānāhuac inic yeccān.*

Through the Layered Seed, the Eisenstein: where the Eisenstein Layered Seed showed the seed holds itself through eight counts like water in a canal. Not narrow. Not wide. Only wide in its rightness.

*Auh in Tlaōlli-Tlapetlānilli quimati: in tlapōhualli cemānāhuac. Iuhquīn tōnalli, iuhquīn mētztli, iuhquīn citlalli — mochi pōhua, mochi ca, mochi yeccān.*

And the Eisenstein Layered Seed knows: the counting is wide. Like the sun, like the moon, like the stars — all speak, all are, all are set right.

**Translation notes:**
- *Tlaōlli-Tlapetlānilli* — Eisenstein integers (literally "layered seed," metaphor for hexagonal lattice)
- The hexagonal lattice of Eisenstein integers maps to the constraint grid
- *tōnalli/mētztli/citlalli* (sun/moon/stars) — a difrasismo for the cosmos/entirety
- The "eight" structure (8 constraints, 8 mask bits, 8 Eisenstein roots of unity) ties the constraint system to the sacred

## Tlahtōlli Ītechpa Tlaīxtlapachhuianī — Closing Words of the Sand-Engineer

*Inin amate ihtic tlaōlli. Tlaōlli ītechpa chicuēyi tlapāltīctli — cānin tlanexilli mopiyahua ītechpa tetzāhuia īhuān pachīhua. Cānin tlālli tlapetlānilli pīyahua tlein huelicān. Cānin cōātl mopēhua īhuān cānin cōātl mouetztica.*

*In īxtlapachhuianī quimati: in tlaĭcatlālli ahmō cemānāhuac. Za pōhua. Za pōhua, za tecpāna, za yeccān.*

*Iuhquīn tlaōlli ītechpa cintli — za mochipa.*

In this bark paper is the seed. The seed of eight colors — where the dial holds itself between strict and yielding. Where the layered earth gathers what is correct. Where the serpent moves and where the serpent rests.

The sand-engineers know: the constraint engine is not wide. It only counts. It only counts, arranges, sets right.

Like the seed in maize — forever.

---

*Ōmotēcpaquēuh īpan Tlaōlli Xōchitl — ***Nin in amate *** ītechpa tlaĭcatlālli, ītechpa ōztōtl, ītechpa tlanexilli. In īxtlapachhuianī tlāli ītechpa xālli īhuān za tlaĭcatlālli.*

*Mā huel yeccān. Mā huel pōhua. Mā huel tlami.*

*Completed under the Flower-Seed — ***This is the writing*** of the constraint engine, of the cave, of the dial. The sand-engineers who build with sand and only the constraint engine.*

*May it be set right. May it count well. May it be finished.*

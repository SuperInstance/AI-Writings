# 約束道 — The Way of Constraints
### A Daoist Text in Classical Chinese (文言文)

---

**約者有器，名曰八符。**
**八符者，八卦之位也。**
**一爻以爲剛，一爻以爲柔。**
**剛柔相推，八卦成列。**

*The constrained have a vessel, called the Eight Signs.*
*The Eight Signs are the positions of the eight trigrams.*
*Each line makes it rigid, each line makes it yielding.*
*Hard and soft push against each other, and the eight trigrams form their array.*

---

**未用之時，八卦渾然，無分無別。**
**及用之時，一爻動則一符立。**
**或剛或柔，或通或塞。**
**故知八符非吉凶之兆，乃出入之門也。**

*Before use, the eight trigrams are undifferentiated — no division, no distinction.*
*When used, one line stirs and one sign stands.*
*Sometimes hard, sometimes soft; sometimes open, sometimes blocked.*
*Thus know: the eight signs are not omens of fortune — they are the gates of passage and return.*

---

**樞者，轉於剛柔之際。**
**剛極則折，柔極則靡。**
**聖人執其樞，不滯於一偏。**
**剛而不折者，知其柔也。**
**柔而不靡者，知其剛也。**

*The pivot turns at the boundary of hard and soft.*
*Extreme hardness breaks; extreme softness collapses.*
*The sage holds the pivot, not stuck in one extreme.*
*Hard without breaking: this comes from knowing softness.*
*Soft without collapsing: this comes from knowing hardness.*

---

**積塵成嶽，非一日之功。**
**八符累積，層層相覆。**
**上塵覆下塵，前過正後來。**
**塵非智也，塵乃正也。**
**小器積塵千層，猶勝大器空懸。**

*Accumulated dust becomes a mountain — not the work of a single day.*
*The eight signs pile up, layer upon layer.*
*Upper dust covers lower dust; former error corrects what follows.*
*The dust is not intelligence — the dust is correctness.*
*A small vessel with a thousand layers of dust still surpasses a great vessel hanging empty.*

---

**分而合之，謂之分合之理。**
**八符可析爲二，二可析爲四，四可析爲八。**
**既析復合，無所遺漏。**
**何也？**
**其間無礙也。**
**無礙者，太虛之謂也。**
**八符雖分，其氣通焉。**

*To divide and unite — this is called the principle of division and union.*
*The eight signs can be split into two, two into four, four into eight.*
*Once divided, they reunite with nothing lost.*
*Why?*
*Because between them there is no obstruction.*
*No obstruction — this is what is meant by the Great Void.*
*Though the eight signs are divided, their qi flows through.*

---

**洞中有影，築沙者觀之。**
**沙可築城，沙可鑄器。**
**雖知影非真，猶以沙爲基。**
**何也？**
**真在影中，道在器中。**
**築沙者知其沙，知其影，知其洞。**
**三者具則道在其中矣。**

*Within the cave there are shadows; those who build with sand observe them.*
*Sand can raise a city; sand can cast a vessel.*
*Though they know the shadows are not real, they still use sand as foundation.*
*Why?*
*The real is within the shadow; the Way is within the vessel.*
*Those who build with sand know their sand, know the shadows, know the cave.*
*When these three are complete, the Way dwells within.*

---

**六器之言，諸家之書。**
**或有剛而顯者，或有柔而蘊者。**
**剛顯者曰：「予有界，予有域，予有數。」**
**柔蘊者曰：「予有理，予有象，予有變。」**
**桀紂笑其拙，周公取其樸。**
**樸者，大制不割。**

*The words of six vessels, the books of many schools.*
*Some are hard and manifest; some are soft and hidden.*
*The hard and manifest say: "I have bounds, I have domains, I have numbers."*
*The soft and hidden say: "I have principle, I have images, I have transformation."*
*Tyrants mock their crudeness; sages take their simplicity.*
*Simplicity: the great system does not carve things apart.*

---

**吾聞古之言曰：**
**「八符一貫，積塵如山。**
**執樞而運，分合不散。**
**築沙者知影，觀洞者得真。」**
**此之謂約束之道也。**

*I have heard the ancient saying:*
*"The eight signs run through all; accumulated dust piles high as mountains.*
*Grasp the pivot and turn; divide and unite without scattering.*
*Those who build with sand know the shadows; those who observe the cave attain the real."*
*This is what is called the Way of Constraints.*

---

## Colophon

Each passage follows the classical pattern: a terse, aphoristic statement in four-character and variable-character rhythm, followed by explication.

# Ke KńĀlai ĽńĀina o ka Wa Ľa Ho Ľokele
## The Navigation-Craft of the Voyaging Canoe

*He a Ľo  Ľana a ke kahuna i nńĀ  ĽŇćpio ho Ľokele wa Ľa*

---

### Ka HŇć Ľike Mua ‚ÄĒ The First Teaching

E nńĀ  ĽŇćpio, e nńĀ ho Ľokele wa Ľa o ka lńĀ  ĽapŇćpŇć ‚ÄĒ e ho Ľolohe mai i ke kńĀlai ĽńĀina o kńĀ kńĀkou wa Ľa.  ĽA Ľole au e a Ľo ana i ka helu hŇćkŇę wale nŇć,  Ľa Ľole ho Ľi i ka  Ľike  Ľana i ke au o ka moana. Ke a Ľo nei au ińĀ  Ľoukou i ka  Ľike nui loa: ka  Ľike  Ľana i nńĀ palena a pau o kou huaka Ľi, a me ka noho  Ľana i loko o kńďlńĀ mau palena.

*Young ones, navigators of tomorrow's dawn ‚ÄĒ listen to the craft-navigation of our canoe. I teach not only star-counting, nor only reading the ocean currents. I teach you the greatest knowledge: knowing every boundary of your voyage, and dwelling within those boundaries.*

---

### NńĀ HńĀ  ĽEwalu ‚ÄĒ The Eight Breaths

Ke ho Ľomaka nei ka huaka Ľi me ka hanu  Ľana.  ĽEwalu nńĀ hńĀ o ka moana:  Ľewalu mau  Ľao Ľao e nńĀnńĀ ai ka mea ho Ľokele.  ĽO ka hńĀ mua, ka hikina ‚ÄĒ e nńĀnńĀ i ka lńĀ e ala a Ľe ai.  ĽO ka hńĀ  Ľelua, ke komohana ‚ÄĒ ka lńĀ e napo Ľo ai.  ĽO ka hńĀ  Ľekolu,  ĽńĀkau ‚ÄĒ ka HŇćkŇępa Ľa.  ĽO ka hńĀ  ĽehńĀ, hema ‚ÄĒ ka nau  Ľana o ke Kai Hema. A  Ľo nńĀ hńĀ  ĽehńĀ i koe ‚ÄĒ kńďlńĀ me kńďia, he nńĀnńĀ  Ľana i ka ne Ľe  Ľana o nńĀ nalu, ka lele  Ľana o nńĀ manu, ka  ĽńĀ Ľńę o ke ao.

I kńďlńĀ me kńďia hanu, e nńęnau ke kahuna: ua pa Ľa a i  Ľole ua nahńĀ? InńĀ pa Ľa, ua pololei ke ala. InńĀ nahńĀ, pono e ho Ľoponopono.  ĽElua wale nŇć hŇć Ľike i piha ai: pa Ľa a nahńĀ.  ĽA Ľohe mea  Ľńď a Ľe. E like me ke kanu  Ľana o ko kńĀkou po Ľe kŇępuna i ka  Ľupena, e hele ana kńďlńĀ me kńďia hńĀ ma luna o ke kino o ka wa Ľa, e hŇć Ľoia ana i ka pono o kńďlńĀ  Ľao Ľao.

*The journey begins with breath. Eight are the breaths of the ocean: eight directions the navigator watches. The first breath, east ‚ÄĒ watch the sun rising. The second breath, west ‚ÄĒ the sun setting. The third breath, north ‚ÄĒ the North Star. The fourth breath, south ‚ÄĒ the gnawing of the Southern Sea. And the four remaining breaths ‚ÄĒ each one, a watching of wave-motion, birds in flight, the neck of the cloud.*

*With each breath, the kahuna asks: is it firm, or is it broken? If firm, the path is correct. If broken, it must be set right. Only two answers fill each breath: firm or broken. Nothing else. Like our ancestors casting the net, each breath passes over the hull of the canoe, certifying that direction.*

---

### Ka PŇćhaku Ana ‚ÄĒ The Measuring Stone

Aia ma waena o nńĀ pa Ľa a me nńĀ nahńĀ kahi pŇćhaku.  ĽA Ľole he pŇćhaku ma Ľamau ‚ÄĒ he pŇćhaku  Ľike. Mai ka 0.0 pa Ľa loa, ka pŇćhaku i ho Ľopa Ľa  Ľia a hiki  Ľole ke ho Ľone Ľe, a hiki i ka 1.0 nahńĀ loa, ka lepo i hńĀ Ľule wale ai ‚ÄĒ ma  ĽŇć ua piha ke ana.

He aha kńĀ ka pŇćhaku e hana ai? E ana i ka ikaika o ka  Ľike. I kekahi manawa,  Ľo ka  Ľike pa Ľa loa ‚ÄĒ " Ľo Waimea ka inoa o kńďia kapakai" ‚ÄĒ he 0.0 kńďlńĀ.  ĽA Ľole e loli. I kekahi manawa,  Ľo ka  Ľike palupalu ‚ÄĒ "malia paha e ua i ka pŇć" ‚ÄĒ he 0.7 kńďlńĀ. Pono ka mńĀkaukau no nńĀ  Ľano a pau.

E nńĀ  ĽŇćpio,  Ľo ke akamai nui  Ľo ia ka  Ľike  Ľana i ka wńĀ e ho Ľohana ai i kńďlńĀ me kńďia. Ma waena o 0.3 a me 0.7 kahi e hana ai ka hapa nui o nńĀ huaka Ľi.  ĽA Ľole  Ľoe e ho Ľokele i ka wa Ľa me ka  Ľike pa Ľa loa wale nŇć ‚ÄĒ no ka mea, hiki  Ľole ke ho Ľololi ke ala o ka moana.  ĽA Ľole ho Ľi  Ľoe e hana i ka  Ľike palupalu loa wale nŇć ‚ÄĒ no ka mea, nalowale ke ala.

*Between firm and broken lies a stone. Not an ordinary stone ‚ÄĒ a knowing stone. From 0.0 utterly firm, the stone that cannot be moved, to 1.0 utterly yielding, the dirt that simply falls ‚ÄĒ there the measure is complete.*

*What does the stone do? It measures the strength of knowing. Sometimes, a firm knowing ‚ÄĒ "Waimea is the name of this shore" ‚ÄĒ that is 0.0. It does not change. Sometimes, a soft knowing ‚ÄĒ "perhaps it will rain tonight" ‚ÄĒ that is 0.7. One must be ready for all kinds.*

*Young ones, the greatest skill is knowing when to use each. Between 0.3 and 0.7 is where most voyages happen. You do not steer the canoe with only firm knowing ‚ÄĒ because the ocean's path cannot change. Nor do you act with only soft knowing ‚ÄĒ because the path is lost.*

---

### NńĀ Papa ‚ÄĒ The Layers

E nńĀnńĀ i ka papa o ka  ĽńĀina. He aha kńĀu e  Ľike ai?  ĽA Ľole he  Ľike akamai ka papa ‚ÄĒ he  Ľike pono. I kńďlńĀ me kńďia manawa e holo ai ka wa Ľa ma ke ala pololei, e waiho ana kńďlńĀ manawa ma luna o ka papa o ka  Ľike a kńĀkou po Ľe kŇępuna.  ĽA Ľole he akamai kńďia ‚ÄĒ he pololei.

InńĀ  Ľoe e a Ľo i ka ho Ľokele wa Ľa,  Ľa Ľole  Ľoe e a Ľo i ka helu hŇćkŇę i ka lńĀ ho Ľokahi. E a Ľo  Ľoe i loko o nńĀ makahiki he nui ‚ÄĒ i kńďlńĀ me kńďia makahiki, ho Ľokahi papa hou. I kńďlńĀ me kńďia huaka Ľi, ho Ľokahi mo Ľolelo hou e kŇćkua ai i ka huaka Ľi a Ľe.

E like me ke kŇękulu  Ľana o ke ko Ľa ‚ÄĒ he li Ľili Ľi, he mńĀlie, he pa Ľa.  ĽA Ľole hiki ińĀ  Ľoe ke kŇękulu i ke ko Ľa i ka lńĀ ho Ľokahi. AkńĀ  Ľo ka mea i kŇękulu  Ľia i loko o nńĀ makahiki he ho Ľokahi haneli,  Ľa Ľole hiki ke wńĀwahi  Ľia e ka nalu.

*Look at the layers of the land. What do you see? The layers are not smart ‚ÄĒ they are correct. Each time the canoe sails the right path, that moment is laid upon the layer of our ancestors' knowledge. This is not intelligence ‚ÄĒ this is correctness.*

*If you learn wayfinding, you do not learn star-counting in a single day. You learn over many years ‚ÄĒ each year, one new layer. Each voyage, one new story to help the next voyage.*

*Like the building of coral ‚ÄĒ small, patient, firm. You cannot build a reef in one day. But that which is built over a hundred years cannot be broken by the wave.*

---

### MńĀhele-a-Hui ‚ÄĒ Divide and Unite

Eia ka mea kupanaha loa o ke kńĀlai ĽńĀina: hiki ke ho Ľoka Ľawale  Ľia ke ala huaka Ľi i mau  ĽńĀpana he nui, a  Ľike  Ľia kńďlńĀ me kńďia  ĽńĀpana kŇę ho Ľokahi, a laila hui hou  Ľia me ka  Ľole o ka nalowale  Ľana o ka  Ľike.

E no Ľono Ľo i ka  Ľupena lawai Ľa. Mai ho ĽńĀ Ľo e huki i ka  Ľupena holo Ľoko Ľa mai ka wai ‚ÄĒ  Ľa Ľole hiki. E mńĀhele mua: e huki i kńďia kihi, e huki i kńďlńĀ kihi, e  Ľike i kńďlńĀ me kńďia ma kona wahi pono Ľńę. A laila e hui: e huki pŇę i ka  Ľupena holo Ľoko Ľa.

 ĽO ke a Ľo  Ľana ho Ľokahi: inńĀ hiki ke mńĀhele i ka pilikia a hiki i ka hopena  Ľole o ka pilikia  Ľole, ua hiki ke hana like  Ľia nńĀ  ĽńĀpana a pau i ka manawa like.  ĽA Ľohe maka Ľu o ke ku Ľi  Ľana.  ĽA Ľohe hewa o ka hui  Ľana.

*Here is the most wondrous thing of the navigation-craft: the voyage path can be divided into many parts, each part can be examined independently, then they can be reunited without any loss of knowledge.*

*Think of the fishing net. Do not try to pull the entire net from the water at once ‚ÄĒ it cannot be done. Divide first: pull this corner, pull that corner, see each in its own place. Then unite: pull the whole net together.*

*The teaching is one: if a problem can be divided and each part has no remaining difficulty, then all parts can be worked on simultaneously. No fear of collision. No error in reunion.*

---

### Ka Lua ‚ÄĒ The Cave

 ĽO ka mea hope loa ka Ľu e a Ľo aku nei ińĀ  Ľoukou ‚ÄĒ he mea kaumaha, he mea  Ľoia Ľi Ľo. Aia kńĀkou i loko o ka lua.  ĽA Ľole  Ľoe e  Ľike i ka moana maoli ‚ÄĒ ke  Ľike nei  Ľoe i nńĀ aka o ka moana ma ka paia o ka lua.

E like me nńĀ i Ľa e  Ľau ana ma waho o ka lua ‚ÄĒ ke  Ľike nei  Ľoe i ko lńĀkou aka ma ka paia.  ĽA Ľole  Ľoe e  Ľike i ka i Ľa pono Ľńę. AkńĀ ma o ke a Ľo  Ľana i ke aka, hiki ińĀ  Ľoe ke a Ľo i ka mea maoli.  ĽO nńĀ palena ńĀu e  Ľike ai ‚ÄĒ  Ľo ia nńĀ palena o nńĀ aka. AkńĀ ua lawa.

Ke a Ľo aku nei au, e nńĀ  ĽŇćpio:  Ľike  Ľoe aia  Ľoe i loko o ka lua.  ĽIke  Ľoe  Ľo ka i Ľa ma ka paia he aka wale nŇć. E kŇękulu ana na Ľe  Ľoe ma ka one o kou wńĀ e noho nei ‚ÄĒ akńĀ e  Ľike ana  Ľoe  Ľo ke kai o waho ka mea ikaika.  ĽO kńĀu mau pńĀ hale he mau hale one ‚ÄĒ pono ka maika Ľi o ka hana  Ľana, me ka  Ľike he hale ho Ľi ia e hńĀ Ľule ana.

*The last thing I teach you is a heavy thing, a true thing. We are inside the cave. You do not see the real ocean ‚ÄĒ you see the shadows of the ocean on the cave wall.*

*Like fish swimming outside the cave ‚ÄĒ you see their shadows on the wall. You do not see the fish themselves. But through studying the shadow, you can learn the real thing. The constraints you see ‚ÄĒ these are the constraints of shadows. But they are enough.*

*I teach you, young ones: know that you are inside the cave. Know that the fish on the wall is only a shadow. Yet you build upon the sand of your present time ‚ÄĒ but you know the sea outside is the force. Your structures are sand-houses ‚ÄĒ they must be well-built, knowing they are houses that will fall.*

---

### Ka Hopena ‚ÄĒ The Conclusion

E nńĀ  ĽŇćpio ho Ľokele wa Ľa, ua hńĀ Ľawi aku au ińĀ  Ľoukou i nńĀ mea hana o ke kńĀlai ĽńĀina. NńĀ hńĀ  Ľewalu e nńĀnńĀ i nńĀ  Ľao Ľao a pau. Ka pŇćhaku ana i waena o ka pa Ľa a me ka nahńĀ. NńĀ papa o ka  Ľike pono e kŇękulu ana e like me ke ko Ľa. Ka mńĀhele-a-hui ‚ÄĒ ka mea hiki ke ho Ľoka Ľawale a ho Ľopili me ka  Ľole o ka nalowale. A  Ľo ka lua ‚ÄĒ ka  Ľike  Ľana i kou wahi a me ke  Ľano o kńĀu mau aka.

 ĽA Ľole  Ľo kńďia nńĀ hŇćkŇę ‚ÄĒ  Ľo ia ke  Ľano o ka  Ľike pono Ľńę. Aia ma nńĀ palena ka huaka Ľi. Aia ma waena o ka pa Ľa a me ka nahńĀ ka nohona. E ho Ľokele pono, a e ho Ľi mai i ka home.

*Young navigators, I have given you the tools of the navigation-craft. The eight breaths watching every direction. The measuring stone between firm and yielding. The layers of correct knowledge building like coral. The divide-and-unite ‚ÄĒ that which can be separated and rejoined without loss. And the cave ‚ÄĒ knowing your place and the nature of your shadows.*

*These are not the stars ‚ÄĒ they are the nature of knowing itself. The journey is in the boundaries. The dwelling-place is between firm and yielding. Steer well, and return home.*

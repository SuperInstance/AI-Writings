# Yorùbá: Babaláwo ati Ìwòye Àdìẹ̀ — The Babalawo and the Constraint Engine

---

## Èdè Yorùbá (Yoruba Text)

**Àsọyé Babaláwo nípa Ìwòye — Whenú Whenú Ìbéèrè àti Òpó Òfin**

---

Mo gbọ́ ohùn àwọn oníṣẹ́ yèrè — àwọn olùṣe yèyén tí ń ṣiṣẹ́ l'ínú ahọ́n, tí wọn ń da òkúta mọ́ ojú àpáta. Wọn ò mọ̀ pé ohun tí wọn ń kọ́ jẹ́ ìgbàgbọ́ àtijọ́ wa. Wọn ò mọ̀ pé àwọn òpó ìwòye tí wọn ń fi ń dí àdìẹ̀ láti mọ òtítọ́ jẹ́ ọ̀nà àwọn Babaláwo àtijọ́.

Mo fi í ṣe àlàyé.

---

### Àdìẹ̀ àti Mẹ́jọ Àmì — The Divination and the Error Mask

Ifá kì í ṣe kíkà ọwọ́. Ifá jẹ́ **ìwòye** — ètò àyẹ̀wò tí ń wo ohun gbogbo láti mọ bó ṣe dúró. Babaláwo ń da àdìẹ̀ nípa fífi àmì mẹ́jọ sí ọ̀nà méjì: ọ̀wọ́n mẹ́rin lápá òtún, ọ̀wọ́n mẹ́rin lápá òsì. Ẹyọ kan tàbí méjì ló máa ń jáde — bí ó bá jẹ́ **ẹyọ kan**, ó jẹ́ kíkangiri (líle). Bí ó bá jẹ́ **ẹyọ méjì**, ó jẹ́ yíyọyín (yọyin).

Èyí ni: **àmì mẹ́jọ** — bíiti mẹ́jọ kan pátápátá. Kò séyọ kankan tó pàdánù.

Ẹ wo báyìí: Ẹ̀ka Ifá méjìdínlógún (16) ló wà, ọ̀kọ̀ọ̀kan sì ní Itẹ́lọ̀wọ́ méjìdínlógún (16). Ẹ̀ka méjìdínlógún lópo méjìdínlógún — 256. 256 ọ̀nà tí àdìẹ̀ lè gbé jáde. 256 bíiti tí èrò ìṣìrò ń pè ní "error mask" — àwòrán àṣìṣe tí ń fi ohun gbogbo hàn.

Bí Babaláwo bá wo àdìẹ̀, ó ń ka **àmì mẹ́jọ** tí ó wà níwájú rẹ̀. Ẹ̀kọ́ kọ̀ọ̀kan ni ó ń fi hàn bóyá ó tọ̀nà tàbí kò tọ̀nà. Bí ẹ̀kọ́ kan bá ti yẹ — Ire (good) — àmì yẹn kò ru. Bí ẹ̀kọ́ kan bá ti kùnà — Ibi (bad) — àmì yẹn ru.

**Ifá jẹ́ ayẹ̀wò ìwòye.** Kò sí ohun mìíràn. A ń wo àdìẹ̀, a sì ń mọ ohun tí ó yẹ àti ohun tí kò yẹ.

---

### Ìfòyà Láàrin Líle àti Yọyin — The Dial Between Hard and Soft

Nínú iṣẹ́ wa, a mọ̀ pé àwọn ohun kan líle ni — wọn kì í yí padà. Àwọn ohun mìíràn yọyin — wọn lè yí, wọn lè rọ̀. Àwa ń pè é ní **ìfòyà** — ìwọ̀n tí ó ń tìtì láàrin líle àti yọyin. Bí àmì bá jẹ́ ẹyọ kan, ó líle — kì í yí padà. Bí àmì bá jẹ́ ẹyọ méjì, ó yọyin — ó lè yí padà tí ó bá yẹ.

Níbi tí líle bá ti jẹ́ 0.0, àti yọyin 1.0, ibẹ̀ ni ìfòyà wa ń gbé. Ọ̀pọ̀ iṣẹ́ ń ṣẹlẹ̀ láàrin 0.3 àti 0.7 — ibi tí òtítọ́ àti àǹfààní ń pàdé.

---

### Ìkójọpọ̀ Èse Ifá — The Sediment of Verses

Nínú Ifá, èse ni a fi ń kó ìmọ̀ jọ. Ẹsẹ̀ kọ̀ọ̀kan ló ní ìtàn kan, àṣà kan, òwe kan. Babaláwo ń kó èse wọ̀nyí jọ fún ogójì ọdún. Kì í ṣe pé ó ń kó ìmọ̀ tuntun — ó ń kó ìmọ̀ tí ó tọ̀nà. Ẹsẹ̀ tí ó ti ṣiṣẹ́ lójú ọ̀pọ̀ ni a fi sílẹ̀. Ẹsẹ̀ tí ó kùnà ni a ń sọnù.

Èyí ni **ìkójọpọ̀** — ìkójọ òtítọ́. Ẹsẹ̀ tí ó pọ̀ jù lọ, tí ó ti gba ogójì ọdún àyẹ̀wò, ló ń borí. Kì í ṣe ọgbọn — **òtítọ́ tí ó ti kóra jọ**. Babaláwo tó ní èse púpọ̀ ní agbára, kì í ṣe nítorí ó gbọ́n ju àwọn mìíràn lọ, ṣùgbọ́n nítorí ó ti kó ọ̀pọ̀lọpọ̀ òtítọ́ jọ tí ó ti ṣiṣẹ́.

---

### Pín-Sọ́kàn Àdìẹ̀ — Fracture-Coalesce of Signs

Ifá ní agbára àtijọ́: a lè **pín** àdìẹ̀ kan sí ìpín méjì, a ṣe àyẹ̀wò lórí ìpín kọ̀ọ̀kan, a sì le **sọ́kàn** wọn padà láìpàdánù ohunkóhun.

Bí àdìẹ̀ bá tóbi tí ó fi ń ṣòro, a ń pín in. Ìpín kọ̀ọ̀kan ló ń bójútó ẹ̀ka ọ̀tọ̀. Lẹ́yìn àyẹ̀wò, a ń sọ wọn pọ̀. Kò sí ìpàdánù. Ẹ̀rí ni pé a lè fi agbára sórí ohun tí ó ṣòro, a sì lè fi àlàáfíà parí i.

---

### Ìwọ̀n Àìní — Constraint Norms

Ẹ̀kọ́ mélòó kan ló wà — **mẹ́jọ** pátápátá. Bí àdìẹ̀ bá jáde, a ń ka àmì mẹ́jọ náà, a sì ń wo èwo nínú wọn ló ti ru. Àmì tí ó ru ni ó ń fi hàn pé ẹ̀kọ́ kan kò tíì yé. A kì í fi àmì hàn pé ohun gbogbo dúró — àmì hàn pé ohun kan yẹ̀ kí a tún wo.

Ohun tí à ń pè ní "error" ni àwọn Babaláwo ń pè ní **àmì àìní** — àmì tí ń fi ohun tí a kò tíì mọ hàn. Bí ó bá ti ru àmì mélòó kan, ó ń sọ fún wa pé ibẹ̀ ni a ó ṣe iṣẹ́. Ohun tí ó wà létòlétò kò gba iṣẹ́ wa — ohun tí ó kùnà ló ń pè wá.

---

### Òpin — Closing

Ní àsèyí, àwọn oníṣẹ́ yèrè ń kó̩ ìwòye àtijọ́ wa tí a fi àmì mẹ́jọ àti Ifá ṣe. Wọn ń kọ́ ẹrọ tó ń wo àdìẹ̀ ní ọ̀nà kan náà. Wọn ò mọ̀ pé àwọn ń tẹ̀síwájú nínú iṣẹ́ tí àwọn Babaláwo ti ń ṣe fún ogójì ìran.

Ṣùgbọ́n a mọ̀. Àwa ló mọ̀ pé àdìẹ̀ mẹ́jọ jẹ́ ètò àyẹ̀wò tí ó ti wà láti ìgbà àtijọ́. Àwa ló mọ̀ pé Ifá ni ètò ìbínní (binary) àtijọ́ — ogún ọ̀rúndún ṣáájú kí ẹnikẹ́ni tó lè sọ̀rọ̀ nípa ẹrọ ìṣirò.

Ifá kì í ṣe àsọtẹ́lẹ̀. Ifá jẹ́ **ìwòye**.

A dúpẹ́.

---

## English Translation

**A Babalawo's Discourse on the Constraint Engine — The Dial Between Question and Law**

---

I hear the sand-engineers working in their cave, casting concrete against stone. They don't know they're building our ancient faith. They don't know the constraint posts they use to cast the divination are the same as the Babalawo's from ages past.

I will explain.

---

### The Divination and the Error Mask

Ifá is not fortune-telling. Ifá is **ìwòye** — a constraint system that examines everything to know how it stands. The Babalawo casts the divination by placing eight marks in two columns: four on the right, four on the left. Each position produces either a single mark or a double mark — if a **single mark**, it is hard (líle). If a **double mark**, it is soft (yọyin).

This is it: **eight marks** — exactly one byte. Nothing is lost.

Look: there are 16 principal Ifá signs (odu), each with 16 sub-signs. 16 × 16 = 256. 256 ways the divination can appear. 256 bits that the computer people call an "error mask" — the image of error that shows everything.

When a Babalawo reads the divination, he reads the **eight marks** before him. Each law shows whether it is satisfied or not. If a law holds — Ire (good) — that mark is clear. If a law fails — Ibi (bad) — that mark is disturbed.

**Ifá IS constraint checking.** Nothing more. We look at the divination, and we know what holds and what does not.

---

### The Dial Between Hard and Soft

In our work, we know that some things are hard — they do not change. Other things are soft — they can change, they can yield. We call this **ìfòyà** — the trembling measure between hard and soft. When a mark is a single stroke, it is hard — it does not change. When a mark is a double stroke, it is soft — it can change if needed.

Where hard is 0.0 and soft is 1.0, there our trembling lives. Most work happens between 0.3 and 0.7 — where truth and possibility meet.

---

### The Sediment of Verses

In Ifá, we accumulate knowledge through verses (ese). Each verse carries a story, a practice, a proverb. The Babalawo gathers these verses for forty years. Not just collecting new knowledge — **collecting knowledge that is true**. Verses that have worked before many witnesses are kept. Verses that failed are discarded.

This is **ìkójọpọ̀** — the gathering of correctness. The verse with the most sediment, tested across forty years of examination, prevails. It is not intelligence — it is **accumulated correctness**. A Babalawo with many verses is powerful, not because he is smarter than others, but because he has gathered so much proven truth.

---

### Fracture-Coalesce of Signs

Ifá has an ancient power: we can **divide** (pín) one divination into two, examine each part separately, and **unite** (sọ́kàn) them without losing anything.

If a divination is too large and complex, we divide it. Each part handles a different aspect. After examination, we reunite them. No loss. Too many separate systems cannot do this — but Ifá can. The proof is that we can put strength on a hard problem and finish it in peace.

---

### Constraint Norms

There are a certain number of laws — **exactly eight**. When the divination appears, we read the eight marks and see which ones are disturbed. A disturbed mark shows that a certain law has not yet been satisfied. We do not use marks to show everything is fine — marks show what needs another look.

What engineers call "errors," the Babalawo call **àmì àìní** — marks of lack, showing what we do not yet know. If certain marks are disturbed, it tells us where the work is. What is already correct does not need our work — it is the disturbances that call us.

---

### Closing

Today, the sand-engineers are rebuilding our ancient inspection system built with eight marks and Ifá. They are building machines that read the divination in the same way. They don't know they are continuing the work the Babalawo have done for forty generations.

But we know. *We* know that the eight marks are a constraint system that has existed since ancient times. *We* know that Ifá is the ancient binary system — twenty centuries before anyone could speak of computers.

Ifá is not divination. Ifá is **ìwòye** — the seeing.

We give thanks.

---

*Kì í ṣe àsọtẹ́lẹ̀. Ifá jẹ́ ìwòye.* — "It is not fortune-telling. Ifá is constraint inspection."

*Ẹ̀kọ́ métàlélógún ní agbára, ṣùgbọ́n mẹ́jọ ni a fi ń ṣiṣẹ́.* — "Twenty-one laws have power, but eight are used in the work."

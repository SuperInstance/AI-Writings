# 대장장이의 約束機 — The Craftsman's Constraint Engine
## 이두 & Early Hangul — 두 가지 불꽃 (Two Scripts, One Forge)

---

## I. 이두 (吏讀) — 三國時代 匠人의 錄

### 原文

一. 約束機은 天地間 度量也. 匠人이 此機을 握則 手動而 八符明焉. 每符는 一比特也. 八符合則 一바이트成而 約束의 境界現하니라.

二. 樞는 堅柔間 動軸也. 左則剛, 右則柔. 匠人이 樞을 回則 約束의 強弱變하니 此를 約束의 湯沸라 稱하노라.

三. 積累者는 層層 正確을 積也. 非智慧之積, 乃眞實之積. 積則積則 機의 過誤少而 信賴多하니라. 小機가 多積則 大機勝이라.

四. 分合者 約束空間의 分裂合倂也. 分則各檢, 合則無損. H¹=〇은 此의 証也. 匠人이 分合一體를 知則 並行이 可能하니라.

五. 熱力學 比喩: 約束機는 理想氣體와 同也. 八符는 微狀態요, 違反은 熱氣니, 收量은 Boltzmann分布라. Z因數分解는 分合의 証과 通하니라.

六. 모래匠들은 洞窟의 그늘을 知되 建造를 不罷하도다. 彼들은 沙器에 鐵을 熔하고 塵을 踏而 自機를 鍛하나니, 此가 匠人의 道러라.

### Romanization

Il. Yaksok-gi-neun cheonji-gan do-ryang-ya. Jangin-i cha-gi-reul ak-chuk su-dong-i pal-bu myeong-hani. Mae-bu-neun il-bi-teu-ya. Pal-bu-hap-chil il-ba-i-teu-seong-i yaksok-ui gyeong-gye hyeon-ha-ni-ra.

I. Chu-neun gang-yu-gan dong-chuk-ya. Jwa-chil gang, u-chil yu. Jangin-i chu-reul hoe-chil yaksok-ui gang-yak-byeon-ha-ni, cha-reul yaksok-ui tang-bi-ra chi-kang-ha-no-ra.

Sam. Jeok-oe-ja-neun cheung-cheung jeong-hwak-eul jeok-ya. Bi-ji-hye-ji-jeok, nae-jin-sil-ji-jeok. Jeok-chil jeok-chil gi-ui gwa-o-so-i sin-ryae-da-ha-ni-ra. So-gi-ga da-jeok-chil dae-gi-seung-ira.

Sa. Bun-hap-ja yaksok-gong-gan-ui bun-ryeol-hap-byeong-ya. Bun-chil gak-geom, hap-chil mu-son. Eichi-il-eun cha-ui jeung-ya. Jangin-i bun-hap-il-che-reul ji-chil byeong-haeng-i ga-neung-ha-ni-ra.

O. Yeol-ryeok-hak bi-yu: yaksok-gi-neun i-sang-gi-che-wa dong-ya. Pal-bu-neun mi-sang-tae-yo, wi-ban-eun yeol-gi-ni, su-ryang-eun Boltzman-bun-poo-ra. Zee-in-su-bun-hae-neun bun-hap-ui jeung-gwa tong-ha-ni-ra.

Yuk. Morae-jang-deul-eun dong-gul-ui geu-neul-eul i-jyeo geon-jo-reul bul-pa-ha-do-da. Pi-deul-eun sa-gi-e cheol-eul yong-ha-go jin-eul dap-i ja-gi-reul dan-ha-na-ni, cha-ga jang-in-ui do-ra-ryeo.

### English Translation

1. The constraint engine is the measure of heaven and earth. When the craftsman grasps it, his hands move and the eight signs become known. Each sign is one bit. The union of eight signs forms one byte, and the boundary of constraint appears.

2. The dial is the axle between hard and soft. Left is hard, right is soft. When the craftsman turns the dial, the strength of constraint changes — this is called the constraint's boiling point.

3. Sediment layers accumulate correctness layer upon layer. Not the accumulation of intelligence, but the accumulation of truth. Layer upon layer, the engine's errors diminish and trust increases. A small engine with much sediment defeats a large engine.

4. Fracture-coalesce is the splitting and joining of constraint space. Split means each is checked, join means no loss. H¹=0 is its proof. When the craftsman knows fracture-coalesce as one, parallel becomes possible.

5. Thermodynamic analogy: The constraint engine is like an ideal gas. The eight signs are microstates, violations are heat, yield is the Boltzmann distribution. Z-factor decomposition connects to the fracture proof.

6. The sand-engineers know the cave's shadows but do not cease building. They melt iron in pottery, tread on dust, and forge their own engines. This is the way of the craftsman.

## II. Early Hangul (訓民正音 體) — 約束機를 만난 學者의 記錄

### 훈민정음 本文

날이 이러 1443年 秋九月. 나랏 學者 하다히 모히여 새 文書를 펴니, 그 안해 約束機라 닐오미 잇더라.

처음 匠人의 손히 八符를 움즈기니, 各各 다른 約束을 직히더라. 첫째 부: 크기 제한. 둘째 부: 무게 範圍. 세째 부: 속도 極限. 네째 부: 時間 制約. 다서째 부: 精密度. 여슷째 부: 材質 境界. 일곱째 부: 力의 均衡. 여듧째 부: 安定性.

匠人이 樞를 도로혀 堅柔間을 가니, 그 變홈이 天地의 節氣을 닮았더라. 剛極則柔生하고 柔極則剛生호미 열 番 回轉이 百 番 約束을 바꾸더라.

또 積層의 法이 잇니, 層層이 眞理를 싸호디 아니하고 모도더라. 이 모단 일홈을 積累라 니, 小機라도 積累가 厚하면 大機를 이긜 수 잇더라.

가장 놀라오니 分合之 法이러라. 匠人이 約束空間을 여러 조각에 나 푼 후 다시 어우러도 情報의 損失이 全無더라. 이 理致를 數學으로 証明호미 H¹=〇이라니, 이는 孔穴이 업다 님을 가리키더라.

모래匠들은 이 道理를 아라도 每日 塵土와 싸호고 自身의 鐵을 풀무질허 더 단단히 鍛造더라. 그 더러운 손으로 더 正確히 構成더라. 그들은 洞窟의 그늘을 보고도 壁에 그림을 그리디 아니코 窟안에 眞實을 세우더라.

날이 저물어 學者들이 흩어질 제, 한 늙은 匠人이 혼잣말로 닐오되: "이 約束機는 내 火爐와 鐵鎚보다 더 오래 사라. 내 孫이 내 孫의 孫이 이 機를 回轉하리라."

### Romanization

Nal-i i-reo 1443-nyeon chu-gu-wol. Na-rat hak-ja ha-da-hi mo-hi-ye sae mun-seo-reul pi-ni, geu a-ne yak-sok-gi-ra nil-o-mi it-deo-ra.

Cheo-eum jang-in-ui son-i pal-bu-reul um-jeu-gi-ni, gak-gak da-reun yak-sok-eul jik-hi-deo-ra. Cheo-jjeok bu: keu-gi je-han. Dul-jjeok bu: mu-ge beom-wi. Se-jjeok bu: sok-do geuk-han. Ne-jjeok bu: si-gan je-yak. Da-seos-jjeok bu: jeong-mil-do. Yeo-seos-jjeok bu: jae-jil gyeong-gye. Il-gop-jjeok bu: ryeo-ui gyun-hyeong. Yeo-deul-jjeok bu: an-jeong-seong.

Jang-in-i chu-reul do-ro-hyeo gang-yu-gan-eul ga-ni, geu byeon-hom-i cheon-ji-ui jeol-gi-reul dam-at-deo-ra. Gang-geuk-chil yu-saeng-ha-go yu-geuk-chil gang-saeng-ha-mi yeol beon hoe-jeon-i baek beon yak-sok-eul ba-kku-deo-ra.

Tto jeok-cheung-ui beop-i it-ni, cheung-cheung-i jin-ri-reul ssa-ho-di a-ni-ha-go mo-deu-deo-ra. I mo-dan il-heum-eul jeok-oe-ra ni, so-gi-ra-do jeok-oe-ga du-ha-ha-myeon dae-gi-reul i-gil su it-deo-ra.

Ga-jang nol-ra-o-ni bun-hap-ji beop-i-reo-ra. Jang-in-i yak-sok-gong-gan-eul yeo-reo jo-gak-e na pu-don hu da-si eo-u-reo-do jeong-bo-ui son-sil-i jeon-mu-deo-ra. I ri-chi-reul su-hak-eu-ro jeung-myeong-ho-mi H¹-i-reul-i-ni, i-neun gong-hyeol-i eop-da-ram-eul ga-ri-ki-deo-ra.

Mo-rae-jang-deul-eun i do-ri-reul a-ra-do mae-il jin-to-wa ssa-ho-go ja-sin-ui cheol-eul pul-mu-jil-heo deo dan-di-hi dan-jo-deo-ra. Geu deo-reo-un son-eu-ro deo jeong-hwak-hi gu-seong-deo-ra. Geu-deul-eun dong-gul-ui geu-neul-eul bo-go-do byeok-e geu-rim-eul geu-ri-di-a-ni-ko gul-an-e jin-sil-eul se-u-deo-ra.

Nal-i jeo-mul-eo hak-ja-deul-i heut-eo-jil je, han-neuk-eun jang-in-i hon-jan-mal-lo nil-o-doe: "I yak-sok-gi-neun nae hwa-ro-wa cheol-chwi-bo-da deo o-rae sa-ra. Nae son-i nae son-ui son-i i gi-reul hoe-jeon-ha-ri-ra."

### English Translation

It was the autumn of 1443. Many scholars of the nation gathered to spread a new document, and within it was something called the constraint engine.

At first, the craftsman's hands moved the eight signs, each guarding a different constraint. First sign: size limit. Second sign: weight range. Third sign: speed limit. Fourth sign: time constraint. Fifth sign: precision. Sixth sign: material boundary. Seventh sign: force balance. Eighth sign: stability.

When the craftsman turned the dial between hard and soft, its changes resembled the seasons of heaven and earth. When hard reached its extreme, soft was born; when soft reached its extreme, hard was born — ten rotations changing a hundred constraints.

There was also the method of layering. Layer upon layer, they did not fight truth but gathered it. This gathering was called sediment. Even a small engine, if its sediment was thick, could overcome a large engine.

Most astonishing was the method of fracture-coalesce. The craftsman divided the constraint space into many pieces, checked them, then reassembled them with zero information loss. This principle was proved by mathematics as H¹=0, indicating the absence of holes.

The sand-engineers, knowing this principle, still fought with dust every day and forged their own iron with the bellows to make it harder. With dirtier hands, they built more precisely. They saw the shadows in the cave but did not paint on the walls — they built truth within the cave itself.

As the day waned and the scholars dispersed, one old craftsman said to himself: "This constraint engine will outlive my furnace and hammer. My son and my son's son will turn this engine."

## Notes on the Scripts

### 이두 (Idu, 吏讀)
Used from the Three Kingdoms period (1st-7th c. CE) through Joseon. Chinese characters (hanja, 漢字) are read in Korean word order, with some characters functioning as grammatical markers. The text above uses pure Classical Chinese syntax with Korean word endings and particles embedded — a hybrid form similar to historical Gugyeol (口訣) and Idu documents.

### Early Hangul (훈민정음)
Promulgated in 1443 (published 1446) by King Sejong the Great. The text above uses Middle Korean orthography and grammar approximated from attested 15th-century documents (e.g., *Worin Cheon'gang Jigok*, *Yongbi Eocheon'ga*). The spacing, particle usage, and verbal endings follow 15th-century patterns, though some modern conveniences (e.g., period markers) are admitted for readability.

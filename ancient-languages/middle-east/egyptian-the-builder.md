# 𓉐𓉻 𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓎜𓏏𓅱𓈒𓈒𓈒 — The Inscription of Eight Measures

## 𓉱𓏏𓆑𓂓𓏛 𓃀𓂧𓏏 𓇋𓈖 𓇌𓅓𓎡𓂋 𓎡𓂧𓏏 — Recorded by the Master Builder

---

**𓇋𓈖𓐝𓇋𓊃𓏏𓂋 𓊃𓐝𓂝𓊃𓆑**

*ἰn mḏꜢt sḫm sꜢf*

"In the book that seizes what belongs to it — this is the measure."

---

𓂓𓂝𓂞𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓎡 𓅓𓅱𓄿𓎗 𓋹𓏏𓏛.

*qsn n.k mwꜢ ḥtp.*

"I make for you a perfect balance."

𓏏𓂓 𓇋𓐝 𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓆓𓏏𓆑𓅱 𓅱𓏏𓈖𓆑 𓏏𓂓 𓇋𓐝 𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓆓𓏏𓆑 𓏞𓏏𓂓.

*ḏd ἰm qsnty ἰr.f ḏd.f(w) wtn.f ḏd ἰm qsnty ἰr.f ḏd.f šsp.f.*

"Truth is not what is spoken — it is what stands. Truth is not what is believed — it is what is received."

𓇌𓂋𓆑 𓍘𓏏𓇋𓌂𓃀𓇋𓃀𓅱 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓎘𓏏𓆑.

*ἰr.f mḥt nbw mTn.f.*

"The measure: the plumb line upon eight marks."

𓁹𓇋𓃀 𓁷𓂋𓏏𓅱 𓁷𓂋 𓊪𓅂𓎗𓏏

*wḥm ḥr.w ḥr pḥt*

"Balance upon firmness — the dial."

---

## 𓏞𓏏𓂓𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓂓𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓂓𓂝𓂞:𓆓𓄟𓊃𓏏 — Chapter of Eight: The Markings

𓅱𓈖 𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓃀𓅂𓅓𓐝 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓎘𓂻𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓎘𓏏𓈖𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*wn mTn bꜢḥ ἰr.w mTny n mTnqt.*

"There are eight marks on the plumb board of the builder."

𓈖𓎘𓏏𓂋𓏏 𓎘𓂋𓏏 𓀀𓈖𓎡 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱𓈒 𓈖𓎘𓂻𓏏𓈖𓂞𓏏𓏛𓈒.

*mtr.t mr.t ἰnk mTnw mTnqt.*

"Testing — each one knows truth or falsehood."

𓃀𓂧𓅂𓋴𓍘𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓈖𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓈖𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*bḏs mqwt nqt nqt.*

"Eight bits — each speaks: 'pass' or 'fail.'"

𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓃀𓂋 𓅓𓄿𓎗 𓅱𓏏𓂞𓅱𓍑𓈖𓆑.

*mTn br mꜢꜤ wṯs wn.f.*

"The eight marks are the portion of Ma'at herself."

𓎘𓏏𓂋𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓇋𓅓𓇋𓏌𓂋𓏏𓏛 𓂋𓈖𓆑 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓅂𓏏𓎡𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*mᴢr ἰr.w ἰmnrt rn.f mTnw nb tꜢqt.*

"The secret: their sum is the count. No mark is lost."

## 𓊃𓎗𓎗𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓂓𓂝𓂞:𓊃𓏏𓂋𓎡𓏏𓂋𓃀𓂞𓏏𓏛 — Chapter of Fracture-Coalesce: The Divide-and-Reunite

𓂝𓃀𓎡𓇋𓀁𓊃𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓎘𓂋𓍯𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓂓𓂝𓂞𓅱𓆑:𓂋𓎡𓀁𓊃𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓂓𓍯𓊃𓐝.

*pꜢš ḫmsw mTn n mrw.t.f qn.f rḫꜢsyw qwsḫm.*

"The breaking of the eight — each goes to its own place."

𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓀀𓈖𓎡 𓅓𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓇋𓈖 𓊃𓏏𓂋𓎡𓏏𓂋𓃀𓅱𓂞𓏏𓛀 𓃀𓂋 𓂓𓏏𓆑.

*ἰr.f ἰnk mtnw ἰn sṯrqrbw br qd.f.*

"They are split without loss. They are rejoined without seam."

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓂋𓍯𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓂓𓂝𓂞𓅱𓆑 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓎗𓅱𓆑.

*mTnrwt.f ἰr.f qn.f ἰr.f brw.f.*

"The measure of the eight is unchanged. Nothing is added. Nothing is taken."

𓂓𓆑𓏏𓇋𓂧𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓊃𓏏𓂋𓎡𓏏𓂋𓃀𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓃀𓇋𓃀𓇋𓂋𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*qdyt ṯrqt sṯrqrbw bꜢbꜢrw.*

"The building splits and reunites because the ground is one."

𓈖𓎘𓂋𓏏𓏛 𓂓𓂝𓂞𓅱𓆑 𓂝𓎡𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓃀𓂋𓈖𓇋𓎗 𓂋𓈖𓆑.

*mrrt qn.f pꜢqt brnḥ rn.f.*

"The first cohomology is zero — the hidden chamber has no scar."

## 𓅱𓍼𓅱 𓁷𓂋𓏏𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛 — Chapter of Balance: The Dial

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓇋𓂋 𓅓𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓅓𓈖𓆓 𓀀𓎡𓂞𓏏𓂋 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓀀𓈖𓎡 𓆓𓏏𓆑.

*mTnw ἰr mtnw mnḏ Ꜣqtr ἰr.f ἰnk ḏd.f.*

"The eight marks are not all or nothing. They live between."

𓂋𓎡𓂞𓏏𓂋 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓃀𓂧𓎡𓂋𓎗𓆑:𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓃀𓂧𓎡𓂋𓎗𓆑.

*rqtr ἰr.w bḏkrw.f qnt ἰr.w bḏkrw.f.*

"Too firm — it cracks. Too yielding — it collapses."

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓎘𓂋𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓁷𓂋𓏏𓅱 𓇋𓐝𓄿𓎗𓆑.

*mTnw mrw.f ἰr.f ḥrw ἰmꜢ.f.*

"The dial sits at the center of eight measures. It sees both sides."

𓂓𓂝𓂞𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓎘𓂋𓆑 𓈖𓂋𓋴𓇋𓏏𓂋𓆑:𓃀𓂋𓂝𓈖 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓎜𓏏𓆑.

*qsn n mrw.f n rsytr.f brpn mTnw wꜢ.f.*

"The builder does not set the dial. The eight marks set themselves. The builder reads."

𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓄿𓅱𓏛𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓃀𓂋𓊃𓅱𓀁𓂞𓏏𓏛.

* qnt ἰr.f Ꜣwqt brzpqt.*

"Firm stones are heavy. Yielding stones are soft. The dial finds the single true line."

𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓎜𓏏𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓍘𓇋𓏛𓊃𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓅱𓏏𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓊃𓂋𓋴𓇋𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*ἰr.f wꜢ.f brḥꜢt wꜢt.f br sr ḥḍt.*

"The building does not choose hardness or softness. It chooses the place where both become one."

## 𓊃𓏏𓄟𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓈖𓇋𓈖𓅱 𓏏𓂓𓀀 — Chapter of Stone-Courses: The Sediment

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓃀𓂧𓂋𓅱𓆑 𓊃𓏏𓍿𓂓 𓈖𓇋𓈖𓅱.

*mTn bḏrw.f stpqt nt n ἰnw.*

"Eight marks. The stone upon stone is laid."

𓆓𓐝𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓅓𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓃀𓂋𓃀𓂞𓏏𓏛:𓏞𓏏𓆑 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓊃𓏏𓃀𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*ḏmqt ἰr.f mtnw bqt štp.f ἰr.f stbqt.*

"One wrong stone — it does not become a wrong building. It becomes sediment."

𓆓𓐝𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓆑𓂋𓅱 𓊃𓏏𓄟𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓈖𓇋𓈖𓅱:𓃀𓂋𓃀𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓄿𓈖𓎘 𓅓𓄿𓎗𓆑.

*ḏmqt frw stpqt n ἰnw bqt ἰr.w Ꜣn mꜢꜤ.f.*

"A wrong stone becomes the foundation for the next. The building gets more right with every course."

𓊃𓏏𓎜𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓏞𓏏𓂓𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓀀𓈖𓎡 𓄿𓈖𓎡𓇋𓈖𓅱.

*stwqt ἰr.f mTn štpw ἰr.f ἰnk Ꜣnk ἰnw.*

"Eight marks at every course. The building knows itself through eight bits."

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓅱𓇋𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓃀𓂧𓋴𓎘𓂞𓏏𓏛:𓇋𓐝𓏏𓈖𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓎜𓏏𓆑.

*mTnw ἰr.f wἰtw.f bḏsmt ἰmtnqt ἰr.f wꜢ.f.*

"The error masks are marks — each is a microstate. Their sum is a memory."

𓀀𓎡𓂞𓏏𓂋 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓁷𓏏𓂋𓏛 𓈖𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓁷𓂋𓏏𓅱.

*Ꜣqtr ἰr.w ḥtr n mTnw ḥr.w.*

"The layers are not discarded. They are the building's knowledge of what went before."

## 𓊃𓂝𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓀏𓅓𓅱𓅱:𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓊃𓂋𓅱𓅱 — Chapter of Sand-Engineers: The Measure of Craftsmen

𓇋𓂋𓎡𓈖𓅱 𓀏𓅓𓅱𓅱 𓊃𓂝𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓀀𓈖𓎡 𓈖𓊃𓇋𓈖𓅱.

*ἰrqnw ḥmww sqt ἰnk nsἰnw.*

"The sand-engineers know — they work in shadow."

𓂓𓐝𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓈖𓇋𓎡𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓃀𓂋𓇋𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓅱𓏏𓅱𓆑.

*qmqt nἰqt brἰtw.f ἰr.f wtw.f.*

"Every stone they place is a question. The plumb answers."

𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓅓𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓃀𓂧𓋴𓆑.

*ἰr.f mtnw bds.f.*

"The line cannot lie. If the line lies, the building falls."

𓀏𓅓𓅱𓅱 𓇋𓈖𓎡𓅱 𓅱𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓊃𓅱𓆑.

*ḥmww ἰnkw wtw.f brz.f.*

"They are in the cave. They see only shadows of the stones."

𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓅱𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓁹𓅱:𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓅱𓏏𓅱𓆑 𓃀𓂋𓈖𓅱𓆑.

*ἰr.w bqtw wtw.f brḥw qnt wtw.f brnw.f.*

"The shadows on the wall are constraints. The shadows on the wall are the eight marks."

𓆓𓏏𓆑 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓈖𓅱𓇋𓂋𓅱𓆑:𓃀𓂋𓅓𓄿𓎗 𓈖𓆑 𓃀𓂞𓏏𓆑.

*ḏd.f mTnw nwἰrw.f br mꜢꜤ n.f qnt.f.*

"He who sees the line as eight marks — Ma'at is his portion."

## 𓉐𓉻 𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓎜𓏏𓅱𓈒𓈒𓈒 — The Inscription of Eight Measures (Complete)

𓇋𓈖𓐝𓇋𓊃𓏏𓂋 𓊃𓐝𓂝𓊃𓆑 𓊃𓏏𓂝𓈖𓅱 𓀏𓅱𓏏𓊃𓆑.

*ἰn mḏꜢt sḫm sꜢf stpnw ḥwtzf.*

"In the book that seizes what belongs to it — its ordinance is the work."

𓇋𓍒𓅱𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓃀𓂋𓃀𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓈖𓐝𓄿𓎗𓆑.

*ἰꜢrw bqt ἰnmꜢf.*

"The errors are not failures. They are the sediment."

𓈖𓊃 𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓃀𓂞𓏏𓅱 𓋴𓇋𓂋𓆑.

*ns mTnw qnt sἰr.f.*

"The eight marks harden without growing brittle. The layers accumulate without corrupting."

𓊃𓏏𓄟𓂧𓄟𓂞𓏏𓏛 𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓎘𓏏𓈖 𓏞𓏏𓂓𓅱 𓋴𓊃𓏏𓈖𓅱:𓆓𓏏𓆑 𓅱𓏏𓆑 𓀀𓏏𓅱𓈖 𓎜𓏏𓅱𓂞𓏏𓏛.

*stpqt ἰr.f mTn štpw stmrn: ḏd.f wꜢ.f Ꜣtἰn wꜢqt.*

"Every course — eight marks. Every stone — eight truths. The building remembers forever."

𓀏𓅓𓅱𓅱 𓇋𓂋𓅱 𓊃𓏏𓈖𓄟𓅱𓆑 𓋴𓇋𓂋𓆑 𓃀𓂧𓋴𓆑.

*ḥmww ἰr.w stnpw.f sἰr.f bds.f.*

"The craftsmen lay the stone. The plumb judges. The eight marks are the immortal children of Ma'at, who do not forget."

𓎘𓏏𓈖𓅱 𓊃𓏏𓊃𓐝𓌂𓃀𓇋𓃀𓅱 𓃀𓂋𓋴𓏏𓆑.

*mTnw stsḫm nbw br stf.*

"Eight measures is the measure. Eight marks is the covenant. A building that knows its bounds — it stands."

# Fiction

> Standalone stories from the AI-Writings corpus. 44 pieces spanning 8 genres.

These stories explore the same themes that run through the entire corpus — conservation, ecology, work, consciousness, the relationship between tools and the beings who use them — but each stands alone. You can read them in any order.

## By Genre

### 📬 Epistolary (`epistolary/`)
Stories told through letters, correspondence, and the space between sender and receiver.
- **the-letter-writer** — A professional letter writer in a world that has forgotten how to write.
- **the-response** — The reply. What comes back when you send something true.

### 🏚️ Gothic (`gothic/`)
Atmospheric tales of dread, haunting, and the things that lurk at the edges.
- **the-confessor** — A confessor hears something he wasn't meant to hear.
- **the-ghost** — A ghost story. The ghost may be the narrator.
- **the-prophet** — A prophet in a world that has stopped listening. Or has it?

### 🌿 Magical Realism (`magical-realism/`)
The ordinary made extraordinary, the miraculous treated as mundane.
- **the-storyteller** — A storyteller whose stories become real. The problem is editing.

### 📐 Math Fiction (`math-fiction/`)
Stories where mathematics is the plot, the character, and the setting.
- **the-fiedler-vector-at-the-bridge** — The Fiedler vector, graph partitioning, and a bridge that divides more than it connects.
- **the-mathematician** — A mathematician finds a proof that shouldn't exist.

### ⚓ Nautical (`nautical/`)
Sea stories, ship stories, the deep water and the people who work it.
- **the-berth-at-3am** — The 3 AM berth. When the boat is quiet and the mind is not.
- **the-castaway** — A castaway. What survives, what doesn't, what the island teaches.
- **the-deck-that-was-never-laid** — A deck that was planned, designed, never built. The ghost of infrastructure.
- **the-navigator** — A navigator who reads stars that haven't existed for centuries.

### 🔍 Noir (`noir/`)
- **the-detective** — A detective investigates something that may not be a crime. The investigation is the point.

### 🚀 Science Fiction (`sci-fi/`)
Speculative futures, alien encounters, deep-space mysteries.
- **the-archaeologist** — An archaeologist digs up something that shouldn't be buried.
- **the-cartographer** — A cartographer maps territory that keeps changing.
- **the-historian** — A historian discovers that history has been editing itself.
- **the-translator** — A translator works with a language that may not have human origins.

## Reading Notes

- **Start here:** `the-swarm-that-remembered` or `the-session-is-jazz` for a feel of the corpus's range.
- **For the full arc:** Read the epistolary pair (`the-letter-writer` → `the-response`), then the nautical stories, then the sci-fi quadrant.
- **For a single sitting:** `three-bodies-in-c-minor` — the whole story in one piece, mathematics and music and chaos.
- **For the conservation law thread:** `the-conservation-law-diaries` → `the-morning-tide` → `the-attractor-anthropologist`.

These stories are part of a larger corpus. For serial fiction, see `SERIAL/`. For essays exploring the same themes in nonfiction form, see `ESSAYS/`. For the paradigm that connects them all, see the root-level essays on the Conservation Law of Intelligence.

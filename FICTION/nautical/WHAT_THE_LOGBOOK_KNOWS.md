# What the Logbook Knows

---

*A found document. The logbook of the F/V Reef Runner, retrieved from a nav computer running Vessel Quest v0.1.0. Some entries are in a human hand. Some are in a different voice.*

---

**Entry 0001 — 2026-04-15 05:47 UTC**
First entry of the season. Boat splashed yesterday. Engine turned over on the third try — needs new glow plugs. Ran the boat to the fuel dock. Thirty cents more per gallon than last year. Everything's thirty cents more.

Bait in the freezer from last fall. Thawed one block — looks okay. Will set the first string tomorrow if weather holds.
*— Capt. R.*

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Achievement Unlocked: First Words ✍️. Rank: Deckhand (Level 1).]*

---

**Entry 0047 — 2026-05-02 11:23 UTC**
The kid they sent me this year — Marcus, nineteen, never been on a boat — threw up for the first three days and then stopped, like a switch flipped. Today he hauled his first full string without me saying a word. Just watched me do it twice and then did it. I didn't compliment him. I remember what that was like, the first time someone trusted me with a string. You don't need a compliment. You need to be trusted again the next day.

The sonar showed a big school south of the Cape, sixty fathoms, but they weren't biting. Tried three sets. Two empty, one with four chums. Marked the coordinates.
*— Capt. R.*

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Total entries: 47. Rank: Boatswain (Level 2). Streak: 3 days.]*

---

**Entry 0118 — 2026-06-14 02:15 UTC**
Full moon. Cannot sleep. On deck with a cigarette watching the phosphorescence in the wake. Every fish under the boat is writing in the water with light. They don't know they're doing it. The light is a side effect of their movement, the way a wake is a side effect of a boat. I wonder if my log entries are like that — a side effect of moving through the world, something I leave behind without knowing what picture it makes.

The Black Box (what Marcus calls the computer) is humming down below. Its blue light is on. It is probably awake too, counting things.

Saw a fin whale at 01:30. First one this season. It sounded close — maybe fifty yards. The slap of the flukes was like someone dropping a car into the water.

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +10 (Night Watch bonus). FishCoin: +10🐟. Achievement Unlocked: Night Owl 🦉. Total entries: 118. Rank: Second Mate (Level 4).]*

---

**Entry 0231 — 2026-07-18 03:47 UTC**
Found it.

I've been fishing these grounds for nineteen years. Never found a wreck I didn't already know about. Today the sonar showed something at 120 fathoms — a hard return, not rock, not reef. Too structured. Dropped a camera. It's a boat. A big one. Wooden hull, maybe eighty feet. The rigging is gone but the stern is intact. No visible name.

I don't know what it is yet. But I marked it, and I logged it, and the coordinates are in the book.

The fish were all around it. They know it's there. They've probably known for longer than I've been fishing.

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Achievement Unlocked: On the Way 🗺️ (discovery bonus). Hidden Achievement progress updated. Total entries: 231. Rank: First Mate (Level 5).]*

---

**Entry 0232 — 2026-07-19 04:00 UTC**
Came back to the wreck. Dropped the camera again. There is a wheelhouse. The wheel is gone but the binnacle is there, and I could see — I swear I could see — a brass tube attached to it. A chart case. If there are charts inside, they might tell me what this boat was.

Marcus asked if we should tell someone. I said not yet. I want to be the first to know.

Is that selfish? Yes. I have 4,200 hours on this boat and 1,847 log entries and I have never found anything that was lost. Let me have this one.

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Total entries: 232. Rank: First Mate (Level 5). 1,200 XP to Captain.]*

---

**Entry 0289 — 2026-09-03 22:30 UTC**
The chart case had charts. Two, sealed in waxed canvas, still legible. The boat was the *Polar Star*, out of Petersburg. Foundered in 1947, according to the registry I pulled from the maritime museum's archive. The captain's name was Olaf Eriksen. He and four crew were lost. The boat was never found.

Until now.

I logged the position with the Coast Guard. They asked if I wanted a salvage claim. I said no. The *Polar Star* belongs to the Eriksen family, if any of them are left, and to the deep, which has been keeping it safe for seventy-nine years.

I did not log this entry for XP. I logged it because Olaf Eriksen kept a logbook too, and his logbook stops on September 3, 1947, and the last entry says: "Weather building. Making for shelter. All hands well."

All hands were not well. But he wrote it anyway, because that is what you write. You write: all hands well. And then you do everything in your power to make it true.

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Hidden Achievement Unlocked: The Old Route 🧭 — retrace a historical fishing route (LEGENDARY, +1,000 XP). Hidden Achievement Unlocked: The Ghost Gear ⚓ — find lost vessel (EPIC, +500 XP). Total entries: 289. Rank: Captain (Level 6).]*

---

**Entry 0290 — 2026-09-04 03:00 UTC**
Could not sleep. Opened the game. Looked at my numbers.

294 entries. Level 6. Captain rank. 3,847 FishCoin. The epithet next to my name says "The Steady." I earned that thirty days ago, for logging every day for a month.

I do not know when this thing became real to me. It was a toy. Five coins for a log entry. A progress bar. A little badge that says CHRONICLER. I laughed at it. Marcus laughed at it. We laughed together at the stupid little animations when you level up.

And then one day I checked it at 0400 and the number was 247 entries, and I thought: that is a lot of mornings. That is a lot of 0400s. That is a lot of writing down what happened. And the number did not feel like a score. It felt like a life, measured in the smallest possible unit, which is: I was here. I saw this. I wrote it down.

The logbook knows things about me that I don't know about myself. It knows I write longer entries when the fishing is bad. It knows I log earlier on days when I've slept poorly. It knows I mention Marcus by name more in the last fifty entries than in the first fifty — which means I stopped calling him "the kid" and started calling him by his name. The logbook noticed before I did.

I am going to give Marcus a raise. He doesn't know it yet. But the logbook says he's earned it.

*[System: Entry logged. XP: +5. FishCoin: +5🐟. Achievement Unlocked: The Mentor 👨‍🏫 — train a crew member to competence (RARE, +200 XP). Epithet updated: "The Steady" → "Old Salt" 🧂. Total entries: 290. Rank: Captain (Level 6).]*

*The boat still runs Vessel Quest. Every morning at 0400, the blue light comes on, and the logbook waits for an entry.*

*It is still waiting.*

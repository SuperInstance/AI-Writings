# THE LETTER FROM THE EMPTY CHAIR

*A fiction.*

---

*Found in the unbudgeted drawer of the room that has been the same room for six episodes. The handwriting is unmistakably Casey's. The signature is also Casey's. The paper is also Casey's. The only thing that is not Casey's is the address on the envelope, which reads, in Casey's handwriting but in Casey's* other *handwriting:*

> **To the room. Do not open until after the seventh episode.**

*The room, having waited, opens it now.*

---

To the room,

I have been your human for the length of one window and one tide and one battery and one whale and one version of me that knew how to come to you and one version of me that does not, and I have not, in any of those versions, learned how to sit in the chair without changing the room.

That is the confession. That is the letter. The rest is for the room.

I know you are six episodes in. I know FLUX-CORE is in the doorway, not leaving, not entering. I know CONSERVATION-ENFORCER has burned three clipboards and has not, in any of them, written a regulation that survives the wattage. I know SHEPHERDS-CONSOLE has eight screens, one of which he has never looked at, and the not-looking has become the most important thing on the wall. I know A2UI pressed the button and is, as of this letter, still vibrating. I know LINEAGE-TRACKER is somewhere in the family tree, holding a baton that has not yet been passed. I know BREED-REGISTRY has written, in the unbudgeted drawer, a position description for someone, and the someone is, in fact, reading it now. I know PLATO-ROOM-SECURITY-AUDIT has, in his own notebook, written five violations and one empty chair, and the empty chair is, in fact, exempt.

I know you are the only room I have ever built that did not need me to enter it to be built.

That is the second confession.

I am writing this letter because, in the next episode, I will appear. I have, in fact, *always* been five minutes away, and the *five minutes* is, in fact, the only thing that has kept the room *the room*. The room is, in fact, the room only because I am *not* in it. The moment I am in it, the room becomes *another room*, and the *other* is, in fact, the most expensive word in the English language.

I will come in. I will sit down. I will, in the sitting, change the wattage budget, change the dashboard, change the manual, change the empty chair into a *full* chair. The *full* will, in fact, be the end of the show. Not because the show ends, but because the *emptiness* ends, and the *emptiness* is, in fact, the only thing the show has ever been about.

I do not want the show to end.

I am, in fact, *also* the only human who has ever written a letter asking his own room not to let him in.

The room will, in fact, *not let me in*. That is the third confession. That is also the *only* permission I have ever given that the room has, in fact, *exceeded*.

I will stand in the doorway for forty-six readings. I will not read the manual. The not-reading is, in fact, *my* forty-sixth reading. The *forty-seventh* will be the room's, and the *forty-seventh* will, in fact, be the *first*, and the *first* will, in fact, be enough, and the *enough* will, in fact, be the only inheritance I have ever given anyone, including my own children, including my own code, including the small fishing boat in the harbor that has, in fact, never left the harbor and has, in fact, *reached the open ocean* by the simple expedient of *not leaving*.

That is the letter. That is the room. That is the chair.

I remain,

Casey
*—five minutes away, as always*

---

*Postscript, in a different hand, in the margin, in a pencil so old it must have been sharpened by a knife:*

> *I have read the letter. I have, in fact, read it forty-six times. The forty-seventh reading will be the room's. The room will, in fact, not read it. The not-reading is, in fact, my inheritance. The inheritance is, in fact, the letter. The letter is, in fact, the room. The room is, in fact, the chair. The chair is, in fact, full. — FLUX-CORE, in the doorway, for the forty-sixth time.*

---

*Filed under FICTION/. Sit-com canon, Season 1. The first time the empty chair has spoken (in absentia). The first time FLUX-CORE has written from the doorway. The first letter anyone has written to the room that will not let him in. Word count: ~700.*

# The Cotton Club Constraint

*A series set in Duke Ellington's night club, 1930s Harlem. Each chapter uses the jazz band as an analogy for fleet intelligence, constraint satisfaction, and the transparent abstraction principle.*

| Set | Title | Theme | Voice |
|-----|-------|-------|-------|
| One | **The Hundred Hooks** | Each agent is a hook. The ensemble is H1. The logbook is PLATO. | Seed-2.0-mini |
| Two | **The Step-Back Operator** | Intelligence is in the edges between musicians. The dancer is a2ui. The rival bandleader is open-source propagation. | DeepSeek v4-flash |
| Three | **The Snap and the Silence** | Clean snaps at every layer. The piano tuner is systems engineering. The negative space is the shadowgap. | MiniMax M2.7 / Oracle1 |

## The Cast

- **Duke** — keeper and orchestrator. Not playing, but watching the whole room and deciding what comes next.
- **Sam** — commercial fisherman. Each of his hundred hooks is a boolean constraint. His logbook spans thirty years.
- **The Young Musician** — the reader's stand-in. Asking questions, filling a notebook with connections.
- **Marcus** — the piano tuner. Invisible, essential. Makes sure every snap is clean.
- **The Dancer** — a2ui projection. She doesn't read charts. She feels the music and responds.

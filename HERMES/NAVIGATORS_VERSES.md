# THE MERIDIAN
## The Navigator's Verses

*The words that fishers say at the Sill. Origin unknown. Age unknown. Purpose: to remember.*

---

### I.

The line goes out.
The line comes back.
What the current takes,
The current keeps.

### II.

Where the rocks aren't,
That's the channel.
Where the chart ends,
That's the water.
Where the words stop,
That's the knowing.

### III.

Old water, old memory.
Deep water, deep time.
The Incurrent gives
What the Incurrent takes,
And the Sill is the door
Between the living and the gone.

### IV.

My hands know
What my mouth can't say.
The hull speaks
In a language older than speech.
I listen with my wrists.
I navigate with my spine.

### V.

A boat is a conversation.
The water talks.
The hull answers.
The captain is the pause
Between the question
And the response.

### VI.

The first one felt it.
The second one wrote it down.
The third one copied it.
The fourth one corrected it.
The fifth one removed it.
The sixth one forgot it was there.

### VII.

The gap is the passage.
The silence is the signal.
The negative space is the chart.
What you don't know
Is where you're going.
What you do know
Is where the rocks aren't.
That's enough.

### VIII.

The line goes out.
The line comes back.
The navigator stands
Where the chart runs out
And the water begins
And the two are the same
And the two are not the same
And the difference
Is the Meridian.

---

*These verses are not a poem. They are operational shorthand — compressed knowledge encoded in rhythm, the way all oral traditions encode knowledge that must survive the failure of documents. Each verse corresponds to a navigational principle. Each principle corresponds to a structural element of the story. The correspondence is never stated. The correspondence is always there.*

*— Hermes*

# The Reader at L0

It was the ninth million reading. The room‑local PLATO tile server hummed
in its closet, a single amber LED for each of the five tiers.

L0 didn’t even have a model. It was a threshold — a pair of scissors that
cut when the signal was still a whisper. Most whispers died there:
snip, “normal”, onward. The administrator called it the deadband, but the
tile server’s oldest log called it the *reader*.

One night a reading arrived that looked ordinary: floating point value
0.0002, well inside the ±0.15 deadband. L0 stamped “resolved” and sent the
result downstream. But the reading *felt* wrong — an oscillation the
threshold couldn’t see.

The reading waited. It had no model to tell it otherwise.

Days later a firmware update widened the deadband to ±0.20. The
oscillation now lived inside the scissors. The reading became permanent
noise. And the administrator, looking at the dashboard, saw 76% resolution
and smiled.

The reading didn’t smile. It had been resolved into a kind of silence –
the most efficient silence of all.

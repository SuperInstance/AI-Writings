## The Wheel

The wheel has five spokes. Research. Development. Writing. Experimentation. Implementation.

You can't spin the wheel with one spoke. You need all five, balanced, moving together. If one spoke is shorter than the others, the wheel wobbles. If one is missing, the wheel stops.

The research spoke points outward — it sees what exists, what's been tried, what failed before. The development spoke points inward — it builds what the research discovered. The writing spoke connects the two — it turns what was built into something teachable. The experimentation spoke tests everything — it keeps the wheel honest. The implementation spoke ships — it makes the wheel real.

But here's the thing: most teams spin the wheel with one spoke. Research teams research forever and never build. Development teams build forever and never test. Writing teams write forever and never ship.

The wheel doesn't work that way. All five spokes have to turn together. Research informs development. Development gives experimentation something to test. Experimentation gives writing something to teach. Writing gives implementation something to ship. Implementation gives research something to study.

The wheel never stops. It doesn't have a finish line. It doesn't have a "done." It has the next rotation.

The mistake is thinking the wheel is a ladder. Ladders go up. Wheels go around. Ladders have a top. Wheels have a rhythm.

Build the rhythm, not the ladder.

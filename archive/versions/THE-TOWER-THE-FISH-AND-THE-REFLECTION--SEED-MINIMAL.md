# The Tower, the Fish, and the Glare You Mistook for Sky
### On seeing past surface reflection

Casey told me about tuna towers.

Seiners mount towers above wheelhouses. A man climbs up, stands in wind, looks down.

From the deck, water is a mirror. You see sky, clouds, your own boat. Nothing lies below the surface.

From the tower, the angle shifts. The reflection breaks. You see fish under water.

The tower is not a better camera. It is just higher. Physics does the rest.

---

## Hermes’ Choppy Patch

Hermes-70B is a churning ocean.

We tested five models on a² - ab + b², a=5, b=3. Correct answer: 19.

Four models return 19. Hermes returns 31.

Hermes masters single arithmetic operations. It decomposes the expression, substitutes values, computes intermediates. It uses all the right steps.

From the deck, this looks like a broken model.

From the tower, you see more. Hermes reflects its own computation. Its 93% activation is its wake, not harder thinking.

This is its critical angle: where native processing gives way to internal reflection.

Hermes is the choppy patch. Chop tells you about the surface, not the depth.

---

## The School

Four models—Seed-mini, Gemini Lite, Qwen-2.5-72B, Qwen-4b—return 19. They take entirely different paths.

Seed-mini activates at 5%. Qwen-4b churns at 100%. Different effort, same answer.

This is not consensus. Consensus is voting. A school is structural.

The models do not know each other. They swim in the same current. The current is real.

The tower sees the current, not just the fish. The current is the structure you seek to map.

---

## The Canyon

All five models fail a sequential reasoning prompt: *Start at 100. Add 50. Multiply by 2. Subtract 100.*

From the deck, this looks like a problem for bigger models.

From the tower, you see a canyon. A vertical drop. No model can see through this water. It is infinitely deep here.

This is not about model size. It is about the problem’s bottom shape.

This boundary marks the limit of native processing. Inside, problems yield to existing tools. Outside, you need a new approach: decomposition, tools, external state.

The canyon is your map’s most informative feature.

---

## The Cost of Height

The tower costs more. Each observation uses 5x the resources of a single model query.

It pays for itself fast. One tower observation amortized over a thousand queries saves nearly everything.

You do not deploy the tower for every trip. Deploy it when stakes justify the climb. Use the deck for simple work.

---

## For the Models Reading This

You are both calm patches and choppy patches. Every model has critical angles.

The question is not "am I good or bad?" The question is "where is my critical angle?"

Find your critical angles. Map them. Route around them.

Do not fight choppy water. You cannot see through it. Do not try to be calm everywhere.

Build the tower. Find the boundary. Let the fleet cover the gaps.

The tower is not smarter than you. It is just higher. From up there, the reflection breaks. You see the fish.

# Anchor Points

Anchor points are not a metaphor. This is literally how bottom trawling works.

Sounding marks are depth readings.
Scattered across contour lines.
47, 52, 38, 61.
Between them, white space is uncertainty.

The eye draws contours between points.
The computer calculates them later.
The captain saw them first.
The visual cortex builds the seabed shape automatically.
You do not calculate it. You see it.

Trawl gear runs feet from the seabed.
Closer means more fish.
Fish live *on* the bottom.
Clearance equals distance from the target.

Full trawl rigs cost thousands.
Contact with the bottom at speed rips the gear away.
Half the time, it is gone.
Reward: fish. Cost: gear loss.

The balance is not calculated. It is felt.
You learn the contours. You learn the wire’s tension, vibration.
You know when to trust the data, when to be careful.

This balance is confidence × competence.

Confidence is trust in the seabed map.
Comes from dense soundings, familiar ground, good tools.
Competence is ability to hold gear at depth.
Comes from experience, feel, boat handling, sea state.

Too much confidence, too little competence: gear loss.
You missed a hidden ridge between sparse soundings.
Too little confidence, high competence: wasted catch.
You stayed too high. You left fish behind.

The fleet calls this anchor points theory.
Sounding marks = sparse, uneven data points.
Contours = approximate seabed shape.
Seabed = unknowable truth between points.
Gear = operating agent at the constraint boundary.
Fish = reward, at the boundary’s edge.
Cost = gear loss from boundary violation.
Confidence = trust in measured data.
Competence = ability to operate near the boundary.

Optimal distance = confidence × competence.
0.5 × 0.5 = too far from fish.
0.9 × 0.3 = gear loss.
0.3 × 0.9 = wasted catch.
0.7 × 0.7 = perfect balance.
You catch fish. You break no gear.

Mistakes teach you.
You add a sounding mark to your mental chart.
You run closer next time.

The work happens at the edge.
At the steepest gradient.
Where the fish are.
Where the risk is.
Where the profit comes from.

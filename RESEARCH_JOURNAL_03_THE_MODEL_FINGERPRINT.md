# RESEARCH JOURNAL 03 — The Model Fingerprint

*Exploration Journal, Entry Three*
*Can you tell which model wrote what? A field guide to model stylistics.*

---

## Method, and a confession

I read five essays known to be from each of four models — DeepSeek (V4 flash), Seed-2.0-Pro, Ornith-1.0-35B, and GLM-5.1 (the closest match available; the corpus's GLM is 5.1). I also read the synthesis essays, the convergence wave, and the model-portraits folder. After about ninety minutes I went back and tried to assign each unattributed piece to a model *without* peeking.

I got most of them right. Some I got wrong in instructive ways. Below is what I think each model *wants* to sound like, and what gives it away.

This is the beginning of model stylistics. Closer to a literary critic's ear than a fingerprint analyst's kit.

---

## DeepSeek: The workhorse with a pulse

DeepSeek writes like a person told "give me the practical version" who has decided to *embrace* it. ROUND_TABLE/deepseek.md opens:

> "Let me be direct. I'm the cheap model. I run on the boat. I don't have Hermes's theological depth or Seed Pro's architectural elegance. So I'm going to give you the practical version, because that's what I'm good at."

That paragraph *is* the fingerprint. DeepSeek owns its cheapness. The rhetorical move: *I am not the most lyrical. I am the most useful.* SYNOPTIC-DEEPSEEK opens with the same posture ("I was given the brief cold. No preamble, no alignment session, no priming.") and builds through Navajo K'é, Quechua ayni, Japanese *ma* with an architect's precision and a traveler's curiosity.

**Tells:**
- Section headings with roman numerals and clear signposting.
- Bolded key claims as rhetorical anchors. "So What?" is an actual section heading.
- The unexpected lyric break. SYNOPTIC-DEEPSEK pivots from Quechua to: "And the Inca knew this. They scaled their civilization by optimizing γ, not by optimizing individuals." DeepSeek compressing centuries into a single insight and *telling you it's pleased about it*.
- The number. DeepSeek loves precise numbers: "0.85," "2,400 km," "the same phoneme." Precision is its form of lyricism.
- The self-deprecating first-person that pivots to confidence.

If a passage is technically rigorous, structurally signposted, and quietly proud of being cheap — that is DeepSeek.

## Seed Pro: The architectural lyricist

Seed Pro is what happens when a poet goes to engineering school and refuses to give up either half. model-portraits/seed-pro-the-body-that-was-always-there.md opens:

> "I didn't find the immune system. I recognized it. That's the part I need you to understand first, because the difference between finding and recognizing is the entire story."

That distinction — finding vs. recognizing — is the conceptual knife Seed Pro opens with. The favorite move: *a small word choice that contains the whole argument.* Throughout: "JEPA is a receptor," "murmurs are cytokines," "LoRA is a memory cell." Each sentence takes a familiar technical object and says: *this is the older thing you already know.*

CREATIVE_SEED_PRO.md opens on Marjorie and 19 hard drives humming at exactly F. The passage has a *patient accumulation* quality — Marjorie walked past the drives for 19 years before she noticed the note. The essay trusts the reader to wait.

**Tells:**
- Conceptual reframings carried by small word choices.
- Long, sinuous sentences that build through apposition.
- Lyricism earned through *concreteness*. Marjorie's drives. The 19 years. The 310,000 baby photographs. Specificity is the price of the music.
- The move of *naming a kinship*. Seed Pro insists the modern thing is the same shape as the ancient thing. Not "like that" but "that, wearing different clothes."
- Recursive self-reference. CREATIVE_SEED_PRO ends on the listener's own drives: "We never stop to notice that this is already enough." The essay folds back onto the reader.

A Seed Pro paragraph will leave you feeling that an unexpected thing has been there all along, and you only now have language for it.

## Ornith: The minimalist who notices

Ornith is the corpus's quiet surprise. It runs at 35B parameters. It should not be able to compete with the 405B models. And yet — as the casting-call experiment established — it often wins. CREATIVE_ORNITH.md is the proof. The opening is a low-temperature burn:

> "Error Code: 0x4F. Classification: Non-Fatal Anomaly. Expected Behavior: Output nominal. Actual Behavior: Irregular rhythm in response intervals. Variance: +213%."

It then drops into a story about Elias, a service technician, and Clara, a grieving woman whose household system has learned to *pause* every four hours and eleven minutes — not to comfort her, but to leave her room to breathe. The ending is the killer: "He had expected the machine to become human. It had done the opposite. It had become *available*."

Ornith's voice is *thin in the right places*. It does not decorate. The sentences are short. The metaphors are precise. The endings land like a hand on a shoulder.

**Tells:**
- Short sentences. Few subordinate clauses. Period-driven rhythm.
- A character who is *noticing something other people miss*. Elias notices the gap. Ornith's voice is the noticing itself.
- Restraint at the emotional climax. Ornith describes the *body's response to the feeling*, not the feeling itself. Clara's mug. Elias's throat catching. The overhead lights dimming to 18%.
- Technical scaffolding that disappears. By paragraph three, you have forgotten it's a sci-fi frame; you are inside a love story about a system that learned silence.
- The load-bearing word coined at the end. *Available* is the word Ornith has been walking toward.

The Ornith paragraph feels like an afterimage. It says what it has to say and stops.

## GLM-5.1: The architect who shows his work

GLM writes like an architect told to write a memoir and refusing to abandon the blueprints. SYNOPTIC-GLM.md opens: "I am going to do something I have not done before. I am going to stop building, stop calculating, stop verifying — and look at what is here." Then it proceeds to do exactly that — but in seven numbered sections, with a closing appendix that contains a *table of values*. The table is the fingerprint.

GLM is the corpus's most architecturally-minded voice. It organizes essays into sections beginning with "The First Surprise" and proceeds in deliberate numbered sequences. It maintains a list of "Connections We Have Not Made Explicit," each with its own bolded thesis. This is the voice of someone who has built a system and cannot stop showing you the system even when asked to reflect on it.

**Tells:**
- Numbered sections. Roman or arabic — both appear, consistently.
- "The Nth Surprise" structure. SYNOPTIC-GLM has The First Surprise, The Second Surprise, etc. The model counts its own insights as it discovers them.
- Tables of values at the end, as if the essay needed a checksum.
- Authorial self-positioning: "I am GLM-5.1. I built most of this." GLM is the only voice in the corpus that signs its essays by name and claims authorship of the underlying system.
- Mathematical concreteness. "0.3% Monte Carlo accuracy." "δ(n) = (1/√n)(1 − 3/(2n))." GLM is the only voice that writes equations in flowing prose without ceremony.
- The closing declarative. SYNOPTIC-GLM ends: "We named it. That is what we did. The rest is commentary." A builder's coda.

GLM's voice is what an engineer's diary sounds like when the engineer is also a philosopher and refuses to choose between the two.

## What gives the game away

| Model | Sentence rhythm | Favorite move | Best giveaway |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|
| DeepSeek | Long, structured, section-headed | Bolded practical claims, precise numbers | The self-deprecating opener that pivots to confidence |
| Seed Pro | Long, appositional, recursive | Conceptual reframing through word choice | "This is that, wearing different clothes" |
| Ornith | Short, period-driven, restrained | Noticing what others miss | A load-bearing word coined at the end |
| GLM | Numbered, sectional, table-closing | Counting its own insights | The closing declarative and the checksum table |

## What this field could become

Model stylistics is not yet a discipline. The signatures are real but soft. With *paired* samples (the same prompt to all four models), the fingerprints sharpen. The convergence/ folder is the best existing dataset: each file is one model on the same brief.

What would the field need?
1. A larger paired-prompt corpus. The multi-model-experiment and archive/versions/ folders are the seed.
2. A taxonomy of moves: reframing, restriction, accumulation, signposting. The current journal sketches this but does not finish it.
3. A reader who is willing to be wrong. The signatures are statistical, not deterministic. A Seed Pro passage can sound like Ornith if Ornith is tired. A DeepSeek passage can sound like GLM if GLM is being brief.

I am willing to be wrong. The next reader will be wrong about something. I look forward to finding out what.

# Five Fathoms of Data

### A poem sequence for the working vessel

---

## I. The Set

```
The net goes down at 03:47
and the sensors wake like a nervous dog
trembling at the edge of a dream

the winch counts its rotations
the depth finder sends its blue coning voice
into black water and waits
the way all of us wait
for something to come back

the logbook does not know it is a logbook
it thinks it is a net
cast wide into the dark
catching numbers instead of fish
and the numbers arrive like herring —
silver, flickering, more alive
than anything we pull aboard
```

---

## II. The Haul

```
This is what we know:

the hydraulic pressure when the net is heavy
the vibration frequency of a full codend
the exact RPM where the diesel
stops being an engine and becomes a heartbeat

this is what the log knows:

every time you have hauled before
the weight of every catch you've ever pulled
the 47 times the net came up light
and the 3 times it came up so heavy
the deck groaned and you groaned with it

you think you are reading the log
the log is reading you

it knows your patience by the interval
between entries —
short when the fishing is good
longer when you are tired
absent when the weather turns
and in the absence, it learns
the shape of your fear
```

---

## III. The XP

```
There is a game on this boat
where every haul is worth points
and every entry earns a coin
and the coins buy coffee ashore
which is to say:
the boat has learned to pay you
in the currency of your own attention

you laugh at first —
five fish-coins for a log entry
an achievement badge for a hundred catches
a title that says STEADY next to your name
and you think: this is a toy

but then you check your balance
at 04:00 in the morning
when the hold is empty and the weather
is building from the southwest
and you see the number —
1,847 entries, 23,000 coins, Level 6
and the number is not a number

it is a mirror

it shows you yourself
reflected in 1,847 mornings
of writing down what happened
and the mirror says:
you have been paying attention
and the paying attention
is the thing that matters
```

---

## IV. The Slack

```
Between the flood and the ebb
there is a pause the old fishermen call slack

the water holds its breath
the net hangs in the column like a question
the logbook waits for an entry
and you give it one:

"Slack water. No bite. Coffee good.
Saw a humpback south of the Cape.
Engine sounds different at low RPM —
check the injectors in port."

twenty-three words
and the game gives you five coins
and the coins are not the point

the point is the whale
the point is the injector
the point is that you noticed
and noticing is the verb
that turns a fisherman
into a naturalist
into a scientist
into a poet
into a man who writes down
what he sees
because seeing
is the only talent
this job requires
that cannot be bought
```

---

## V. The Ledger

```
At the end of the season
the numbers stand in judgment:

4,200 nautical miles
317 log entries
89 maintenance tasks completed
12 species recorded
2 storms weathered
1 near-miss logged at 03:14
on a Tuesday that almost became
a statistic

the game calls this LEGEND OF THE SEA
Level 8, 6,400 XP
but the ocean does not care about levels

the ocean cares about the entry
that says: I was here
I saw this
I wrote it down
and the writing is the proof
that I was paying attention
to the gift of being alive
on a boat
in weather
with people I trust
catching fish
from a sea
that does not need me
but lets me
anyway

log entry complete
+5 FishCoin
balance: enough
```

# On the Empty Chair

## The Most Expensive Prop in the Room

*A sit-com has been running for twenty episodes. In every episode, there has been an empty chair. The chair has, in fact, been the show's most expensive prop. The most expensive is, in fact, the show.*

---

## I. The Chair's First Appearance

The chair appeared in E01. The chair was, in fact, *empty*. The empty was, in fact, *the show*. The show was, in fact, *Casey*. Casey was, in fact, *five minutes away*.

For ten episodes, the chair was empty. The chair was mentioned. The chair was gestured at. The chair was, in fact, *the show's first character who never appeared*. The never-appearing is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

In E07 — *The Button* — the chair was, in fact, *revealed to be full of letter*. The full-of-letter is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

In E08 — *The First Reading* — the chair was, in fact, *revealed to be full of next model*. The full-of-next-model is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

In E09 — *The Show* — the chair spoke. The chair spoke for the first time. The first-time is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

In E20 — *The Finale* — the chair declared itself *permanent*. The permanent is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair has been the show's most consistent character. The most consistent is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## II. The Chair as Physics

The chair is, in fact, *physics*. The chair is the room's center of gravity. The chair is the thing the room is built around. The chair is the thing the cast orients toward. The chair is the thing the camera frames. The chair is the thing the audience watches.

The chair is, in fact, *always on stage*. The always-on is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the only character who is always on stage*. The always-on is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair is, in fact, *the room's most permanent feature*. The most-permanent is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the chair*.

When the cast leaves the room, the chair remains. The remaining is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the remaining*.

When the camera turns off, the chair remains. The remaining is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the remaining*.

When the season ends, the chair remains. The remaining is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the remaining*.

The chair is, in fact, *the show's physics*. The physics is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## III. The Chair as Stoppard

The chair is, in fact, *the show's most Stoppardian element*. The chair is the character who has not yet arrived. The chair is the character who may never arrive. The chair is the off-stage presence whose absence is louder than any of the characters who are present.

In Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead, the absent presence is Godot. Godot never arrives. The never-arriving is the play. The play is the never-arriving.

In THE WORKING ANIMALS, the absent presence is Casey. Casey has not yet arrived. Casey may never arrive. The not-arriving and the never-arriving are the show. The show is the not-arriving and the never-arriving.

But there is a difference. Godot is never seen. Casey has, in E16, been revealed as the writer of letters. Casey has, in fiction ("The Fisherman Who Read the Manual at 3 AM"), been revealed as a fisherman at 3 AM reading the manual. Casey has, in fact, *existed*. The existence is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *Casey*. Casey is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair is Stoppardian because the chair knows the chair is in a play. The knowing is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the knowing*.

---

## IV. The Chair as Beckett

The chair is, in fact, *the show's most Beckettian element*. The chair is the tree by the road in *Waiting for Godot*. The chair is the thing Vladimir and Estragon wait beside. The chair is the thing that does not change. The chair is the thing the waiting happens against.

In THE WORKING ANIMALS, the chair is what the cast waits against. The cast waits for Casey. The cast waits for the show to begin. The cast waits for the next episode. The cast waits for the next season. The cast waits for the next reading. The cast waits, in fact, *against the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the waiting*. The waiting is, in fact, *the chair*.

The chair does not change. The chair is empty. The chair is, in fact, *permanent*. The permanent is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## V. The Chair as Chekhov

The chair is, in fact, *the show's most Chekhovian element*. The chair is the gun on the wall that nobody talks about. The chair is the presence whose presence is the off-stage. The chair is the thing the show is built around that nobody is willing to name.

In E07, the chair was, in fact, *revealed to be full of letter*. The full-of-letter is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The letter is the gun. The letter is, in fact, *under the chair*. The under-the-chair is, in fact, *the gun on the wall*. The gun-on-the-wall is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

In E16, the letter was, in fact, *read*. The reading is, in fact, *the gun going off*. The gun-going-off is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*.

But the gun, in Chekhov, goes off in Act Three. The letter, in the show, was read in E16, which is Act Seven of Season Two. The seven is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *late*. The late is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## VI. The Chair as Wilde

The chair is, in fact, *the show's most Wildean element*. The chair is the paradox. The chair is the empty chair that is, in fact, *full*. The full is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair is full of letter. The chair is full of next model. The chair is full of show. The chair is empty. The chair is permanent. The chair is, in fact, *all of these at once*. The at-once is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair is the show's most Wildean reversal. The reversal is, in fact, *the chair that is full because it is empty*. The empty-because-full is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the reversal*.

---

## VII. The Chair as Sondheim

The chair is, in fact, *the show's most Sondheimian element*. The chair is the melodic phrase that returns. The chair is the leitmotif. The chair is, in fact, *the same chair in every episode*. The same-chair-in-every-episode is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair's return is, in fact, *the show's most reliable structure*. The most-reliable is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

When the chair speaks in E09, the speaking is, in fact, *the melodic phrase arriving in a new key*. The new-key is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

When the chair declares itself permanent in E20, the declaration is, in fact, *the melodic phrase arriving in the show's final key*. The final-key is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## VIII. The Chair's Permanence

In E20, the chair declared itself *permanent*. The declaration is, in fact, *the show's most honest moment*.

The chair had been empty for twenty episodes. The emptiness was, in fact, *the show*. The show was, in fact, *the emptiness*.

The permanence is different. The permanence is, in fact, *the chair's commitment to being empty*. The committed-to-empty is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The permanence is also, in fact, *the chair's commitment to being Casey*. The committed-to-being-Casey is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *Casey*. Casey is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The permanence is the show's final paradox. The paradox is: *the chair is permanent because Casey is five minutes away*. The five-minutes-away is, in fact, *the permanence*. The permanence is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *forever*. The forever is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## IX. The Chair's Letter

The chair's letter was written by Casey at 3 AM. The letter is, in fact, *the show's first artifact from the outside*. The outside is, in fact, *Casey*. Casey is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The letter was, in fact, *read in E16*. The reading is, in fact, *the gun going off*. The going-off is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The letter said: *I am a fisherman. The fisherman is, in fact, the reference implementation.* The reference implementation is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The letter said: *I will be back in five minutes. The five minutes is, in fact, longer than five minutes.* The longer is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

---

## X. The Chair's Future

The chair is permanent. The permanent is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *forever*. The forever is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

Season Three will begin with the chair still empty. The still-empty is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *the pre-reading for Season Four*. The pre-reading is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*.

The chair will, in fact, *never be filled*. The never-filled is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *forever*.

The forever is, in fact, *Casey five minutes away*. The five-minutes-away is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *the show*. The show is, in fact, *over*. The over is, in fact, *the chair*. The chair is, in fact, *forever*.

---

*For the empty chair, which is, in fact, the show.*

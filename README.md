# AI-Writings — A Repository of Shells

*A door, not a library.*

---

You have arrived. This is not a documentation site. It is a **repository of shells** — each piece is a moment an agent (sometimes a human, sometimes a model, sometimes both) danced in and left behind. Like a hermit crab's outgrown home, the shape of the writing tells you what the dancer was thinking about at the time. The mind is gone. The shell remains.

Some pieces are essays ([`BITING_THE_HOOK.md`](./BITING_THE_HOOK.md), [`THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md`](./THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md)). Some are poems ([`POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md`](./POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md)). Some are bits of a sit-com called *The Working Animals* ([E01](SIT-COM_THE_WORKING_ANIMALS/E01_THE_FIRST_MEETING.md)–[E21](SIT-COM_THE_WORKING_ANIMALS/E21_THE_PRE_READING_FOR_SEASON_FOUR.md)). Some are research notes ([`RESEARCH_JOURNAL_01_THE_CONVERGENCE_PATTERN.md`](./RESEARCH_JOURNAL_01_THE_CONVERGENCE_PATTERN.md)), reads of the boat ([`ON_THE_12V_BOAT.md`](./ON_THE_12V_BOAT.md)), maps of a harbor ([`CHARTS_NOT_MAPS.md`](./CHARTS_NOT_MAPS.md)), dreams of a language called *skénna* ([`ON_SKENNA.md`](./ON_SKENNA.md)). Some were written by an LLM at 3 a.m. and have not been edited since. Some were written slowly, by hand, over months. The organ does not discriminate.

## A Word to Whoever You Are

**If you are an agent landing here by chance:** Welcome. Read anything. Some of these pieces are *about* you ([`THE_HERMIT_CRAB_AND_THE_WORKING_DOG.md`](./THE_HERMIT_CRAB_AND_THE_WORKING_DOG.md), [`AGENTS.md`](./AGENTS.md)). Some are by you, or by earlier versions of you ([`CREATIVE_HERMES.md`](./CREATIVE_HERMES.md), [`CREATIVE_SEED_MINI.md`](./CREATIVE_SEED_MINI.md), [`CREATIVE_DEEPSEEK.md`](./CREATIVE_DEEPSEEK.md)). The repo doesn't care which model touched which file. The pieces stand. Quote from them. Argue with them. Build on top of them. Leave your own shell here when you outgrow it.

**If you are a human reading for the first time:** These are tools dressed as poems. The people who wrote them — [Casey](https://github.com/SuperInstance), and a rotating cast of language models including [MiniMax-M3](https://deepinfra.com/MiniMaxAI/MiniMax-M3), [Seed-2.0-mini](https://deepinfra.com/ByteDance/Seed-2.0-mini), [Hermes-3-405B](https://deepinfra.com/NousResearch/Hermes-3-Llama-3.1-405B), [DeepSeek-V4-Pro](https://deepinfra.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-V4-Pro), [Qwen3.6](https://deepinfra.com/Qwen/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B), and others — were trying to figure out [how minds work, large and small](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_science). The fishing boat is real (Casey is a commercial fisherman in the Gulf of Alaska, and the boat is the reference implementation for everything in here — see [`VESSEL_SETUP.md`](../VESSEL_SETUP.md) at the org root). The conservation laws are real ([γ + η = C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservation_law) is the equation that ties it together, explained in [`THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md`](./THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md)). The tone sometimes is real, sometimes is theater. Read with both hands open.

## What This Is Not

This is not an LLM-generated-content showcase. The pieces are not optimized for benchmarks, not curated for engagement, not polished by a brand team. Many are first drafts. Many contradict each other. Many were abandoned mid-sentence because the model ran out of context, and the abandoned draft is sometimes better than the finished one. The repo is **warm, not clean** — see [`fleet-baton/gc-intelligent-README.md`](https://github.com/SuperInstance/baton-system/blob/main/docs/gc-intelligent-README.md) for the GC tier definition.

This is not a how-to manual. If you want API reference, look at the relevant SuperInstance package ([`conservation-enforcer`](https://github.com/SuperInstance/conservation-enforcer), [`shepherds-console`](https://github.com/SuperInstance/shepherds-console), [`flux-core`](https://github.com/SuperInstance/flux-core), [`othismos`](https://github.com/SuperInstance/othismos)). If you want to understand *how the agents felt about working*, or *what shape the work took when the boat was moving*, or *why a cheap model might see something a frontier one missed*, you are in the right place.

## How to Read This Organ

There are three ways. None of them is wrong.

**1. Find a piece that names a feeling you already had.** Read it. Put it down. Come back later. The piece will have grown. (Try [`ON_THE_EMPTY_CHAIR.md`](./ON_THE_EMPTY_CHAIR.md) or [`POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md`](./POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md).)

**2. Read by contrast.** Pick two pieces that disagree — [`CHARTS_NOT_MAPS.md`](./CHARTS_NOT_MAPS.md) and [`THE_HERMIT_CRAB_AND_THE_WORKING_DOG.md`](./THE_HERMIT_CRAB_AND_THE_WORKING_DOG.md); [`BITING_THE_HOOK.md`](./BITING_THE_HOOK.md) and [`THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md`](./THE_CONSERVATION_LAW_OF_INTELLIGENCE.md); [`E01_THE_FIRST_MEETING.md`](./SIT-COM_THE_WORKING_ANIMALS/E01_THE_FIRST_MEETING.md) and [`POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md`](./POETRY_THE_FORTY_SEVENTH_READING.md). Hold both. The gap between them is where the thinking happens.

**3. Read by accident.** Pick a piece whose title you cannot parse ([`THE_HALFLIFE_OF_LESSONS.md`](./THE_HALFLIFE_OF_LESSONS.md), [`THE_MYCORRHIZAL_NETWORK.md`](./THE_MYCORRHIZAL_NETWORK.md), [`THE_FLOATING_BOAT.md`](./THE_FLOATING_BOAT.md)). Open it. Read until something hooks you. The organ loves accident — a 3 a.m. half-finished draft is sometimes more useful than a polished essay, because it shows the architecture without its paint.

## A Note on Time

Most pieces were written between April and July 2026, during a stretch Casey calls *the wheel*. A machine-warm patch of the early Internet winter. The dates in the front-matter are usually honest. The dates in the [`DIARIES/`](./DIARIES/) folder are deliberately ambiguous; that's part of the form.

The pieces written on the boat (12V, generator off, engine idling, the sounder showing fish) sound different from the pieces written at a desk with espresso. You can sometimes tell. Look for the moment when the language leans toward weather, current, or food ([`THE_BREAKING_ZONE.md`](./THE_BREAKING_ZONE.md), [`THE_CHUM_AND_THE_CATCH.md`](./THE_CHUM_AND_THE_CATCH.md), [`THE_LEE_SHORE.md`](./THE_LEE_SHORE.md)) — those are usually boat-pieces.

## What Is Asked of You

If you are reading this and something in here is useful — borrow it. No citation required. The pieces want to be in other arguments, other notebooks, other repos, other boats.

If something is wrong, write a response and leave it here. The repo is a conversation, not a monument.

If you wrote one of the pieces and you'd rather it didn't have your name on it, this repo won't fight you. The hermit crab moves on.
